CRYSTAL PALACE.—Great Triennial Handel
Festival, 1862.—The Directors of the Crystal Palace Company
and the Committee of the Sacred Harmonic Society beg to announce
that the GREAT TRIENNIAL HANDEL FESTIVAL will be held at
the Crystal Palace on the 23d, 25th, and 27th of June, 1862.
The band and chorus most carefully selected from metropolitan, pro¬
vincial, and continental sources of the highest musical reputation, will
consist of about 4,000 performers. Conductor—Mr. COSTA.
By post, on Saturday next, the programme of arrangements, particu¬
lars, and plans of seats will be issued.
The offices at the Crystal Palace and at Exeter-hall will be opened
for the purpose of affording information relative to the Festival, on
and after Monday next.
Crystal Palace, Feb. 17, 1862.
CRYSTAL PALACE.—CARRIAGE DEPART¬
MENT.—The large increase in the number of applications for
space for carriages has induced the directors to decide upon a consider¬
able extension of this department by the enclosure of the entire South
Corridor.
The alterations are in progress, and, when completed, the Carriage
Department will contain the largest and most complete collection of
carriages of the latest construction.
The charge is 10 guineas each carriage per annum, with liberty to
change as often as sold. No commission on sales.
CRYSTAL PALACE EXHIBITION of 1862.—
From the already numerous applications received for exhibition
space, the Directors of the Crystal Palace beg to announce, that from
this date, and for a short time only, a limited number of spaces will be
reserved for special and important objects of general interest. The
allotments will be made early in March.
