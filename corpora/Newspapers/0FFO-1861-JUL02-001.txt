CHALLENGE to M. BLONDIN.—ROYAL
PAVILION GARDENS, North Woolwich, Monday, July 8,
1861. Mr. D. ALBERTE, the celebrated English rope-walker, will
make his FIRST terrific ASCENT on a rope stretched across the gar¬
dens, 1,000 feet in length and 150 feet high. Mr. D. Alberte never
having had the same chance as the here of Niagara of walking over
the Falls, begs to inform him, wherever it is possible to erect a rope, no
matter how high or how great a distance, he is prepared to do all M.
Blondin dare do. The rope will be fixed by Wednesday next. Any
person wishing to see it will be admitted for that purpose free from
10 a.m. to 3 p.m. daily, and any persons not being satisfied with his
performance on Monday next shall have their money returned on ap¬
plication to the proprietor. A list of his performances for his first
ascent will be advertised in a few days. Admission, including railway
there and back, 1s.
