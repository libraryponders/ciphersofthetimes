ON APOLOGY.—24, Cavendish-street, New North¬
road, July 1st, 1875.—Sir.—I desire to convey to you my most
sincere regret for having given circulation to a statement that fever
was prevalent amongst the employés of your establishment. I am
now satisfied that the report was untrue and without soundation. I
beg to offer you the most ample APOLGY for the great injury which
unintentionally I have caused you by circulating the report. I also
thank you most sincerely for your generosity in refraining from taking
proceedings against me. You are at libert, to make whatever use you
think proper of this letter, and especially to publish it in such papers
as you niny deem expedient.
I am, Sir, your grateful and obliged servant,
