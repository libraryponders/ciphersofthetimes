TO the RELATIVES of DANIEL HOLTON.—
Daniel Holton, lately a labourer on the Government Railway,
Ballaarat, Victoria, who stated himself to have come from London,
died in the Ballaarat Hospital on the 25th February, 1869. Mr. Joseph
Bowman claims bank deposit receipts to the value of £403 of his, as
gifts or donations mortis causa, and the Supreme Court of the Colony
of Victoria proposes to grant administration of the personal estate of
the said Daniel Hoiton, as intestate, to the said Joseph Bowman,
unless some person better entitled to be personal representative appiies
through a proctor to the said Court within four months of this adver¬
tisement. The above Daniel Holton left other property in Victoria, to
which his next of kin are supposed to be entitled.

—Notice is hereby given, that the Board of Charity Commissioners
for England and Wales propose to approve and certify a SCHEME to
be submitted to Parliament for the ESTABLISHMENT and REGU¬
LATION of the KELLY COLLEGE.
Full copies of the proposed Scheme may be inspected free of cost
daily, except on Sundays, during the space of one caiendar month from
the first publication of this notice, at the offices of Messrs. Gurney,

Town Clerks of Exeter, Devonport, Plymouth, Barnstaple, and Bidc¬
ford; at the Guildhall, in Bodmin; and the Town-hall, at Okehamp¬
