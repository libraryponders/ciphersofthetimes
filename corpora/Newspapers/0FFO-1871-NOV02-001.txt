THAMES CONSERVANCY.—Notice is hereby
given, pursuant to the provisions of the Petroloum Act, 1871, that
the Conservators of the River Thames have framed the following
BYE-LAW relative to vessels carrying betroleum, and that it is the in¬
tention of the Conservators to apply to the Board of Trade for a con¬
firmation thereof, according to the provisions of the said Act.
"The Conservaters of the River Thames, in exercise of the powers
and authority verted in them by the Act of Parliament, made and
passed in the 34th and 35th years of the reign of Her present Majesty,
chapter 105, intituled An Act for the safe Keeping of Petroleum and
other substances of a Like Nature, and of every other authority them
hereunto in anywise enabling, do order and direct as follows: That is
to say, That no ship or vessel laden either wholly or in part with petto¬
leum, rock oil, Rangoon oil, Burmah oil, or oil made from petroleum,
coal schist, shale, peat, or other bituminous substance, or any pro¬
ducts of petroieum, or of any of the above-mentioned oils, wnich said
oils, or products of oils, when tested, in manner ret forth in Sche¬
dule 1 to The Petroieum Act. 1871," give off an inflammable vapour
at a tomperature of less than 100 degress of Fahrenheit's thermometer,
shall for any purpose whatever be navigated, lle in, or ne moored in
any part of the River Thames above or westward of Thames Haven.
And all such ships or vessels being laden at aforesaid shall, when
moored or anchored, lie singly and Apart from each other, with a clear
space of not less than 100 feet of water separating them.
Any objection to the above bye-law should be addressed to the
undersigned Secretary, at the Thames Condervancy-office, 41, Trinity¬
square, Tower-hill, London, within one month froin the date hereof.
E. BURSTAL, Secretary.
