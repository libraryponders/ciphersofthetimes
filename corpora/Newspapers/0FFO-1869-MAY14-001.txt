Joseph Esdaile, one of the registered public officers of a company or
copartnership called "The London and Westminster Bank," for and
on behalf of the said Company, is plaintiff, and Sarah Jane Teversham,
spinster, and you, the said William John Barrett (out of the jurisdic¬
tion), are defendants, whereby it was ordered that the plaintiff should
be at liberty, on or before the 15th day of May inst., to insert in the
London Gazette and The Times newspaper a notice of a subpoena to
hear judgment, bearing date the 6th day of May, 1869, having been
issued in this cause, and that publication of such notice in manner
aforesaid should be deemed from the time when the insertion thereof
in the London Gazette and The Times newspaper should have been
made good service of the said subpona to hear judgment on you, the
said defendant William John Barrett. In pursuance of such order
notice is hereby given, that a subposna to hear judgment in this cause
was issued in the following form:—"Victoria, by the grace of God, of
the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland, Queen, Defender
of the Faith.—To Sarah Jane Teversham, spinster, and William John
Barrett (out of the jurisdiction), greeting. We command you, and
every of you, that you appear before our Lord High Chancellor on the
7th day of June next, or whenever thereafter a certain cause now de¬
pending in our High Court of Chancery, wherein Joseph Esdaile (one
of the registered public officers of a company or copartnership called
the London and Westminster Bank, for and on behalf of the said Com¬
pany) is plaintiff, and Sarah Jane Teversham, spinster, and another
are defendants, shall come on for hearing, then and there to receive
and abide by such judgment and decree as shall then or thereafter be
made and proxounced, upon pain of judgment being pronounced against
you by default. — Witness ourself at Westminster, the 6th day of May,
in the 32d year of our reign.—Romilly M. R."— Dated this 12th day o
May, 1869. ROY and CARTWRIGHT, 4, Lothbury, Solicitor
or the Plaintiff, Joseph Esdaile.

SECOND GREAT FIRE in MINCING LANE, in
which MILNERS SAFES have done their duty.
25, Mincing-lane, London, April 30, 1869.
Gentlemen.—It affords us pleasure to inform you that in the severe
fire early this morning, at 25, Mincing-lane, our books, valuable papers,
&c., were all preserved in your safe, while our offices on the second floor
and all their contents were destroyed. We have great pleasure in bear¬
ing testimony to the efficiency of your safes, and shall strongly recom¬
mend them to our friends. Please supply a new safe, 4½B size.
We are, Gentlemen, yours truly,
KING and HOVIL.
