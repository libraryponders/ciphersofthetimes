PUBLIC APOLOGY.—Whereas, proceedings were
recently commenced against meiby the proprietor of the Patent
Glessen Blue for selling blue colouring matter in bottles labelled "The
Patent Glessen Blue," which was not the true Giessen blue, and after
such proceedings were commenced the said proprietor, at my request,
agreed to discontinue the same on my making a public apology, paying
allcosts, and entering into the following undertaking; Now I do hereby
APOLOGISE to the said proprietor, and undertake that I will not
hereafter sell any blue (other than the true Glessen blue) which shall be
so labelled as to be likely to induce the public to believe that it is the
patent Giessen blue which is manufactured at No. 43a, London-wall,
London. —Dated this 15th June, 1864.
JAMES BEVAN.
