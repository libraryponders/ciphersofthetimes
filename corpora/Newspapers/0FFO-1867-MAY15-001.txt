IN CHANCERY.—FARINA v. CATHERY and
others.—Caution.—The Vice-Chancellor Sir William Page Wood
having, on the 26th of April, 1867, by the decree made upon the hearing
of the above cause, instituted by Johann Maria Farina, Gegenuber
dem Julichs Platz (opposite the Julichs Platz, Cologne), against the de¬
fendants, to restrain the use in the United Kingdom of labels containing
the name and address "Johann Maria Farina, Gegenuber dem Josephs
Platz," printed or engraved in letters and characters such as to render
the label an imitation of the plaintiff's trade mark, awarded a PER¬
PETUAL INJUNCTION, restraining the defendants, Charles Cathery
and Eugene Carmouche, their warehousemen, clerks, and agents, from
thereafter selling perfumed water or liquid not manufactured or pre¬
pared by the plaintiff, in bottles having such labels as in the bill men¬
tioned pasted thereon, or rolled up, or packed in such wrappers as in
the bill referred to, or any other labels and wrappers being imitations,
with colourable variations only, of those invented and used by the
plaintiff, and from in any way using any labels or wrappers, or other
particulars which should represent or have the appearance of represent¬
ing the plaintiff as the manufacturer or preparer of the perfumed
water contained in such bottles, or from otherwise using the trade
marks of the plaintiff, or of the same or a like description as those
used by the plaintiff:—All persons are hereby cautioned from pur¬
chasing, selling, exposing for sale, or otherwise dealing in perfumed
liquid or EAU DE COLOGNE, contained in bottles having affixed to,
or pasted thereon, labels upon which the name and address, "Johann
Maria Farina, Gegenuber dem Josephs Platz," is printed or engraved
in such letters and characters, or with such accompaniments as to
render it an imitation of the plaintiff's label and trade mark.—Dated
this 9th day of May, 1867.
