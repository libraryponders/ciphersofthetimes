Notice is hereby given, that all persons intor

under the Statute 25 Geo. II., c. 36 (except in the case of a transfer of
any licence as
creinafter mentioned), must, two months at the least
before Monday, the 2d day of October next, which will be the com¬
mencement of the next Michaelmas Quarter Session, cause a notice (in
the undermentiomed form) to be affixed upon the outer door or other
conspicuous part, of the premises sought to be licensed, and upon the
outer door of the police station or other place of confinement in the
parish in which such premises shall be situate, and must also serve
copies of such last n entioned notice apon the Churchwardens and Over¬

said 21 day of October next to me, at the Sessions-houses, Clerkenwell,
and to the Clerk of the Petty Sessions of the division wherein the
premises are situate, of their intended application.
In every case when any person having a music or dancing licence
shall transfer his premises to any other person, such incoming tenant
shall give one month instead of two months' notice as above required.
Form of Notice.
"I. — of— do hereby give notice that I intend to
apply, under the provisions of the Statute 25 Geo. II., c. 36, at the
next Michaelmas Quarter Session for the county of Middlesex, to be
holden at Clerkenwell on the 5th day of October, 1865, for a license
for (here insert the particular sort or sorts of public entertainment for
which the licenee is required) to be carried on within the house or pre¬
mises situate at —, or known by the name of (as the case may bel,
and now in the occupation of me, the subscriber hereto."
And notice is also given, that every person presenting any petition
against the grant or renewal of any licence must leave with me, at the
Seasions-house, Clerkenwell, 14 clear days before the said 2d day of
October, a copy of such counter-petition, for the inspection of the party
applying for such grant or renewal.
And that every applicant for a licence is required to attend the Court
personally on the said 5th day of October, which will be the day for
granting licences, unless a reason satisfactory to the Court shall be
assigned for his absence.
RICHARD NICHOLSON, Clerk of the Peace.
