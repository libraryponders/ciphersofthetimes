WHEREAS the Commissioners in Lunacy have in¬
timated their intention to institute proceedings against me,
the undersigned Ellen Matthews, of No. 1, Sidmouth-villas, Mayday-¬
road, West Croydon, in the County of Surrey, for having received
into my house and taken the care and charge of two lunatics without
having first obtained a licence for that purpose in compliance with
the provisions of the Act of the 8th and 9th Victoria, cap. 100, being
an Act for the regulation of the care and treatment of lunatics; and,
whereas, in consideration of the acknowledgement and apology here¬
inafter set forth, the said Commissioners have, under the special cir¬
cumstances of my case, consented to stay such proceedings. Now, I, the
said Ellen Matthews, do hereby ACKNOWLEDGE that I have
VICLATED the LAW by receiving into my house and taking the
care and charge of two lunatics without having first obtained the
necessary liceuse. And I do hereby express my deep regret for
having so done, and do thus publicly apologise for the same, and I
do solemnly promise not again to offend in like manner.—Dated this
3rd day of May, 1879.
ELLEN MATTHEWS.
VANDERCOM, LAW, and HARDY, 23, Bush-lane,
London, Solicitors to the Commissioners in Lunacy.
