GABRIEL, Mayor,
ACOURT of MAYOR and ALDERMEN of the
city of London, holden in the Inner Chamber of the Guildhall of
the city of London, on Tuesday, the 28th day of May, A.D. 1867, and
in the 30th year of the reign of Her present Majesty Victoria, of the
United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland Queen, Defender of the
Faith, &c.
Whereas the Court of Mayor and Aldermen of the city of London
have, in pursuance of the powers and authorities in them vested in
that behalf, from time to time made divers orders and regulations,
appointing the standings or places within the said city of London and
the liberties thereof, for carts, carrs, wains, or waggons to stand and
ply for hire, and to regulate the number of such carts, carrs, wains,
or waggons to stand in such places respectively, and the distances at
which they shall stand one from another, and as to the times of their
so standing and plying for hire, and otherwise regulating the same:
and whereas it appears to this Court that for the purposes of public con¬
venience and accommodation it is expedient that the standing for 22
carts in Lower Thames-street, heretofore established under the autho¬
rity of the Court, should be entirely discontinued and abolished.
Now, therefore, this Court, in pursuance of the powers and authori¬
ties vested in or enabling them in that behalf, doth hereby REPEAL
and RESCIND so much and such part only of any and every order and
regulation now in force as relates to the STANDING of CARTS in
LOWER THAMES-STREET, and doth hereby direct and order that
the said standing shall be entirely discontinued.
And this Court doth hereby further order and direct that this order
and regulation shall become and be in full force and effect upon
from the 1st day of July, A.D 1867
