BOLOGY.—To John Willis Clark, Esq., Trinity
College, Cambridge.—Sir.—On the 12th May, 1873, as Solicitor
for Charles John Merry laccused of having forge; your name to a
cheek for £250, upon which charge he now stands committed to take
his trial at the neat Cambridge Assizes), I at the Town-hall, Cambridge,
put certain questions to you in cross examination, imputing to you
immoral conduct, and you having brought an action against me for
slander, with the object of vindicating your character, from such im¬
putations, I now hereny in terms the most onequivocal and most ample
WITHDRAW in the fullest sense the IMPUTATIONS so made upon
you and further express my belief, that they are wholly and absolutely
false and unfounded. I therefore offer to you the expression of my
sincere regret, that I as an advocate should have been made the
medium for eriginating so gross and base a slander upon you. I ven¬
ture therefore to ask you to accept this retractation and apology as
evidence of my sincerity, and beg you to withdraw iu ther proceedings
in the action brougnt by you against me, and I authorise you to make
suce use of this apology as you or your advisers may think necessary.—
Dated this 31st day or May, 1873.
F. POLAND ADCOCK, Solr., Cambridge.
Witness, George H. Lewis, 10, Lly-place, Holborn, Solicitor for
J. W. Clark, Esq.
