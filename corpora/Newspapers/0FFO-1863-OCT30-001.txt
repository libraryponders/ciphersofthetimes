TWENTY-FIVE POUNDS REWARD.—In the
Matter of Henry Binnell, of Birmingham, in the county of
Warwick, tailor, a bankrupt.—Whereas the said Henry Binnell, lately
carrying on business in Summer-lane, Birmingham aforesaid, as a
tailor, was on the 14th of September last duly adjudicated bankrupt
by His Honour the Commissioner of the Birmingham District Court
of Bankruptey; and whereas the said HENRY BINNELL has
ABSCONDED, and it is believed is about to quit the kingdom for New
Zealand with a considerable sum of money and cloth goods, intending
to defraud his creditors: Now, therefore, notice is hereby given, that
£25 reward will be paid by the Assignees chosen under the bankruptcy
to any person giving such information as shall lead to the apprehen¬
sion of the said Henry Binnell. He is 35 years of age, 5 feet 3 inches
high, hair black, whiskers and moustache also black, and latter bushy,
mouth large, complexion dark. Binnell is supposed to be travelling
under the name of "Tremiskin," and to have with him a female, of
lady-like appearance, about his own height. Information to be given
to Messrs. Treherne and Wolferstan, solicitors, 75. Aldermanbury, city.
London, solicitors to the assignees; or to Mr. Chief Superintendent
Glossop, Birmingham; George Kinnear. Esq., official assignee, or
Messrs. Hodgson and Son, solicitors, 13, Waterloo-street, Birmingham.
