PRECIOUS STONES.—FIVE HUNDRED POUNDS RE¬
WARD.—Whereas, between the 18th June and 1st July last, dia¬
mond ornaments of the value of £10,000 were stolen, the property of
a firm of West-end jewellers; and whereas the diamonds, rubies, and
emeralds have been removed from the settings and mountings, and the

about the size of a shilling, having contained two rows of stones,
and one centre stone (all pave set). The outer circle has contained 21
stones, inner circle eight stones, rather larger, mounted in silver with
gold pin; catch gone. Information to be furnished to Messrs. Lewis
and Lewis, 10, Ely-place, Holborn, Solicitors; or to Superintendent
Williamson, Detective bepartment, Scotland-yard. Note.—The re¬
wards of £1,000 and £300 respectively advertised in this journal in the
