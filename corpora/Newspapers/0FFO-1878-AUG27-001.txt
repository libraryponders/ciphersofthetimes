FOREST ARBITRATION.—I, the Arbitrator appointed by the
bh Forest Act, 1878, hereby give notice that all CLAIMS and RE¬
PRESENTATIONS relating to the matters hereafter mentioned are
to be delivered at my office, No. 6. Old Palace-yard, Westminster,
S.W. on or before the 18th day of October next, that is to say:—
1. Every person claiming a portion of the Pink Lands mentioned in
section 11, sub-section (2) of the said Act, as having been on or before
the 14th of August, 1871, actually covered with buildings or actually
enclosed and used as a garden belonging to some building, or as a
curtilage of some building, is to specify what portion of land he so
claims.
2. Every person claiming to own a portion of the Pink Lands used
as a nursery garden, and representing that it is unnecessary or unde¬
sirable as regards the recreation or enjoyment of the public that such
land should be thrown open, is to specify what portion of land he so
claims.
3. Every person claiming to retain any of such lands is to state
what consideration he proposes to give for having his titie quieted ac¬
cording to section 11, sub-section (6) of the said Act.
4. Every owner of the soil of a portion of the Pink Lands who is
satisfied that the same will be thrown open, is to specify what he
claims as purchase-money for the soil thereof, with the minerals
thereunder and the timber thereon, according to section 11, sub¬
section (3) of the said Act.
N.B.—The Pink Lands are those waste lands of Epping Forest
which have been unlawfully enclosed since the 21st day of August,
1351, and still remain enclosed.
See section 1, sub-section (1) of the Act.

mentioned in section 1, sub-section (2) of the Act.
