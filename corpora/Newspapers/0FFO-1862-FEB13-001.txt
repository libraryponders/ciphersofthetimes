A SSAULT of ARMS.—By permission, and under
the immediate patronage of, Lieut.-Col. the Hon. D. C. Fitz¬
gerald de Ros and the officers of the First Regiment of Life Guards.—
An ASSAULT of ARMS will be given at Willis's Rooms, King-street,
St. James's, on Saturday, the 15th February, 1862, for the benefit of the
widow of the late Corporal Shaw. On this occasion the use of the foil,
broadsword, sabre, and bayonet, &c., will be illustrated by the most
distinguished swordsmen of the Household Cavalry Brigade, including
Corporals-Major Sutton and St. John, Corporals Galbraith, Binnie, and
Cornish, of the First Life Guards; Corporals-Major Waite and Greg¬
son, and Corporal Butt, of the Second Life Guards; and Corporals
Bee, Learmont, and Wordsworth, of the Royal Horse Guards; assisted
by several eminent amateurs and professors—Fontaine, Nerrier, and
Terrein, who have kindly volunteered their services. Committee of
Management:—O.M. Corporal-Major Waite, Corporal-Major Sutton,
C.M. Corporal-Major St. John, Corporal Binnie. The band of the First
Life Guards will attend, under the direction of Mr. Waddell. Tickets,
5s. and 2s. 6d. each, to be had of the Committee, at Hyde-park Barracks,
and at the door Doors open at half-past 3, and commence at 4 o'clock.
