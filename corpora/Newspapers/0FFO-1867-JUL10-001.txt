THE FORD and LOWICK TURNPIKE TRUST
ACT, 1867.— Whereas in and by the 21st section of the "Ford
and Lowick Turnpike Trust Act, 1867," it is enacted that "The
trustees shall within one calendar month after the passing of this Act¬
give notice to the several persons named in the first scheduie by letter
sent by post, addressed to each of the said several persons at his last
known place of abode, if known,or if not known, then the said trustees
shall publish a notice once in the London daily Times newspaper, and
once in a newspaper circulated in the said county of Northumberland,
to the effect that unless the person or persons severally claiming to be
respectively entitled to the several principal sums set forth in the said
first schedule, shall, on or before the expiration of one calendar
month from the posting of such letter, or of the publication of such
notice respectively, deliver to the clerk for the time being of the trus¬
tees, notice in writing claiming such principal sums respectively, and
shall within three calendar months after the delivery of such notice,
satisfactorly prove his or their right to receive such principal sum or
sums respectively, the several and respective principal sums in respect
of which no such notice and proof of title shall be so given and esta¬
blished, together with all interest, and arrears of interest thereon,
will be wholly barred and extinguished, and the same several principal
sums, and all interest and arrears of interest thereon respectively, are in
such case of default of notice and proof of title as aforesaid, hereby de¬
clared to be respectively wholly barred and extinguished accordingly;
and whereas the PLACE of ABODE of THOMAS SMITH, one of
the persons named in the said first schedule, is not known:—Now, there¬
fore, the said trustees do hereby give notice that unless the said
Thomas Smith shall in compliance with the said hereinbefore recited
section give the notice thereby required and prove his title to the sum
standing in the said first schedule in the name of him the said Thomas
Smith, the same with all interest, will be wholly barred and extin¬
guished, as directed by the said Act.—Berwick-upon-Tweed, dated
this 4th day of July, 1867.
