NOTICE is hereby given, that the ORDER here¬
under set forth has been issued by the Lords of Her Majesty's
Most H. nourable Privy Council.
MONCKTON.
Guildhall, 14th May, 1879.
At the Council Chamber, Whitehall, the 8th day of May, 1879.
By the Lords of Her Majesty's Most Honourable Privy Council.
"Present: Lord President, Lord George Hamilton.
"The Lords and others of Her Majesty's Most Honourable Privy
Council, by virtue and in exercise of the powers in them vested
under the Contagious Diseases (Animals) Act. 1878, and of every
other power enabling them in this behalf, do order, and it is hereby
ordered, as follows:—
"1. This Order shall take effect from and immediately after the 9th
day of May, 1879, and shall cease to have effect from and immediatsly
after the 3rd day of June, 1879, except as regards the recovery of any
penalty incurred thereunder before that day: and werds in this
Order have the same meaning as in the Foreign Animals Order.
""2. Notwithstanding anything in the Foreign Animals Order or in
any other Order of Council, cattle brought from the United States of
America to the Port of London may be transhipped in the Millwall
Docks from the vessel in which they are brought to another vessel for
conveyance to the foreign animals wharf: known as the Foreigh
Cattle Market, at Deptford, subject to the following couditions:—
First. That the vessel into which the cattle are transhipped be a
vessel propelled by steam power (in this Order called the steamer).
Second. That the owner, agent, or charterer of the steamer has
received the special permission of the Commissioners of Customs so
to employ the steamer under this Order.
"3. Those cattle shall be transhipped in the presence and under the
supervision and control of an officer of Customs, who shall remain
in charge of the cattle on board the steamer until its arrival at the
Foreign Cattle Market at Deptford.
"4. The steamer shall proceed direct to that market, and the cattle
shall be landed there in the presence and under the supervision and
control of an officer of Customs.
"5. The steamer shall, immediately after each occasion of use be
cleaused and disiniected in manner provided respecting vessels by
Article 5 of the Cleansing and Disinfection Order of 1878.
6. Cattle so transuipped shall continue to be deemed foreign
cattle.
"7. If anything is done or omitted to be done in contravention of
any of the provisions of this Order, the owner or consignse of the
cattle, and the owner and the master of the steamer, and the company
owning the Millwall Docks, shall each be deemed guiity of an offence
against the Act of 1878.
Ba . L. PEEL."
