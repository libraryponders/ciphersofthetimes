NOTICE is hereby given, that the following Order
has been issued by the Lords of Her Majesty's Most Honour¬
able Privy Council, viz.:—
At the Council Chamber, Whitehall, the 3d day of April, 1879.
By the Lons of Her Maresty's Most Honourable Privy Council.
The Lords and others of Her Majesty's Most Honourable Privy
Council, by virtue and in exercise of the powers in them vested under
the CONTAGIOUS DISEASES (ANIMALS) ACT. 1878, and of
every other power enabling them in this behalf, do ORDER, and it
is hereby ordered as fol ows:
1. This Orser shall take effect from and immediately after the 4th
day of April, 1879, and shall contmue in force for two months from
its passing and no longer, and words in this Order have the same
meaning as in the Foreign Animals Order.
2. Notwithstanding anything in the Foreign Animals Order or in
any other Order of Council, cattle brought from the United States of
America to the Port of London may be transhipped in the Victoria
Docks from the vessel in which they are brought to another vessel
for conveyance to the foreign animals wharf, known as the Foreign
Cattre Market, at Deprford, subject to the tollowing conditions:—
First. That the vessel into which the cattle are transhipped be a
vessel propelled by steam power (in this Order called the steamer).
Second. That the owner, agent, or charterer of the steamer has re¬
ceived the special permi-sion of the Commissioners of Customs so to
employ the steamer under this Order.
3. Those cattle shall be transhipped in the presence of and under
the supervision and control of an officer of Customs, who shall remain
in charge of the cattle on board the steamer antil its arrival at the
Foreign Cattle Market at Deptford.
4. The steamer shall procee direct to that market, and the cattle
shall be lauded there in the presence of and under the supervision
and control of an officer of Customs.
 5. The steamer shad, immediately after each occasion of use, be
cleansed and disinfected in manner provided respecting vessels by
Article 5 of the Cleansing and Disinfection Order of 187s.
5. Cattle so transhipped shall continue ro be deemed foreign cattle.
7. If anything is done or omitted to be done in contravention of
any of the provisions of this Order, the owner or consignee of
the cattie, and the owner and the master of the steamer, and the
Company owning the Victoria Docks, shall each be deemed guilty of
an offence against the Act of 1878.
C. L. PEEL.

going on. Surely it would be right and well if our rulers would
order the Prayer provided for War, in our Book of Common Prayer,
to be used in all our churches; that, as a nation, we may ask
Almighty God to give us victory, and to watch over our brave soldiers
and sailors.
