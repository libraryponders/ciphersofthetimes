ADAME CICELE ZELLWEGEZ-RYHINER
and her children have the painful duty of making known to their
friends and acquaintances the demise of their dear Husband and
Father, Ulric Zellweger, chief of the banking-house of Messrs. U.
Zellweger and Co., of Paris, who expired at 6 o'clock in the evening
of the 19th January, aged 64 years. His illness, though of long dura¬
tion, was happily not attended by great suffering, and he breathed hin
last peaceably in his native place, Trogen, canton of Appenzell,
Switzerland. Madame Zellweger and her children desire that this
announcement may be accepted in lien of a communication direct,
the large circle of friends and acquaintances of her late husband,
coupled with the interruption of communications with Paris, render¬
ing the latter impossible.—Trogen, January 20, 1871.
