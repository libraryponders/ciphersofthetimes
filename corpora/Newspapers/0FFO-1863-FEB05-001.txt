PERKIN's PATENT.—Mauve Dye.—86, St. Vin¬
cent-street, Glasgow, Jan. 24, 1863.—Dear Sirs.—Referring to your
complaint that the mauve paste lately sold by us has been ascertained
by scientific analysis to be an INFRINGEMENT of the patent
granted to your Mr. Win. Hy. Perkin, on the 25th day of Aug., 1856,
we beg to say, that the paste sold by us has not been of our own
manufacture, or sold by us as such, but has been supplied to us by
Messrs. Petersen and Sichler of St. Denis, as their agents, who gave us
their written assurance that the paste did not infringe your patent.
Since learning from you that you were prepared to prove the infringe¬
ment we have again communicates with Messrs. Petersen and Sichler,
offering them the opportunity of trying the question of infringement
at their own expense. This offer they have not aecepted, and we have
therefore agreed to pay you the sum of £245 8s. 4d., being the amount
agreed to be accepted by you as in full of your claims against us, in
consequence of our having acted as agents for the said Petersen and
Sichler, in the sale of said paste. We also readily give you our under¬
taking not to deal further in the article in question, and we have
handed over to you all the paste we had on hand.—We remain, gentle¬
men, yours obediently,
JAS. DALE and Co.
Messrs. G. F. Perkin and Sons, Greenford-green, Sudbury,
