CHARITY COMMISSION.—In the Matter of
WALTER BIGGS' CHARITY, so far as the same is applicable
to the relief of poor Freémen of the Merchant Taylors' Company.—
By direction of the Board of Charity Commissioners for England
and Wnles, notise is hereby given, that an ORDER is proposed to be
made by them after the expiration of one calender month, to be
computed from the first publication of this notice, having the following
objects, namely:—
Establishing a Scheme for the regulation of the abovementioned
Charity, whereby it is proposed that the amount of the annual pensions
now paid to the four recigients of the Charity shall be increased from
£6 13s. 4d. to £12, and providing that the surplus income thereof shall
be expended in the creation of yearly pensions of £10 each, to be
given from time to time to poor freemen, or to the widows or daughters
of freemen of the Merchant Tuylors' Company, all of whom to be
called Biggs' Pensibners.
Any objections to the proposed order, or suggestions for its modifi¬
cation, may be transmitted, in writing, to the Secretary of the said Com¬
missioners, No. 8, York-street, St. James's-square, London, within 21
days next after the first publication of this notice.
Dated this 9th day of December, 1872.
H. M. VANE, Secretary.
