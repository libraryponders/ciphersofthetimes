BANK of ENGLAND.—Unclaimed Dividend.—
Application having been made to the Governors of the Bank
of England to direct the payment of one dividend on the sum of
£10,000 Consolidated Three per Cent. Annuities, heretofore standing in
the name of JOHN NIXON, of Durham-place east, Hackney-road,
gentleman, deceased and which dividend was paid over to the Com¬
missioners for the Reduction of the National Debt, in consequence
of its having remained unclaimed since the 5th January, 1844; Notice
is hereby given, that, on the expiration of three months from this date,
the said Dividend will be Paid to Stephen Brougham, the surviving
executor, who has claimed the same, unless some other claimant shall
sooner appear and make out his claim thereto.

JOSEPH and JOHN VICKERS and Co.'s NEW
DISTILLERY, Victoria-street, Westminster.—Messrs. Joseph
and John Vickers and Co. beg to inform their friends that they have
no longer any connexion with the Borough-market Distillery. The
excavations for the cellars of the new distillery are rapidly progressing,
and (the plans being complete) the building will now be proceeded
with immediately. Messrs. Vickers continue to occupy the temporary
offices, at the Bridge-house Hotel, London-bridge, S.E., and also the
warehouse and wine cellars at Rutland-wharf, Upper Thames-street,
city, E.C., where attendance is daily given, and the business carried on
as usual, until the new distillery is built.
