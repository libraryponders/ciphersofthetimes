NATIONAL ART TRAINING SCHOOL, South
Kensington Museum.
1. An Open Competition for admission to the National Training
School for Teachers in Schools of Art is offered to candidates of all
classes under the age of 17 years.
2. Every candidate must hold—
 A certificate that he has satisfied the Examiners of the Oxford
or Cambridge Local Examinations, or the Society of Arts, in English
grammar, English history, arithmetic, writing, Latin, or a modern
foreign language.
(b) The Certificate of the Science and Art Department of the Second
Grade Art, in Freehand and Model Drawing.
Application for admission must be made on or before the 20th Oc¬
tober, in Form No. 488, which may be obtained from the Secretary
of the Department.
3. Candidates must submit works in drawing, painting, or designing,
to show the nature and extent of their qualifications. Those whose
works are of sufficient merit will be admitted to undergo a competitive
examination in the following subjects:—
(a) Drawing, from the cast, of ornament or of the figure.
(b) Painting in colour from still life, or from the lite (a Head).
.
ing.
d) Modelling of ornament or the figure (this may be alternative
with subject 35).
4. Allowances for maintenance will be made to successful candi¬
dates as follows:—
(a) On admission an allowance at the rate of £30 a year, not to be
held for more than three sessions without taking the Certificate for
the First Group (Elementary Drawing and Colouring) of the Third
Grade in Art. On taking this Certificate
(b) The allowance may be increased to £52 a year, not to be held
for more than two years without taking a further Certificate of the 3d.
Grade, either for Group 2 (Painting) or Group 4 (Modelling Ornament)
or Group 6 (Architecturai). On taking this second Certificate
(c) The allowance may be increased to £65 a year, not to be held
for more than two years without taking a third Certificate for Group 2
or 3 (Figure Drawing and Painting), or 5 (Modelling the Figure). On¬
taking this third Certificate.
(d) The allowance may be increased to £78 a year for not mo
than two years, to enable the student to prepare for a further cor¬
tificate.
5. The student will then be highly qualified as an Art Teacher, and
will be in a position to earn the grants allowed by the Department on
the results of instruction given in Art Schools.
6. Students may be recommended as qualified to become teachers in
Schools of Art at any period after they have obtained the Certificate
for Group 1, third grade.
