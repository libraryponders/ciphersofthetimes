M'Laine and Samuel Martin, the executors therein named, the said
John Maguire being believed to have perished at sea, together with his
wife and three of their children, namely Samuel Martin, James and
Catherine Buchanan, in the ship Spirit of the Sea, of Liverpool, 499
tons register, of which the said John Maguire was master, and which
sailed from Sydney on the 5th of March, 1868, with a cargo of coals,
bound for Hongkong, and has not since been heard of. Any person ob¬
jecting to probate of the said will being granted as aforesaid, or who can
give any information respecting the said John Maguire, or his said wife
or children, is requested to communicate with Messrs. Andrews and
MacLaine, solicitors for the said executors, No. 60, Donegall-street,
Belfast, Ireland. In the event of no tidings being obtained of the said
John Maguire, showing that he is, or may be supposed to be alive, the
above application for probate (which stands over by order of the said
Court until this advertisement shall have been published in Belfast,
Liverpool, Sydney, and in The Times Newspaper of London) will be
renewed; and such order will be made thereon as to the said Court
shall seem fit; and, if probate shall be granted as aforesaid, the said
executors will be thereby enabled and they intend to administer the
said John Maguire's estate and effects according to the provisions of
the said will ; and unless tidings shall be obtained of his said wife
and children (who take interests under the said will) they will be re¬
spectively assumed to be dead. —Dated this 4th day of March, 1869.
ANDREWS and MAC LAINE, Solicitors for the said Executors,
60, Donegall-street, Belfast, Ireland.
