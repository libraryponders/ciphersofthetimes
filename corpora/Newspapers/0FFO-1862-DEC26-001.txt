NOTICE. —To the WOOL TRADE of GREAT
BRITAIN and FRANCE.—This is to give notice, that on and
after 1st January, 1863, there will be no allowance made for draft on
Irish wool sold in Ireland, all such allowances having been abolished
by an Act of Parliament, introduced by Sir Robert Peel, and passed
into law, 7th August, 1862, under the title of "The Weights and
Measures Act, 25 and 26 Vic., cap. 76," the 13th clause of which relates
to the matter in question:—
"Clause 13. Every article sold by weight shall, if weighed, be
weighed in full net standing beam; and for the purposes of every con¬
tract, bargain, sale, or dealing, the weight so ascertained shall be
deemed the true weight of the article, and no deduction or allowance
for tret or beamage or on any other account, or under any other name
whatsoever, the weight of any sack, bag, cask, firkin, or other covering
in which such article may be alone excepted, shall be claimed or made
by any purchaser on any pretext whatever, under a penalty of not
exceeding s.
Dublin, 17th December, 1862.
