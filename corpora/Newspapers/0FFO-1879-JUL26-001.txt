And whereas, after examination and inquiry, the draft of a Scheme
respecting the said commons has been prepared and printed, pur¬
suant to the said Acts, an abstract of which draft Scheme is here¬

Now the said Inclosure Commissioners hereby give notice that a
printed copy of the said draft Scheme, with a plan of the said com¬
mons annexed, has been deposited at the office of the Leca. Boarl, at
Staines, for the inspection of all persons interested, and ennt during

made to them in writing respecting the said drait Scheme.
(A print of the draft Schoine and a plan of the commons may also

ABSTRACT of DRAFT SCHEME.
1. Staines-commons to be regulated and managed by the Local
Board for the District of Staines.
2. The said Board to preserve the turf and grass, with power to
fence off for short periods such portions as may require rest to revive
the same, to drain, improve, plant, or othereise beautify the com¬
mons, to provide seats for the accommodation of the public, to erect
lodges or other buildings neeessary for the maintenance and manage¬
ment and to set apart or form cricket grounds.
3. The said Board to maintain the commons free from encroach¬
ments, and no fence or other structure to be set up thereon without
their consent in writing.
4. The said Board to frame bye-laws for the preservation of order
and the prevention of nuisances, including all or any of the following
purposes—Lo., the provention of the deposit of rubbish or other
matter, or the illegal taking or injuring of the soil, substrata, or the
produce of the soil, of injury to fences, trees, or seats, of the taking
of birds, birds' nests or oggs, or the shooting or chasing of game or
other animals, and of venicles being driven or borses exercised over
the commons; the regulation of games to be played and of assem¬
blages of persons, and of the use of the rifle ranges on Staines Moor;
the removal of gamblers, cardsharpers, gipsies, vagrants, or any idle
or disorderly persons.
And generally the prevention or restraint of any act or thing tend¬
ing to the injury or disfigurement of the commons or to interference
with the uve there of for purposes of exercise and recreation.
8. With the exception of those who are now by law entitled to do so,
no person to turn out on the commons for grazing any cattle, sheep.
or other animals; and no person other than the owner or owners of
the soil, without the consent in writing of the said Board, to make
any sewers, drains, or other matters of a like nature in or under any
part of the commons.
6. All costs and expenses incurred by the said Board in respect to
the Scheme to be contributed by the said Board as expenses incurred
in the execution of the Public Health Acts.
7. The Scheme reserves all rights of a profitable or beneficial nature
in and over the commons.
8. The Lady of the Manor of Staines, and the owners and occupiers
of lands and tenements in the manor and parish, claim rights and
interests in and over the commons. Such rights to be affected only so
far as is necessary for the purposes of the Scheme.
9. Printed copies of the Scheme, at all times after its confirmation
by Parliament, to be sold to all persons desiring to buy the same at a
price not exceeding sixpence each.
Witness my hand this 10th day of July, 1879,
J. R. MOORE.
