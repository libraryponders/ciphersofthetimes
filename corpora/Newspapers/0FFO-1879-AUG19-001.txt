CHELMSFORD and MALDON RURAL SANI¬
TARY AUTHORITIES.—Appointment of Medical Officer of
Health.—Pursuant to an Order of the Local Government Board, the
Joint Committee appointed by the above Authorities hereby give
notice that they will, on Wednesday, the 3d day of September next,
roceed to the APPOINTMENT of a MEDICAL OFFICER of
HEALTH for the combined Rural Sanitary Districts of Chelmsford
and Maldon, in the county of Essex, comprising 63 parishes, with an
area of 180,792 acres, and a population of 43,853, being the whole of
the Chelmsford and Maldon Poor Law Unions except the parish of
Chelmsford and the Borough of Maldon.
If the Local Government Board should, in pursuance of section 20
of the Public Health Act, 1872, appoint either of the above Autho¬
rities to be a Port Sanitary Authority, the Officer appointed is, if
required by such authority, to act, without extra salary, as the
Medical Officer of Health over the waters and district assigned to
such authority.
The salary will be £600 per annum, to commence from the date of
the confirmation of the appointment by the Local Government
Board (payable quarterly), and will include all travelling and other
expenses and disbursements, except forms, postages, and stationery,
which will be provided by the respective authorities.
The person appointed will be debarred from private practice, and be
required to devote the whole of his time to the dutics of his office; to
reside in some central part of the district to be sanctioned by the
authorities; to attend all meetings and obey all oreers of the respec¬
tive authorities, and to perform all the duties of a Medical Officer of
Health, as prescribed by the order of the Local Government Board of
tue 11th day of November, 1872, or any other orders now or hereafter
to be issued by the said Board. Also to perform all such analyses as
may be required of him by the respective authorities.
The appointment will in the first instance be made for three rea e.
from the date of its confirmation, and will be subject to the approval
of the Local Government Board, and to the right of the authorities,
with the consent of the said Board, to rescind the appointment at any
time on giving one calendar month's previous notice in writing.
Candidates must be legally qualified medical practitioners registered
under the Medical Act, 1856, and must produce their diplomas, certi¬
ficates, or licences to the said Committee.
