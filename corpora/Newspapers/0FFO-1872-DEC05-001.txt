GPPING FOREST COMMISSION.—The Epping
Forest Commissioners hereby give notice that, by Orders dated
respectively the 29th day of November, 1872, made in pursuance of the
authority given to them for that purpose by the "Epping Forest
Amendment Act. 1872," they have ordered, that until the expiration
of the Session of Parlament next after they shall have made their
final Report under the "Epping Forest Act, 1871," or until further
order, all persons whosoever seall be, and they are by the said Orders
prohibited from committing any waste, injury, or destruction of or to
the vert, herbage, trees, shrubs, or other growing things in or upon the
waste land of Epping Forest aforesaid within the respective Manors of
Wanstead with sitone Hall and Theydon Bois, in the County of Essex
(including inclosures of waste lands within the said Manors respectively
made within 20 years next preceding and since the 21st day of August
1871), and all which lands are distinguished on the plans respectively
annexed to the said Orders by the colour green, and all persons affected
by the said Orders respectively, or either of them, are to be at liberty to
apply to the said Commissioners as there may be occasion. Coples of
the said orders and plans are deposited for inspection at the office of
the Commissioners, No. 6, Old Palace-yard, Westminster; the Castle
Hotel, Woodford; the Crown Hotel, Loughton; and the Cock Hotel.
Epping. —Dated this 2nd day of December, 1872.
T. P. WILKINSON, Clerk to the Epping Forest Commissioners.
