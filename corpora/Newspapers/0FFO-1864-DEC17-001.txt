BANK of ENGLAND.—Unclaimed Dividends.—
Application having been made to the Governors of the Bank
of England to direct the payment of three dividends on the sum
of £4,053 1s. 4d. £3 5s. per Cent. Annuities, heretofore standing in the
names of Rev. CHARLES GORE, of Henbury, Glocestershire, de¬
ceased, DANIEL CLUTTERBUCK, of Bradford, Wilts, Esq., de¬
ceased, PHILIP HAYWARD of Chew Stoke, Somerset, Esq.,
deceased, and EDWARD SAMPSON, of Henbury, Glocestershire, Esq.,
deceased, and which dividends were paid over to the Commissioners
for the Reduction of the National Debt in consequence of the first
thereof having remained unclaimed since the 5th April, 1847;—Notice
is hereby given that, on the expiration of three months from this date,
the said Dividends will be paid to Edward Sampson, the sole executor
of Edward Sampson, deceased, who was the survivor, who has
claimed the same, unless some other claimant shall sooner appear and
make out his claim thereto.
