SALMON. FISHERY ACTS,
1861 to 1876.—
CLEDDY FISHERY DISTRICT.—Whereas the Fishery Dis¬
trict of the Rivers East and West Cleddy was formed and its limits
were defined by a certificate, under the hand of the Right Honourable
S. H. Walpole, dated the 30th day of July, 1876, and whereas it is expe¬
dient to ENLARGE the LIMITS of the said FISHERY DISTRICT.
by including therein so much of the const of Pembrokeshire as lies be¬
tween Dinas Head and Strumble Head, and all revers flowing into the
sea between the said points, and being within the said county.
Now, I, the Right Honourable Richard Assheton Cross, one of Her
Majesty's Principal Secretaries of State, do, by virtue of the powers
entrusted to me by the Salmon Fishery Acts, 1861 to 1876, hereby de¬

"The limits of the Cleddy Fishery District, so enlarged as aforesaid,
shall include so much of the Rivers East Cloidy and West Cleddy,
and their tributaries, as is situate within the counties of Pembroke
and Carmarthen; the estuaries of the said rivers; so much of the
coast as lies between Dinas Head, in the county of Pembroke, and St.
Govin's Head, in the same county; and all rivers flowing into the sea
between the said points, and being within the said counties,."
Given under my band, this 16th day of June, 1877.
Whitelall.
RICH. ASSHETON CROSS.
