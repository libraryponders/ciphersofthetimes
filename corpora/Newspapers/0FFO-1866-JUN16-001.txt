by the Banque de Credit Commercial (established in this town), and
also by Mr. Julien de Meester, co-Commissary and Comptroller; con¬
sidering also the decision of this Court on the 8th inst., concerning the
petition presented. Whereas it happens that Louis Lodewyckx and
Ferdinand Lagrelle did not accept the office assigned to them by the
said decision, the Tribunal appoints in their place Guillaume Declercq,
merchant and Judge of this Court, and George Philippe Berdolh, mer¬
chant and Member of the Chamber of Commerce, both of them dwell¬
ing at Antwerp, as Commissaries Comptrollers of the Banque de Credit
Commercial, established at Antwerp, and this during the whole time of
the temporary delay for payment granted, the said Commissaries Comp¬
trollers to take the oath previously as prescribed by Art. 602 (new) of the
Code of Commerce ; the expenses to be at the charge of the Banque.
Given at Antwerp the 13th June, 1866, at half-past 10 o'clock a.m.
