of the High Court of Chancery, dated the 17th day of July, 1863,
made in a cause wherein Joseph Forster Lloyd, of Wednesbury, in the
county of Stafford, coal and iron master, Wilson Lloyd, of Madeley
in the county of Stafford, coalmaster, and William Henry Lloyd, of
Madeley aforesaid, coalmaster are plaintiffs, and Daniel Pearson, of
Madeley aforesaid, and of Dudley Port, in the said county of Stafford,

business in partnership, at Madeley aforesaid, as coal and iron masters,
under the style or firm of the MADELEY COAL and IRON COM¬
PANY), it was declared that the PARTNERSHIP formerly existing
and corried on by the plaintiffs with the defendant ought to stand and
be DISSOLVED as from the 10th day of March, 1863, and the same
was decreed accordingly.
IN the COURT of the COMMISSIONERS for the
SALE of INCUMBERED ESTATES in the WEST INDIES (St.
Vincent).—In the Matter of the ESTATE of ALEXANDER McLEOD,
deceased, exparte John Cochrane and Thomas Patterson, Petitioners,
the 5th day of September, 1863. — Conditional Order for Sale. — Whereas.
a PETITION was presented to the Commissioners on the 1st day of
June, 1863, by John' Cochrane and Thomas Patterson, praying that
the premises mentioned in the first schedule thereto, or such part
thereof as the Commissioners should direct, might be sold, whereupon
and upon reading the said petition, the schedules annexed thereto,
the abstract of title filed therewith, and the affidavit of Richard
Walter Tweedie, verifying the same, it is ordered by Henry James
Stonor, Esq., Chief Commissioner, that the lands, plantations, and
premises in the said first schedule set forth.—that is to say ONE
UNDIVIDED MOIETY of all that PLANTATION or ESTATE
known by the name of Orange-hill Estate, in the parish of Charlotte,
in the Island of St. Vincent, and of all Lands annexed or appurtenant
thereto, be SOLD, at the office of the Commissioners, 8, Park-street,
Westminster, on Tuesday, the 8th day of March, 1864, or on such sub¬
sequent day as the Commissioners shall direct, for the purpose of dis¬
charging the incumbrances thereon, unless cause be shown to the
contrary before the 5th day of December, 1863; and it is further
ordered that office copies of this order be served forthwith on the
devisees of Alexander McLeod, or their representatives, and that this
order be duly advertised.
REGINALD J. CUST, Secretary.
(Seal of the West Indian Incumbered Estates Commission.)
Note.—Any person desirous of showing cause against this order
must lodge at the office of the Commissioners, 8, Park-street, West¬
minster, before the 5th day of December, 1863, a notice, stating briefly
the grounds on which he relies, verified by affidavit, and the same will
be considered and disposed of on or after the 12th day of December,
1863, as the Commissioners shall direct.
BOYS and TWEEDIES, 6, Ely-place, London, E.C.,
Solicitors for the Petitioners.
