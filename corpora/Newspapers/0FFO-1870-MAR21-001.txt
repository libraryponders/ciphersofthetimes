CHARITY COMMISSION.—In the Matter of the
several CHARITIES founded by the settlement and will of
CHRISTOPHER TANCRED, of Whixley, in the county of York,
Esq., deceased.—By direction of the Board of Charity Commis¬
sioners for England and Wales, notice is hereby given, that an ORDER¬
is proposed to be made by them, after the expiration of one calendar
month, to be computed from the first publication of this notice, having
the following objects, namely:—Establishing a scheme enabling the
Governors, after the payment of a fixed stipend of £100 a year to each
of the four students in Divinity, Medicine, and Law respectively, to
apply the surplus insome of the endowment applicable for the benefit
of the said students, and also the income arising from any invested
accumulations of such surplus, in extending the number of student¬
ships in any of the three specified classes, the stipends of such addi¬
tional students not to exceed £100 a year each, and to be paid out of
the income applicable to the class to which they shall respectively
belong.
Any objections to the proposed order, or suggestions for the modi¬
tication thereof, may be transmitted to the said Board, in writing, ad¬
dressed to their Secretary, No. 8, York-street, St. James's-square,
London, within 21 days next after the first publication of this notice.—
Dated this 7th day of March, 1870.
(Signed)
