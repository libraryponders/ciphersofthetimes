sion.—1879 O. No. 125.—Ships FUSI YAMA MOWE and
SEVERN.—Between the Owners of the Fusi Yama Plaintiffs and
the North German Lloyds and others Defendants.—Whereas in an
Action of Limitation of Liabdity instituted in the said High Court of
Justice on behalf of the owners of the steamship or vessel Fusi Yama
against the North German Lloyds and others, the Judge of the Ad¬
mirally Division of the said High Court did on the 24th day of June
1879 amongst other things pronounce that in respect of LOSS or
DAMAGE to Shins Goods Merchandize or other things caused by
reason of the IMPROPER NAVIGATION of the steam vessel Fusi
Yama on the occasion of the collision between the said steam vessel
and the steam vessels Mowe and Severn the owners of the said steam
vessel Fusi Yama are answerable in damages to an amount not ex¬
cceding £7,442 3s. 2d. (such sum boing at the rate of £8 for each ton of
the registered tonnage of the said steam vessel Fusi Yama without
deduction on account of Engine Room) This is therefore to give notice
to all persons having any Claims in respect of the loss or damage
caused as aforesaid that if they do not come in and enter their Claims
in the said Action on or before the 29th day of July next they will be
excluded from sharing in the aforesaid amount Dated this 30th day
