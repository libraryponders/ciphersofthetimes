IN the HIGH COURT of ADMIRALTY of
ENGLAND.—The LENA.—No. 579.—Whereas a cause of SAL¬
VAGE has been instituted in the High Court of Admiralty of England,
on behalf of the owners of the iron screw steam ship or vessel Hawk,
against the brig or vessel Lena (of Lynn), her tackle, apparel, and fur¬
niture, and the cargo now or lately laden therein, together with the
freight due for the transportation thereof, and whereas the said vessel
and cargo are now lying in Lowestoft Harbour, in the county of Suffolk,
under arrest, by virtue of a warrant issued from the registry of the
said Court, and no appearance has been entered in the said cause, this
is to give notice to all persons who have or claim to have any right,
title, or interest in the said vessel Lena, her tackle, apparel, and furni¬
ture, or in the said cargo, that, if an appearance in the said cause be
not entered in the registry of the said Court within six days from the
proclamation of this notice, the Judge of the said Court will order the
said vessel, her tackle, apparel, and furniture, and the said cargo, to be
sold to answer the claims instituted or to be instituted against the
same, or make such order in the premises as to him shall seem right.—
Dated the 4th day of February, 1861.
H. A. BATHURST, Assistant Registrar.
