CHANGE of NAME and ASSUMPTION of ARMS.
Notice is hereby given, that Sir JOHN ERRINGTON, of Pud¬
dington, Cheshire, and Great Glemham, Wiskham Market, Suffolk,
Baronet (heretofore sir John Massey Stanley), having succeeded upon
the death of his brother, Sir Rowland Errington, Barenet, to the
estates devised by the will of Henry Errington, Esquire, deceased,
has, in pursuance of the direction contsined in such will, taken upon
tumself and will henceforth USE, the SURNAME and ARMS of
ERRINGTON only. Sir John Errington has petitioned to the Crown
for the grant of a Royal license to enable him to take and use such
surname and arms only but the consideration of his petition is post¬
poned until the suits of Hervey Bathurit v. Stanley and Craven v.
Stanley, now pending in the Chancery Division of the High Court of
Justice have been decided. Sir John Errington has by deed, bearing
date the 2d day of March, 1876, under his hand and sea), and duly
enrolled in the Chancery Division of the High Court of Justice, ac¬
knowledged and declared and recorded the foregoing facts.
