TWENTY POUNDS REWARD. —Wm. Till,
aged eight months, deserted. Whereas, in the month of April
last, a person calling himself CHARLES TILL, having made an en¬
gagement with a married woman, residing at Islington, as wet nurse,
for the nurture of a child, she, accompanied by her sister, met him
(by appointment) at London-bridge Railway Station, and her sister pro¬
ceeded with him to the railway station at Tunbridge-wells, where the
said child was delivered into the charge of the sister, and was brought
by her to the nurse, with whom it has since remained, but the contri¬
bution towards the child's support having ceased, the nurse has been
compelled to apply to the authorities of the parish of St. Mary, Isling¬
ton, for relief for the said child, and the said child is now chargeable
to that parish; Notice is hereby given, that a reward of £20 will be
paid by the Trustees of the said parish for such INFORMATION as
shall lead to the apprehension and conviction of the offender. The
said Charles Till is supposed to have resided at Tranquil-vale, Black¬
heath; at Greenwich: at East Mount, St. Leonard's; and at Cecil¬
house, Margate. Application to be made to the Clerk to the Trustees,
Parochial-offices, Liverpool-road, Islington, Middlesex.
