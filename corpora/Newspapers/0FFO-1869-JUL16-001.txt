THE UNIVERSITY COLLEGE (LONDON) ACT,
1869.—Notice is hereby given, that by Section 11 of the above¬
mentioned Act, which was passed on the 24th June, 1869, it is enacted
as follows:—
"The executors, or administrators, or assignees respectively of any
proprietor of a share who may within six years immediately preceding
the passing of this Act have died or become bankrupt shall have
the option (to be exercisable within six months after the passing of
this Act, and to be signified in writing, addressed to the Secretary of
the College, at the office thereof) of nominating a person, subject to the
approval of the Council, to be a Governor of the College, and if such
person shall be approved he shall thereupon, as from the date of such
approval, become a Governor of the College, and every such person
shall be deemed to be approved unless disapproved by the Council
(such disapproval to be signified in writing within six months after his
nomination); and if the executors, or administrators, or assignees re¬
spectively of the last proprietor of any such shares do not within six
months after the passing of this Act propose some person who shall be
approved by the Council to fill the place of such proprietor as afore¬
said, all right or interest in respect of such shares shall cease and de¬
termine at the expiration of such six months, or from the date of the
disapproval by the Council of the person nominated in respect thereof
(whichever shall last happen), and the said shares shall thenceforth

The office of the College is at University College, Gower-street,
London, W.C.
JOHN ROBSON, B.A., Secretary of the College.
