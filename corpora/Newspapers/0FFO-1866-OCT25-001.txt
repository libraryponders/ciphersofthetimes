FARRINGDON WARD WITHIN, Oct. 24, 1866.—
At a WARDMOTE holden this day, at the Queen's Arms
Tavern, Newgate-street, before GEORGE VIRTUE, Esq., Deputy of
the said Ward, for the CHOICE of a COMMON COUNCILMAN for
the remainder of the year, in the room and stead of George Dollond,
Esq., deceased, Rowland Faulkner Potter, Esq., was unanimously¬
elected to fill the said office.
It was moved by Deputy Charles Reed, F.S.A., seconded by John
Kelday, Esq., and resolved unanimously;
That this Wardmote desires to resord its sense of the loss sustained
by the Ward of Farringdon Within by the lamented death of their re¬
spected representative, George Dollond, Esq., who has for many years,
as a member of the Court of Common Council, devoted himself with
great assiduity and zeal to the interest of this Ward and of the citizens
at large.
It was moved by Benjamin Pardon, Esq., seconded by George Shaw,
Esq., and res eie

That the thanks of this Wardmote be given to George Virtue, Esq.
(Deputy), for the able manner in which he has presided over the busi¬
ness of this day; that the foregoing resolutions be signed by the Ward
Clerk and advertised.
J. W. WARREN, Ward Clerk.
