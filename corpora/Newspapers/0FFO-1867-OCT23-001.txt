ST a MEETING of Members of the HOME
CATTLE DEFENCE ASSOCIATION, held at the Salisbury
Hotel, Salisbury-square, Monday, October 21st, it was resolved unani¬
mously:—
1. That in the opinion of this Association the Government have not
acted with that good faith to which it was pledged after the refection of
Mr. Conance's amendment which provided for the closing of Thames
Haven as a landing place for foreign stock in the port of London.
. 2. That in the opinion of this Association nothing short of the
establishment of a separate market as near as practicable to the me¬
tropolis, as described by the Metropolis Local Management Act, for
the sale and slaughter of foreign fat animals, will give security and
allow that free transit to the herds of English stock which is so im¬
portant to encourage their production and ensure their safety.
(Signed) JOHN CLAYDEN, Chairman.
No. 81, Fenchurch-Street.
