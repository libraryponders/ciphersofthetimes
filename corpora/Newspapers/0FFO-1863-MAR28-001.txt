MARRIAGES in HONOUR of the MARRIAGE
of the Prince of WALES.—The Incumbent of St. Thomas's,
Lambeth, last week gave notice to his poor people that he would, on
Easter Monday next, perform the marriage service without fees. Forty¬
six couples, gladly availing themselves of the offer (which they describe
as the greatest charity that could be done them), will be married on
that morning. Will any benevolent persons endeavour to further the
Incumbent's moral infiuence over these persons, and add to the kind¬
ness shown them, by ASSISTING him to provide them with a substan¬
tial meal on their Wedding-day?
St. Thomas's Parsonage, Westminster-road, S., March 26, 1863.
MARRIAGE LAW REFORM ASSOCIATION,
instituted for the exclusive object of Promoting the Passing of
an Act to render Lawful Marriage with a Deceased Wife's Sister.—
Information respecting the law applicable to these Marriages, in this
and foreign countries, may be obtained on application, personally or
by letter, at the offices of the Association; where may also be had
numerous publications, showing the opinions of eminent statesmen,
divines, and others, in favour of the repeal of the present prohibition.
There is now no known country in the world, except our own where
a man may not contract a lawful marriage with his deceased wife's
sister.
JOSEPH STANS BURY, M.A., Hon. Sec.
