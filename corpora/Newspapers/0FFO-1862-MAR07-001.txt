MESSRS. RUDOLPH v. FRITTHOFF and
AUGUST von WENNDRICH, Judges, being the judicially
confirmed guardians of the minor son of the late Count Erich Friedrich,
Magnus Diedrich Stenbock (who died at Castle Rottenstein) and
Countess Lucy Stenbock nee Frerichs, named Count Erich Magnus
Andreas Harry Stenbock, have petitioned to the Imperial Court of
Justice for publishing a proclamation, firstly, regarding the agreement
proposed between the present lord of the estates, Kolck, Kyda, Konda,
and Neuenhoff, situate, in Ehstland, the Lieutenant-Colonel and
Chevalier Count Carl Magnus Stenbock, and his deceased eldest son,
the beforementioned Count Erich Stenbock, and countersigned by
Mrs. Lieutenant, Colonel, Theophile, Countess Stenbock nee von
Stuart, by the Countess Dowager Lucy Stenbock née Frerichs, by their
trustees, two witnesses (Mr. R. von Fritthoff and Ensign Count
Michael Stenbock, of the Life Guard Dragoon Regiment), and provided
with various additaments, and, at the same time, with a specification
of the debts of Lientenant-Colonel Count C. M. Stenbock and the late
Count Erich Stenbock, in which are contained, among other items.
stipulations about the rules of succession and entry into the estates of
Kolck, Kyda, Konda, and Neuenhoff, on the part of the late Count
Erich Stenbock, with certain rights and authorities reserved to the
Lieutenant-Colonel Count C. M. Stenbock, as also the settlement of
the above-mentioned debts of Count Carl Magnus and Count Erich
Stenbock from the revenues of the said estates; seeondly, regarding the
will made at Reval on the 27th July, 1860, by the late Count Erich
Stenbock, and published in due order on the 4th May, 1861, in the
Supreme Country Court; and, thirdly, regarding the heritage of the
same Count Erich Stenbock. In accordance with the above, the Impe¬
rial Ehstland Supreme Country Court, in order to give all parties
interested in these concerns an opportunity of looking after their
rights, under distinct reservation of the necessary examination and
judgment of the case, relating to the legal requisites for adjustment to
which the Imperial Ehstland Country Orphan Court is entitled as the
competent authorities of head guardianship on account of the pupilary
interest attached to this case, as also the imperial Ehstland Supreme
Country Court being appointed to watch the affairs officially, SUM¬
MONS all and every one who, as HEIRS to Count ERICH STEN¬
BOCK, or from other judicial reason, and also as Creditors of Lieu¬
tenant-Colonel and Chevalier Count Carl Magnus Stenbock, intend
to urge claims or raise objections against—1. The above-mentioned
agreement and additamens concluded among various members of the
family of Count Stenbock on the 31st March, 1857, regarding the
estates Kolck, Kyda, Konda, and Neuenhoff; 2. Against the said will
of Count Erich Stenbock; and 3. Against the heritage of the late
Count Erich Stenbock, who died at Rottenstein Castle; or who are in¬
debted to, or hold funds, documents, or effects from the deceased, to
appear, within a term of one year from the date hereof, with their
claims or objections and their justifications, before the Imperial
Ehstland Supreme Country Court, and to have them duly registered,
warning all heirs and claimants who neglect the term, that they will
remain without notice ; and the debtors who do not timely appear
shall be sentenced to the payment of double the amount of their debts;
and that, after the expiration of this proclamation, the law will take
its course both in respect of the will of Count Erich Stenbock as also
of the agreement on Count Stenbock's estates, Kolck, Kyda, Konda,
and Nenenhoff.—Signed and sealed at His Imperial Majesty's Supreme
Country Court at Reval, 29th November, 1861. In fidem.
C. E. KOCH, Secretary to H.I.M. Supreme Country Court.
