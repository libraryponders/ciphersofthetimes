having for its object the utilization of the sewage of the district of the
said Board, a certain PIECE of LAND belonging to the Dean and
Chapter of Christ Church, Oxford, now in possession or occupation of
Henry Young, Esq., or his undertenant, and which piece of land is
numbered 472 on the Ordinance Map of the parish of Harrow, in the
hundred of Gore, in the county of Middlesex, and contains 15 acres or
thereabouts, and also a certain piece of land belonging to the trustees
of the estate of the late Richard Heming, and now in the possession or
occupation of Mr. James Row or his undertenant, and which piece of
land is numbered 512, on the aforesaid map, and contains two acres or
thereabouts, and which two pieces of land are situate between a brook
running from Harrow aforesaid to Kenton, called the Kenton Brook,
and the London and North-Western Railway; and notice is hereby
further given, that a plan of the said land and of the proposed under¬
taking may be seen any day (except Sundays), between the hours of 9
in the morning and 8 in the evening, at the office of the said Board, at
Mr. William Woodbridge's, Harrow aforesaid. — Dated this 15th day of
November, 1866.
