COMPANY of London have reason to believe that many guns and
pistols, principally of foreign manufacture, the barrels of which have
never been proved, as with a view to the safety of the public is re¬
quired by "The Gun Barrel Proof Act, 1855," and that such unproved
small arms are frequently used by sportsmen, in ignorance of the fact
that they have not been submitted to the test of soundness and safety,
an omission which experience has proved may at any moment endanger

to take notice that such unproved small arms are liable to seizure
under the above Act, and are also CAUTIONED against selling, pur¬
