MAURITIUS GOVERNMENT RAILWAYS.—The
Government of the Mauritius REQUIRE the SERVICES of a
GENERAL MANAGER, to undertake the administration of the
railways (about 87 miles in length) in that Colony.
He must reside in Mauritius and will have to devote his whole time
and services to the duties connected with the post, under an agree¬
ment for a period of not less than three years.
In addition to a thorough acquaintance with the details of railway
management, candidates must possess such knowledge of practical
engineering as will qualify them for the general direction of locomo¬
tive and maintenance works. A colloquial knowledge of the French
language is desirable.
The salary will be rupees 11,000 per annum, with free passage out
and (conditionally) home again.
Written applications, stating the age and antecedents of the appli¬
cant, and accompanied by copies (not originals) of testimonials, which
will be treated as confidential, may be addressed to the Crown Agents
for the Colonies, Downing-street, London, up to the 20th instant in¬
