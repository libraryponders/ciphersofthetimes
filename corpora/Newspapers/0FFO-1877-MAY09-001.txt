SEA.—CACTION.—To Parents and Guardians.—
On the 14th April, 1877, William Beaujoy (alias Brett), a Member
or a Firm styling itself Brett and Company, was proceeded against
before the Lord Mayor for having, without the necessary licence from
the Board of Trade, supplied an apprentice for the British Slup Slieve
bloom, in violation of the Provisions of Section 197 of the Mer¬
chant Shipping Act, 1864, and was convicted and fined £5 and costs.
Parents and Guardians are informed that if they wish to send a boy to
sea they suould communicate with the Superintendent at the Mercan¬
tile Marine Office at any port in the United Kingdom, or be careful
to ascertam whether the person (other than the owner or master or
their duly apporated agent) undertakins to find a ship for a boy
possesses a licence from the Board of Trade. By order of the Board
of Trade, Marine Department.
