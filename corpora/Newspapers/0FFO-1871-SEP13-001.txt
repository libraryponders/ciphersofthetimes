IN Her MAJESTYS COURT of PROBATE, Prin¬
cipal Registry.—The Reverend MATTHEW LOWNDES, clerk, de¬
ceased.—Matthew Lowndes, late of the Vicarage at Buckfastleigh, in
the county of Devon, clerk in Holy Orders, duly executed his will on the
16th January, 1856, in the presence of George Upton and Mary Upton.
By this will he devised his Vicarage of Buckfastleigh, with the glebe
lands, &c., to the Reverend Richard Champernowne, clerk, curate of
Dartington, Devon, upon certain trusts therein mentioned, and devised
other parts of his real estate to his children, and after giving a certain
portion of his personal estate to his daughter. Anna Julia Elizabeth
Donville Lowndes, he directed the residue of ais furniture and cattle,
and plate and jewellery, to be equally divided between his three chil¬
dren. He appointed his wife, Sophia Elizabeth Templer Lowndes, and
Mrs. Sarah Ellis executrixes. At the foot or end of the said will the
said testator wrote and signed, but did not duly execute, a codicil, dated
May 30th, 1856. He died on the 2nd day of June, 1856. A few
weeks after his death a true copy of the will and codicil was made from
the original, but the latter cannot now be found. Whoever will bring
the ORIGINAL WILL and CODICIL, or give such information as
may lead to its discovery, to Mr. John Elliott Fox, of 65, Chancery-lane,
London, W.C., solicitor, will be REWARDED.
