NOTICE.—Assumption of Additional Surname.—
Craven—Chrimes.—Whereas, in the year 1867, PHILIP HENRY
CRAVEN, the only son of Philip Henry Craven, of Rotherham, in
the county of York, and Emma his wife, was adopted by Richard
Chuames, of Rotherham, in the county of York, Esquire, and has since
heen known as Philip Henry Craven Chrimes. And whereas the
said Philip Henry Craven Chrimes has recently attained the age of 21
years, and with the consent of the said Richard Chrimes has deter¬
mined to permanently ASSUME and USE the SURNAME of
CHRIMES, in addition to and after his original name of Craven, and
for the sake of preserving ev dence thereof has by an Indenture, dated
the 5th day of February, 1873, and made between the said Philip
Henry Craven of the one part, and the said Richard Chrimes of the
other part, declared that he and his issue would thenceforth use the
surname of Chrimes. And whereas the said Indenture was on the
9th day of March, 1878, enrolled in the Chaneery Bivision of Her
Majesty's High Court of Justice, Now, therefore, the undersigned
Philip Henry Craven Chrimes (formerly Philip Henry Craven) doth
hereby give notice to all whom it may concern that he has assumed
and will henceforth use the surname of Chrimes, in addition to his
original surname of Craven, but so that the name of Chrimes shall be
the grincipal or only surname, and that he will in all deeds and writ¬
ings, for all purposes, and on all occasions, use and be known by the
name of Philip Henry Craven Chrims. Dated the 18th day of May,
1878.
PHILIP HENRY CRAVEN CHRIMES.
Witness to the signature of the said Philip Henry Cravan Chrimes,
Robert Pashley, of the firm of Oxley, Pashley, and Coward, of Rother¬
ham, Solicitors for the said Richard Chrimes and Philip Henry
Craven Chrimes.
