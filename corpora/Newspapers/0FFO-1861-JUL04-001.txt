CLIFFORD's PATENT BOAT LOWERING GEAR.—The following
statement, in reference to the loss of a number of the passengers and
crew by the capsizing of one of the boats when being lowered from
the Canadian steamship, having appeared in very many of the London
and country papers, viz, that "Everything being ready, the passengers
and crew got into the boats, all of which were lowered safely, with this
sad exception, one of the boats fitted with Clifford's patent lowering
apparatus capsized when being let loose from the davits, and all on
board perished."—As Clifford's gear was not fitted to the boat alluded
to (see letter below), and written contradiction of this statement
having failed to put a stop to its insertion in certain papers, public
notice is hereby given, that legal proceedings will be taken against
all those who have knowingly circulated so injurious a report, or shall

Liverpool, June 28, 1861.
