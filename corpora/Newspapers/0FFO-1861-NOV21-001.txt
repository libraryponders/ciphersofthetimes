CUBITT, Mayor.
THE First Court of Mayor and Aldermen, held in
the Inner Chamber of the Guildhall of the City of London, on
Tuesday, the 19th day of November, 1861, and in the 25th year of the
reign of Victoria, of the united kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland,

Resolved unanimously.
That the sincere and cordial thanks of this Court be presented to
the Right Hon. William Cubitt, Lord Mayor of this city, for the emi¬
nent and efficient services he has rendered by the dignified manner
in which he has discharged his many and important duties as chief
magistrate.
That this Court is anxious to record its deep feeling of approbation
of the energy displayed by him in originating the fund for the relief
of the famine which was desolating so large a portion of our Indian
possessions, and which, by the timely reliet which was so promptly
sent to our suffering fellow-creatures, was the means of saving so many

That this Court also desires to express its firm conviction that,
having so secured the confidence of his fellow-citi-ens as to be called
upon a second time to fill the important office of Lord Mayor, he will
continue to maintain the dignity, and uphold the privileges of the ci
of London.

THE ARCHITECTURAL EXHIBITION, 1862.—
The Committee beg to announce that the EXHIBITION will
OPEN as usual next year. All works to be sent to 9, Conduit-street, on
the first Monday in March. Carriage from and to the country will be
paid. The Committee beg to remind architects that this exhibition
will certainly be seen by visitors of all nations, and they trust that the
collection of designs and models will worthily represent the position of
the art in Great Britain.
