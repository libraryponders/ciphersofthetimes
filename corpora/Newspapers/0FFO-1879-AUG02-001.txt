GUMBERLAND.—Pursuant to the Act 28 and 29
Victoria, c. 121, sec. 5, I do hereby give notice that I have this
day received from the Ritht Honourable the Secretary of State for
the Home Department the following certificate, namely: —Salmon
Fishery Acts, 1861 to 1876.—West Cumber and.—In exercise of the
powers conferred upon me by the provisions of the 4th and 5th sections
of the Salmon Fishery Act, 1865, I, the Right Honourable Richard
Assheton Cross, one of Her Majesty's Principal) Secretaries of State,
do hereby FORM into a FISHERY DISTRICT the rivers Mite, Esk,
Irt. Calder, and Ehen, as hereinafter defined, and do declare that the
limits of the rivers Mite, Esk, Irt, Calder, and Ehen, and of the
Fishery District of the said rivers shall include the following:—
So much of the said Rivers Mite, Esk, Irt, and Calder, and their
tributarics as lies within the county of Cumberland; so much of the
river Ehen and its tributaries as lies below the weir at the foot of
Enperdale Lake, belonging to the Trustees of the Town and Harbour
of Whitehaven, the estuaries of the said rivers: so much of the coast
as lies between the North Head Lighthouse, near St. Bees Head, on
the north, and Haverigg Point on the south, being the northern
boundary of the Fishery District of the Rivers Kent, Bela, Winster,
Leven, and Duddon; all rivers flowing into the sea between the said
points, and being within the said county : the space between o dinary
nigh and low water mark (hereinafter termed the seashore, between
the said points; and the sea adjoining to the seashore between the
said points for the distance of three miles seaward from ordinary low
water mark, as laid down on the annexed map deposited with the
Clerk of the Peace for the County of Cumberland.
I do hereby certify that the Fishery District of the Rivers Mite,
Esk. Irt, Calder, and Ehen, as above defines is duly formed by me.
Given under my hand this 16th day of July, 1879.
Hlome Office, Whitehall. RICHARD ASSHETON CROSS.

this 24th day of July 1879.
