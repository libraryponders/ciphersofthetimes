PARISH of ST. BOTOLPH, ALDGATE, London.
—TITHES.—In the year 1824 subscriptions were received from
inhabitants and other persons interested in houses and premises in
this parish in aid of an inquiry into the right of the lay impropriator
to the payment of tithes at the rate of 2s. 9d. in the pound. Of the
fund so raised there remaius a small balance invested in the funds,
with an accumulation of dividends and interest thereon, which would
produce about 6d. in the pound on the amount so subscribed. Notice
is therefore hereby given, that such of the subscribers to the said fund
as are now living, and the personal representatives of such as are now
deceased, who may desire to receive his or their proportion of the
fund so remaining, may, on giving written notice of such desire, with
the particulars of his or their claim, to John Annis, Esq., the sur¬
viving trustee, at his house, 121, Minories, within one month from the
date hereof, receive the same; after which time the said John Annis
will proceed to distribute the fund, or the balance then remaining un¬
claimed, among certain of the parochial charities, in accordance with
a proposition for such distribution, made at a meeting of the subscri¬
bers to the fund, held this day. — Dated 17th December, 1861.
W. M. BATHO, Solicitor to the surviving Trus
ee.
