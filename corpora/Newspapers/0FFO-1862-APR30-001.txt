THE Rev. C. H. SPURGEON and ABINGTON.—
I, the undersigned George Abington, of 107, Shoe-lane, in the
city of London, publisher, hereby acknowledge that I have inserted
the name of the Rev. C. H. Spurgeon upon the announce-bills and
title-page of a pamphlet, published by me, entitled "The Pitiful and
Heartrending Confession of Laura Raynor," without his authority,
and that he has no knowledge of, and is in no way connected with, the
authorship of the said pamphlet, and I hereby APOLOGIZE for the
unjustifiable USE I have made of his NAME, promise to withdraw it
from all the existing copies of the work, and thank the Rev. C. H.
SPURGEON for his forbearance in not instituting legal proceedings
against me. — Dated this 28th day of April, 1862.
GEORGE ABINGTON.
Witness—Thos. Hudspith, Clerk to Messrs. Roscoe and Hincks,

ng-street, Finsbury-square, Solicitors for the Rev.

MADRAS MILITARY FUND.—SUBSCRIBERS
receiving PAY in England from Her Majesty's Government are
informed they must make arrangements for the regular payment of
their donations and subscriptions to the undersigned, or the regimental
agents by whom pay is issued will recover the amount upon the sub¬
scriber requesting them to do so, but any arrear from any cause equal
to six months' subscription will involve heavy penalties. Indian officers
who have joined the line are not thereby exempted from continuing
their subscriptions to the Madras Military Fund.
By order of the Home Committee,
GRINDLAY and C., Agents M. M. Fund.
Madras Military Fund office, 55 Parliament-street, S.W.,
April 22, 1862.
