DVERTISEMENT.—In pursuance of Governmen¬
tal orders, the STEAM CORVETTE, DANZIG, having been judged
unfit for further Royal maritime service, is to be SOLD publicly to the
highest bidder, together with her entire inventory, rigging, and engine,
exclusive however of her artillery equipments, on the 13th December,
in the forenoon, at 9 o'clock, upon the Royal-wharf. Danzig. The list of
the inventory, and a minute description of the vesseland engine, are laid
out for public inspection in the registry of the undersigned administra¬
tion, in the offices of the Prussian Consulate at London, Liverpool,
and Portsmouth. Copies of the same may be obtained on payment of
the copying charges. Offers may be tendered by word of mouth as
well as in writing, but must be accompanied by a deposit of 2,000 dol¬
lars, either in ready cash or in stocks or shares, at the exchange noted
on the day of the deposit. The vessel is a paddle-wheel steam vessel,
built in the years 1849 to 1851, in Danzig: her length on deck is 219 ft.
6 in., breadth extreme 33ft. 4 in., depth in hold 21 ft. 3 in., tonnage 1,402?
tons. She has substantial between decks, and is strengthened by iron
diagonal bars, iron knees and bands, copper fastened and coppered,
and rigged as a bark. The engine (400 horse power nominal) was
built in 1851, by Robinson, Russel, and Co., in Millwall.—Danzig,
Sept. 30, 1862.
