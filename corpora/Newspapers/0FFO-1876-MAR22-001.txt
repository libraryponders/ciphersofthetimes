THAMES CONSERVANCY NAVIGATION.—
Notice is hereby given, that the Conservators of the River Thames
propose to make the following BYE-LAWS for the REGULATION
of the NAVIGATION of the RIVER THAMES:—The Conservators
of the River Thames, in exercise of the powers and authority vested
in them by "The Thames Conservancy Acts, 1857 and 1864," "The
Thames Navigation Act, 1800," "The Thames Conservancy Act,
1867," and "Tue Thames Navigation Act, 1870," and of every other
authority them hereunto in anywise enabling, do order and direct as
follows, that is to say.—1. Bye-law No. 14 of the Bye-laws of 1672 for
the regulation of the Navigation of the River Thames allowed by
order of Her Majesty in Council, on the 5th February, 1872, shall, after
these present Bye-laws shall have been allowed by order of Her Ma¬
jesty in Council, be, and the Same is hereby repealed, and, in heu thereof.
—2. All vessels navigating the river between the Albert-bridge, at
Chelsea, and Charlton Pier, shall be navigated singly and separately,
except small boats fastened together, or towed alongside or astern of
other vessels, and except vessels towed by steam. 3. Vessels towed
together by steam shall be placed two abreast if more thaf four
in number, and not more than six shall be towed together at one
time. 4. Above and to the westward of tlie Albert-pridge, at Chelsea,
six vessels, and no more, may be towed together in a single line, at
one time ; the distance between any two of the vessels so towed shall
not exceed 50 feet. Any objection to, or represontation respecting
the above proposed Bye-laws snould be sent to or left with the Secru¬
tary of the Conservators, at No. 41, Trinity-square, Tower-hill, on or
belore the 1st day of May next.
E. BURSTAL, Secretary.
No. 41, Trinity-square, Tower-hill, 18th March, 1876.
