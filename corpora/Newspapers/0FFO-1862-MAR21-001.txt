ST. LEONARD, SHOREDITCH, Middlesex.—
Metropolis Local Management Act, 1855.—At a MEETING of the
Vestry of the parish of St. Leonard, Shoreditch, Middlesex, on
Tuesday, the 18th of March, 1862, it was resolved unanimously, That
this Vestry feel themselves bound to express their surprise at the
clauses proposed by Mr. Avrton to be inserted in the Bill, now before
the House of Commons, "To Amend the Metropolis Local Manage¬
ment Act," especially as to increasing the number of the members
of the Metropolitan Board of Works from 45 to 172, their election by
the ratepayers instead of the Vestry, and the election of chairman to
the Vestry by the ratepayers instead of the Vestry appointing their own
chairman; and also the absolute power given to committees without
control, and the complicated machinery in which it is proposed to be

Taken from the minutes of the Vestry.
W. G. DAVIS, Vestry Cle.
