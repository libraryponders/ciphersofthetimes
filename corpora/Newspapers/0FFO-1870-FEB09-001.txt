RIDDLES PATENTS. —Public NOTICE.—
"Spear" Baling.—As proceedings are pending in the superior
Courts against infringers of these patents and others, notice is hereby
given, that Riddle's first English patent involving use of the spear is
dated May 8th, 1862; that other English, American, and Indian
patents granted to him are in force for this invention; that all packers
of cotton or other goods in America, India, or England are hereby
warned against fastening the bent onds of bands brought side by side
on bales, with bar, lath, or spear; that all receivers of bales at the
port of Liverpool or other English ports, and all unlicensed users
whatever (temporary or otherwise) of such apparatus or furniture
iviz., bands assembled on bales with bent ends, side by side, fastened
with bar, lath, or spear), are liable for past, present, and future piracy.
The part of Riddle's invention referred to is clearly described, with
drawings, in Riddle's Specifications of his several Letters Patent.
Packers, shippers receivers, merchants, mill-owners, and dock com¬
panies are warned accordingly.
W. RIDDLE, C.E., Patentee, 5. Lansdown-terrace, Stockwell,
S.W., London.

articles with the name of "Elkington," or with a Crown.
