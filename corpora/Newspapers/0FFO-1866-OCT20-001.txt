TO the ROYAL MAIL STEAM PACKET COM¬
PANY, 55, Moorgate-street.—Gentlemen,—We beg to express
our regret that in the month of April last we caused to be shipped on
board your steamship Seine a package of drugs, addressed to Dr.
Sealey, Barbadoes, which contained within it a box with two bottles of
muriatic and nitric acid, and that we did not give, at the time of
despatching the package, notice that its contents were of a dangerous
character, as required by the provisions of the Merchant Shipping Act,
and we ibeg to APOLOGIZE for the neglect. We are now aware that
such a package is dangerous if shipped with other cargo.
You having intimated to us that we are liable for penalties for ship¬
ping acids without giving notice of the fact that the package contained
them, we have to thank you for waiving proceedings against us upon
our making this apology, which you may use in such manner as you
may think right. We are, Gentlemen, your obedient servants.
Oct. 11, 1866.	GODFREY and COOKE, 30, Conduit-street.
