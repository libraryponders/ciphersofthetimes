IN the HIGH COURT of JUDICATURE at
BOMBAY, Ordinary Original Civil Jurisdiction.—In the Matter
of the Indian Companies Act, 1866, and of the EIGHTH BOMBAY
LAUDABLE SOCIETY : Alexander Brown, Robert Roberts,
Venayckras Jagonnathjee Sunkersett, Soonderas Raghonatti, and
Framjee Nesserwanjee, the Directors of the Society, Petitioners.—The
Official Liquidators having proposed that the distribution of the
funds of the Society should be made upon the following principle:—
namely, that a deduction of 12 per cent. being made as contemplated
by the 29th Regulation of the Society from the total amount of sub¬
scriptions received during the term of the Society, the amount of such
deduction should be retained by the Liquidators out of the funds in
their hands, and the residue of the last-mentioned funds should
be divided rateably among the holders of shares held on lives subscribed
upon in said Society which have lapsed between its commence¬
ment and its close on 31st of December, 1868, so that, on each
such share as last aforesaid, no more than 10,000 rupees in all shall
be paid to the holder thereof, and if anything shall remain
out of said last-mentioned residue after paying said shareholders
such residue, as well as the amount of said deduction so firstly retained
by the Liquidators as aforesaid, after paying thereout the expenses of
liquidation, should be applied to, or rateably towards, the repayment
of all subscriptions paid on shares held on lives not lapsed up to the
day last aforesaid to the holders of such shares respectively; and after
reading the 24th and 29th Regulations of the said Society, and the
affidavit of William Gilmour Hall, filed the 5th day of April instant, and
after hearing counsel on the part of said Liquidators;—It is ordered
that the scheme so proposed by the said Liquidators be approved, and
the Liquidators be authorized to DIST RIBUTE the said FUNDS in
their hands accordingly, unless cause be shown to the contrary, before
the sitting Judge in Chambers, on Thursday, the 8th day of July, 1869,
and that a copy of this order be inserted in the Bomby Government
Gazette of Thursday, the 15th of April, 1869, and twice within
two weeks from the date hereof in the Times of India, the Bombay
Gazette, the Bombay Summachar, and the Jami-Jamsheed newspapers,
and also twice in the London Times, Daily News, and Standard news¬
papers. — Dated this 5th day of April, 1869.
JOSEPH ARNOULD.
RIMINGTON, HORE, and LANGLEY, Attorneys for the
Official Liquidators
