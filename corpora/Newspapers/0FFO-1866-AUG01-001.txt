SALMON FISHERY ACT, 1865.—Notice is hereby
given, that it is the intention of the Right Hon. Spencer Horatio
Walpole, one of Her Majesty's Principal Secretaries of State, to GRANT
CERTIFICATES, under his hand, forming into fishery districts the
RIVERS CAMEL and FOWEY, as hereinafter defined; and that the
limits of the said rivers for the purposes of the said Act shall be defined
—
Camel.—The River Camel, and its tributaries, situate within
the county of Cornwall, and also the estuary of the said river, and so
much of the coast as lies between the Southern Boundary of the Fishery
District of the Rivers Taw and Torridge at Welcomb Mouth and Peal
Point, Land's-end, both in the county of Cornwall, and all rivers flow¬
ing into the sea between the said points, and being within the said
county.
Fowey. The River Fowey, and its tributaries, situate within the county
of Cornwall, and also the estuary of the said river, and so much of the
coast as lies between the Southern Boundary of the Fishery District of
the River Camel at Peal Point, Land's-end, and the Western Boundary
of the Fishery district of the River Tamar, at Rame Head, both in the
county of Cornwall, and all rivers flowing into the sea between the said
points, and being within the said county.
