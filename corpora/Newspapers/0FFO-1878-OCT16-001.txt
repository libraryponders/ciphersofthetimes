TENDERS for CONDEMNED MEAT, &c.—The
Commissioners of Sewers of the City of London will meet in the
Guildhall of the said City on Tuesday, the 22d of October, 1878, at
half-past 12 o'clock precisely, to receive TENDERS from persons
desirous of UTILIZING, for manurial or manufacturing purposes,
the MEAT, Poultry, Offal, &c., CONDEMNED in the City of London
as unfit for human food, for one year from the 1st of November next.
The Commissioners will supply all carts, horses, and labour, and will
deliver such meat, &c., at the premises of the contractor within five
miles of Guildhall.
A draft of the contract to be entered into can be seen at this office,
where also the quantities of meat condemned during the last three
years may be ascertained.
Tenders must be sealed up and endorsed on the outside "Tender
for Condemned Meat," and be addressed to the undersigned, at his
office, and must tbe delivered before 12 o'clock on the said 22d of
October, and the parties sending in proposals should appear per¬
sonally, or by a duly authorised agent.
The narty whose tender is accepted will be required to give security
for £200 for the due execution of the contract.
The Commissioners do not pledge themselves to accept the highest
or any tender.
HY. BLAKE, Principal Clerk.
Sewers office, Guildhall, October 8th. 1878.
