THAMES CONSERVANCY.—Bye-laws—Fishery.—
Notice is hereby given, that the Conservators of the river Thames
propose to make the following BYE-LAWS for the REGULATION
of the FISHERY:—
The Conservators of the river Thames, in exercise of the power
and authority vested in them by the Thames Conservancy Acts, 1857,
1864, 1867, and 1878, and by the Thames Navigation Acts, 1866 and
1870, and of every other authority them hereunto in anywise enabling,
do order and direct as follows, that is to say:—
1. It shall not be lawful for any person to fish for or take, or attempt
to take, any fish by using a wire, or suare, or hooks (baited or un¬
baited), or any other engine for the purpose of foul hooking, commonly
called suatching or suaring.
2. The operation of the following part of No. 23 of the rules orders,
and ordinances in force from the 1st day of November, 1785, viz:—
Hookermen may hook for eels all the year" is hereby limited to
that part of the River Thames between Kew-bridge and Yantle
Creek.
3. It shall not be lawful for any person to lay night hooks or night
lines of any description whatever between the City stone at Staines
and Kew-bridge, and any person laying fixed lines or hooks by night
or day (commonly called night lines), or taking or attempting to take
eels or fith of any description by such means, shall be deemed as com¬
mitting a breach of this Bye-law.
d. Bye-law No. 1 allowed by Her Majesty in Council at a Court
held on the 30th September, 1873, relating to fence months shall, after
these Bye-laws have been allowed by Her Majesty in Council, be, and
the same is hereby repeated, and in lieu thereof the following is
substituted: That the fence season for fresh-water fish as defined in
the Freshwater Fisheries Act. 1878, be between the 15th day of March
and the 15th day of June, both days inclusive; and any person fishing
on or between these dates respectively shall be deemed guilty of
committing a breach of this Bye-law.
5. So much of the 23d of the said rules, orders, and ordinances in
force from the said 1st day of November, 1765, as relates to the times
during which saimon and trout may be taken shall after these pre¬
sent Bye-laws have been allowed by Her Majesty in Council be, and
the same is hereby repealed, and in lien thereof the following be sub¬
stituted:—
No Salmon, Salmon Trout, or Trout shall be taken between the
10th day of September in each year and the 31st day of March in the
following year, both days inclusive, and any person fishing for or taking
Salmon, Salmon Trout, or lrout on or between these dates respec¬
tively shall be deemed guilty of committing a breach of this Bye-law.
Penalty.—Every person committing any breach of, or in any way in¬
fringing any of these Bye-laws shall be liatie to a penalty of, and shall
forfeit a sum not exceeding £5, which said pen ity shall be recovered,
enforced, and applied according to the provisions of the Thames Acts,
1857 to 1878.
Any objections to or representations respecting the proposed Bye¬
laws shouid be sent to or left with the Secretary of the Conservaters
of the River Thames, at No. 41, Trinity-square, Tower-hill, London,
on or before Friday, the 6th day of June next.
E. BURSTAL, Secretary
Thames Conservancy, 91, Trinity-square, Tower-hill, April 28, 1879.
