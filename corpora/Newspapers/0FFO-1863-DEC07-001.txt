HENRY CHARLES BYRDE, heretofore called
and known by the name of Henry Charles Bird, of Goytrey-house,
in the county of Monmouth, Esq., do hereby give notice, that on the
28th day of this present month of November for and on behalf of
myself and Rebecca my wife, and of my present and future children
lawfully begotten, I did wholly DISCONTINUE to write and use the
SURNAME of BIRD, and assumed, took, and used, and am deter¬
mined at all times hereafter in all writings, documents, actions, suits,
proceedings, dealings, matters, and things whatsoever, and upon all
occasions whatsoever, to sign, describe, and call myself so as to be
distinguished, known, and recognized by the name of Henry Charles
Byrde only; and I further refer all whom it may concern to a deed
poll under my hand and seal, bearing date herewith, and duly enrolled
in the High Court of Chancery, declaring that I have chosen to
discontinue for myself, my wife, and my present and future children
for ever to write and use the surname of Bird, and in lieu thereof to
write, assume, and use the surname of Byrde (as heretofore written
and used by my ancestors). —Dated this 28th day of November, 1863.
HENRY CHARLES BYRDE (late Henry Charles Bird).
