LICENSED VICTUALLERS' ASYLUM.—
Caution.—The Board of Management having ascertained that
certain parties wholly unauthorized by them have been in the habit of
OBTAINING SUBSCRIPTIONS for the Institution, especially uponth
occasion of changes and other settlements, respectfully beg to CAUTION
their friends and the public against such parties, and to request that no
subscription may be paid to anyone except the Chairman, Board of
Management, Auxiliary Committee, Stewards at the Anniversary
Festival, or the Secretary, or in exchange for a printed official receipt.
The Board also particularly request that any friend of the Institu¬
tion who may have paid a subscription, and who has not since received
an official receipt, or a formal acknowledgment from the Secretary, will
favour them by forwarding the name and address of the party to whom
the payment was made, and the date and particulars thereof.
The Board have taken legal advice upon this subject, and are deter¬
mined to avail themselves of all the protection which the law will
allow.
GEORGE IMRIE, Secretary.
No. 67, Fleet-street, July, 1866.
