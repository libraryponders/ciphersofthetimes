IN the HIGH COURT of ADMIRALTY of ENG¬
LAND.—No. 3.181. The OBEY and ARTHUR.—Whereas in a
cause of limitation of liability instituted in the High Court of Ad¬
miralty of England on behalf of George Deslandes, of No. 23, Com¬
mercial-buildings, in the Island of Jersey, shipowner, and others, the
owners of the bark Obey, against the owners of the ship or vessel
Arthur, and the master and crew thereof, the plaintiffs in a cause of
damage instituted in this Court, entitled the Obey (No. 3,158), and
against all and every other person or persons whomsoever having any
right, title, claim, or interest whatever with reference to or arising out
of a collision which occurred on the 22d day of February, 1866, be¬
tween the said vessels Obey and Arthur, the Judge of the said Court
did, on the 31st day of July, 1866, among other things, pronounce that
the owners of the said vessel Obey are entitled to limited liability ac¬
cording to the provisions of the Merchant Shipping Act, 1854, and the
Merchant Shipping Act Amendment Act, 1862, and that in respect of
loss or damage to ship's goods, merchandize, or other things caused by
reason of the improper navigation of the said vessel Obey on the oc¬
casion of the collision between the said vessel and the vessel Arthur
on the 22d day of February, 1866, the owners of the said vessel Obey
are answerable in damages to the amount of £2, 168 and no more, being
at the rate of £8 for each ton of the registered tonnage of the said
vessel Obey; This is therefore to give notice to all persons having any
CLAIM in respect of the LOSS or DAMAGE caused as aforesaid,
that if they do not come in and enter their claims in the said cause on
or before the 31st day of October next ensuing, they will be excluded
from sharing in the aforesaid amount. —Dated this 11th day of August,
1866.
(L.S.) H. A. BATHURST, Assistant Registrar.
