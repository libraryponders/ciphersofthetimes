CHARITY COMMISSION.—In the Matter of the
Charity called the WATERMEN's SCHOOL, at Putney, in the
county of Surrey, founded by the will of Thomas Martyn.—By direc¬
tion of the Boasd of Charity Commissioners for England and Wales
notice is hereby given, that an ORDER is proposed to be made by the
said Board after the expiration of one calendar month, to be computed
from the publication of this notice, having the following objects,

The establishment of a scheme for the future management and regu¬
lation of the abovementioned Charity.
The scheme is deposited at the school-house belonging to the said
Charity, and may be inspected on each week day during the space of 15
days, to be computed from the first publication of this notice, between
the hours of 11 and 1.
Any objection to or suggestions on the proposed order may be made
or transmitted in writing to the said Board, at their office, 8, York-¬
street, St. James's-square. London, within 21 days next after the pub¬
lication of this notice.—Dated this 19th day of December, 1864.
HENRY M. VANE, Secretary.
