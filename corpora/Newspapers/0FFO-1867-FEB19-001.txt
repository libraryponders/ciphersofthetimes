BANK of ENGLAND.—Unclaimed Dividends.—
Application having been made to the Governors of the Bank of
England to direct the payment of one dividend on the sum of £4,000
Consolidated £3 per Cent. Annuities, also one dividend on £700 New
63 per Cent. Annuities, heretofore standing in the names of THOMAS
CROMPTON, of Margate, gentleman, and GEORGE CROMPTON, of
Sellwood-place, Queen's-sim, Old Brompton, gentleman, and which
dividends were paid over to the Commissioners for the Reduction of
the National Debt, in consequence of the same having remained un¬
claimed since the 5th July, 1855, and 10th October, 1855, respectively
—Notice is hereby given, that at the expiration of three months from
this date the said dividends will be paid to Thomas Brown Lee, one of
the executors of George Crompton, who was survivor, who has claimed
the same, unless some other claimant shall sooner appear and make out
his claim thereto.
