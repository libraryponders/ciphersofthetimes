IN the HIGH COURT of JUSTICE, Admiralty
Division, Liverpool District Registry.—The s.s. Ponce.— Whereas
in an action for damage by collision, lately pending in the Admiralty
Division of our High Court of Justice, in the Liverpool District
Registry, which was instituted on behalf of the owners of the ship
BARING BROTHERS and her cargo against the owners of the
steamship or vessel PONCE and her freight, the Judze of the said
Court did, on the 4th day of April, 1878, amonest other things, pro¬
nounce that the collision in question in the said action was caused
solely by the fault, or default, of those on board the defendants' said
steamship or vessel Ponce. And whereas the defendants in the said
action claimed to have their liability in respect of all damage or loss
arising out of the said collision limited, under section 54 of the
Merchant Shipping Act, 1862; and the Judge of the said Court did
also, by the decree of the 4th day of April aforesaid, pronounce that
the defendants in the said action were entitled, in respect of all
DAMAGE or LOSS to any person or persons whomsoever arising out
of the said COLLISION, to the limitation of their liability as afore¬
said, and that the amount of such limitation is the sum of
£6,096 4s. 10d., being at the rate of £8 per ton on the gross registered
tonnage of the said steamship or vessel Ponce, after deducting the
space occupied by the crew, with interest thereon at the rate of £4
per cent per annum from the 22d day of January, 1278, until pay¬
ment, and that upon payment into Court of such sum, with interest
as aforesaid, and on payment of plaintis costs of the said action, to
be taxed, all further proceedings in the said action are to be stayed.
This is therefore to give notice to all persons having any claim in re¬
spect of the loss or damage caused as aforesaid, that if they do not
come in and prove their claims in the said action on or before the
12th day of August next, they will be peremptorily excluded from
sharing in the aforesaid amount and from the benents of the said
order. —Dated this 12th day of July, 1878.
FRANCIS D. LOWNDES, District Registrar, 3, Brunswick¬
street, Liverpool.
