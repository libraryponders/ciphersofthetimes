CAUTION.—The PATENT GIESSEN BLUE.—In
Chancery.—"LEIFCHILD v. BROAD."—In this cause the
Court of Chancery has granted an injunction to restrain the de¬
fendant from selling any blue colouring matter, not manufactured by
the plaintiff, in bottles having labels either similar to or only colourably
differing from the labels used by the plaintiff. As legal proceedings
will be taken against all other parties who may infringe the plaintiff's
patent rights or pirate his trade mark, the trade are hereby cautioned
against dealing in any Giessen blue, except that manufactured by the
plaintiff, at the depot, 43, London-wall, London.

GREAT INTERNATIONAL DOG SHOW during the Derby week,
under the highest patronage. Mr. F. Brailsford, the secretary of the
National Dog Show at Birmingham, has been engaged to superintend

GRAND NATIONAL DOG SHOW, at Ashburn¬
ham-hall, King's-road, Chelsea, adjoining Cremorne.—Mr. E. T.
SMITH begs to inform the numerous ladies and gentlemen that the
reason the Exhibition is fixed for the 23d day of March is in conse¬
quence of so many noblemen and gentlemen forwarding their dogs to
Paris to the Great Exhibition of Dogs which is to be held there in
May next, and which will give so poor an attendance of dogs in London
in May. By exhibiting the dogs at Ashburnham-hall in March there
will be ample time to improve their condition and compete for the
Paris prizes, which will be patronised by the French Court.
