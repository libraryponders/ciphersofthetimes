—Caution. Whereas on the 25th January, 3d May, 17th May,
and 29th May, 1877, in four actions, in which Jas. Hennessy and Co.
were plaintiffs, his Lordship Vice Chancellor Sir Richard Malins
granted an injunction restraining the Defendants in each of the said
four actions from selling or dealing with any bottles of brandy, not
being brandy bottled by the plaintiffs at Cognac, as the plaintiffs case
brandy, or so as to induce purchasers to believe that such bottles of
brandy are the plaintiffs' case brandy; and from selling or dealing
with any bottles of brandy not being the plaintiffs' case brandy, in
cases or labels, branded or labelled so as to denote the plaintiffs
case brandy; and from selling or dealing with as the plaintiffs'
brandy any brandy not being brandy of the plaintiffs. Notice
hereby given, that all persons INFRINGING the TRADE MARKS
of Messrs. Jas. Hennessy and Co. will be forthwith proceeded against,
and printers are cautioned against printing labels or imitation of the
plaintiffs' labels. Information of any infringement will be thankfully
received by their agents, Messrs. Twiss and Browning, 47, Mark-lane,
London and by the undersigned. —Dated this 1st day of June 1877.
MERCER and MERCER, 1, Copthall-court, Throgmorton-street,
London, Solicitors for Messrs. Jas Hennessy and Co.
