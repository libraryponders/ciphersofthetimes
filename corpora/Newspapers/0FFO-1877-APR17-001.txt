FALMOUTH PORT OYSTER and MUSSEL
FISHERY.—Sea Fisheries Act, 1868.—Notice is hereby given,
that the Board of Trade, under the Provisions of Part Three of the
Sea Fisheries Act. 1868 (31 and 32 Vict., c. 45), have, upon the applica¬
tion of the Town Council of the Borough of Falmouth, in the County
of Cornwall, settled and made an ORPER for the following purposes,
viz, for the MAINTENANCE and REGULATION of an OYSTER
and MUSSEL FISHERY in the parts of the Bed of Falmouth Har¬
bour which are set out and described in the Order hereinafter men¬
tioned or referred to.
For limiting the periods during which dredging or fishing for oysters
and mussels shall take place.
For closing any portion or portions of the fishery for such periods as
may be deemed necessary, and subject to the conditions mentioned
in the said Order.
For making bye-laws with the approval of the Board of Trade, for
the reguiation or such fishery.
For granting liceuses, and levyins tolls in respect of such boats and
dredges as shall be used in such fishery.
For appointing a water bailiff or bailiffs to enforce the provisions of
such Order, and the bye-laws and regulations which may be made
thereunder, and for such other purposes as are in the said Order men¬
tioned or referred to.
The said Order, together with the plan of the limits of the fishery,
may be seen and inspected at the offices of the undersigned, between
the hours of 10 o'clock a.m. and 4 o'clock p.m., and copies of such
Order may also be obtained at such offices on payment of a ree of one
shilling.
GENN and NALDER, Solicitors, Church-Street, Falmouth.
