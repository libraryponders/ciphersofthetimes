Gardens, North Woolwich, Friday, June 21, 1861.—These popular
Gardens, 17 acres in extent, beautifully laid out, will be opened on
the above night for a BAL MASQUE on a scale of splendour never
before attempted in this country. The Gardens will be one blaze of
light, under the superintendence of Mr. R. Daffall, illuminator to the
late Vauxhall. Two splendid bands; musical director, Mr. Theodore
Herman. Numerous masters of the ceremonies are appointed, under
the director, Mr. Henry A. Taylor. Mr. J. Nathan, of Castle-street,
Leicester-square, has been appointed costumier. Costumes may be
hired daily there, or at the Gardens, where costumes are on view. Good
dressing rooms are provided, with careful dressers. No one admitted
in clowns', harlequins', or any other objectionable dress. Admission
28. N.B. The Gardens are only 25 minutes ride from London. Spe¬
