HALE, Mayor.
COMMON COUNCIL, holden in the Chamber of
the Guildhall of the city of London, on Thursday, the 17th day

Resolved unanimously—That this Court desires to record its high
appreciation of the valuable services rendered by the Right Hon.
William Lawrence, Lord Mayor of this city, during the past
year, and tenders to him its sincere thanks for the admirable manner
in which he has discharged the arduous and important duties of the
dignified office of Chief Magistrate; for his calm and impartial admi¬
nistration of justice, tempering with merciful consideration the due
enforcement of the law; for his constant readiness in coming and pre¬
siding over an unusually frequent number of meetings of this Court,
thereby enabling the expeditious transaction of public business; for
his anxiety to protect the rights and privileges of the citizens; for his
liberal dispensation of civic hospitality; for his generous support to the
charitable institutions of the metropolis; and for his frank and
courteous demeanour upon all occasions.

ORDER by the LORDS of COUNCIL and SES¬
SION, in pursuance of the Bankruptcy Statute.—Edinburgh,
12th November, 1864.—The Lords of Council and Session having re¬
ceived the SEVENTH ANNUAL REPORT of the ACCOUNTANT
in BANKRUPTCY in SCOTLAND, which, as required by the statute,
shows, as at the close of October, 1863, the state of each Sequestration
in Scotland returned to the Accountant in pursuance of the statute,
do hereby direct that the said Report shall be published by being
made patent to all concerned, at the office of the Accountant, New
Register-house, Edinburgh, for one year from this date, and shall, after
expiry of that period, be transmitted to the Deputy-Keeper of the Re¬
cords; and the Lords direct this order to be published by the Accoun¬
tant in the Edinburgh and London Gazettes, and in one of the adver¬
tising newspapers of Edinburgh, London, and Dublin; and the Lords
direct this order to be engrossed in the Books of Sederunt. —Signed 15th
November, 1864.
