PUBLIC APOLOGY.—Birmingham, Sept. 6th,
1866.—Messrs. R. W. Winfield and Co.—Gentlemen.—As the
author of a book entitled the "Londoniad," containing a statement
in reference to your firm, which has no foundation in fact, and is
libellous in its character, I unhesitatingly acknowledge the injustice
I have done you by its insertion, and tender you my most sincere
APOLOGY. As the book above referred to has been circulated in
Birmingham and elsewhere, I consent to your publishing this apology
in the public newspapers, and in such other manner as you may think
proper; and I undertake forthwith to deliver up to you all the copies
of the book now in my possession or power, and not to circulate any
book, pamphlet, or paper containing the libel complained of. I ac¬
knowledge your kindness in staying legal proceedings on my making
this public retractation and apology and paying the cost of the action
ou have commenced against me.—I am, gentlemen, vours faithfully,
JAMES TORRINGTON SPENCER LIDSTONE.—Witness, William
Docker, solicitor Birmingham
