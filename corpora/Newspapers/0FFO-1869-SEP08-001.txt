WHEREAS the Commissioners in Lunacy have
intimated their intention to institute proceedings against
me, the undersigned Nathaniel Batt Grigg, formerly of Donhead
St. Andrew's, in the county of Wilts, and now of Holcombe
Rogus, near Wellington, in the county of Somerset, a Member of the
Royal College of Surgeons of England, for having received into my
house and taken the care and charge of a lunatic, without having first
obtained, in compliance with the provisions of the Act of the 8th and
9th Vic., cap. 100, being "An Act for the Regulation of the Care and
Treatment of Lunatics," an order of reception and medical certificates;
and whereas, in consideration of the acknowledgment and apology herein¬
after set forth the said Commissioners have, under the special circum¬

nted to stay the prosecution:—Now I, the
said NATHANIEL BATT GRIGG, do hereby acknowledge that I have
violated the law by receiving into my house and taking the care and
charge of a lunatic without having first obtained the necessary order
and medical certificates, and I do hereby express my deep concern, and
regret for having so done, and do thus publicly APOLOGIZE for the
same; and I do solemnly promise not again to offend in like manner.
—Dated this 4th day of September, 1869.
NATHL. B. GRIGG.
VANDERCOM, LAW, and Co., Bush-lane, Solicitors to the
Commissioners in Lunacy.
