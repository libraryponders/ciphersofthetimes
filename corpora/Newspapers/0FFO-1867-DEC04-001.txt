Notice is hereby given, that DANIEL MASKELL, late of Tatters¬
hall, in the county of Lincoln, gentleman (who died on the 12th day of
August, 1867), by his will, proved in the Lincoln District Registry of
Her Majesty's Court of Probate on the 22d day of October now last
past, by John Hodgson, of Sausethorpe, and John Brackenbury, of
Wood Enderby, both in the said county of Lincoln, farmers, gave and
bequeathed the following LEGACIES, that is to say, "the sum of £40
equally between and amongst all the children of his late sister Ann, the
wife of Jarvis Spencer, of Baumber, in the said county of Lincoln,
blacksmith; the sum of £90 equally between and amongst all the chil¬
dren of his late brother Thomas Maskell, late of Langrick Ferry, in
the same county, labourer, deceased; the sum of £50 equally between
and amongst all the children of his wife's brother John Jackson, late of
New York, near Coningsby, in the said county of Lincoln, farmer; the
sum of £30 equally between and amongst all the children of his wife's
sister Rebecca, the wifs of Henry Vinter, of Tumby Allotment, in
Wildmore Fen, in the said county of Lincoln, farmer; the sum of £40
equally between and amongst all the children of his wife's brother Pask
Jackson, late of Wood Enderby, in the said county of Lincoln, but now
of No. 13, Willow-place, Saint Peter's-road, Mile-end, Stepney, in the
county of Middlesex, gentleman; and the sum of £40 equally between
and amongst all the children of his wife's sister Elizabeth, the wife of
William Padley, formerly residing in London, butcher, deceased." All
persons claiming to be entitled under the bequests before mentioned,
and all creditors and other persons having any claims or de¬
mands upon or against the estate of the said Daniel Maskell, are
requested to send particulars thereof to me, the undersigned, on or be¬
fore the 6th day of April next; after which day the said trustees and
executors will proceed to distribute the assets of the said deceased
among the parties entitled thereto, having regard only to the claims of
which they shall then have had notice ; and the said trustees and
executors will not be liable for the assets so distributed, or any part
thereof, to any person of whose claim they shall not then have had
notice. — Dated this 21st day of November, 1867.
LANGLEY JOS. BRACKENBURY, Solicitor,
