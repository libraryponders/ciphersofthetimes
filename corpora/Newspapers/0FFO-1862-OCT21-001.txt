BANK of ENGLAND.—Unclaimed Stock and An¬
nuity.—Application having been made to the Governors of the
Bank of England to direct the re-transfer from the Commissioners for
the Reduction of the National Debt of the sum of £1,000 Consolidated
Three per Cent. Annuities, and £61 1s. 5d. Consolidated Long Annuity
(expired the 5th of January, 1860, heretofore standing in the names
of the Rev. EDWARD ALLEN, of Wilmington, Kent, BENJAMIN
SEWELL, Jun., of Chatham-place, London, merchant, and JAMES
AGNEW, of Harrow, Middlesex, Esq., and which funds were trans¬
ferred to the said Commissioners, in consequence of the dividends
thereon having remained unclaimed since the year, 1847;—Notice is
hereby given that, on the expiration of three months from this date,
the said Consolidated Three per Cent. Stock will be Transferred, and
the Dividends thereon, and also the Dividends in arrear on the said
expired Annuity, will be paid, to the said Edward Allen and James
Agnew, the survivors, who have claimed the same, unless some other
claimant shall sooner appear and make out his claim thereto.

PARALYSIS and EPILEPSY.—The Committee
of the NATIONAL HOSPITAL, Queen-square, Bloomsbury,
announce with deep regret that the funds at their disposal are totally
inadequate to meet the numerous cases presented for relief. More
beds are required. It is needless to dwell on the destitution of the
sufferers, many of whom for want of that help which it is not in the
power of the Committee to afford, become incurable, and in numerous
instances their affliction terminates in insanity. Donations, subscrip¬
tions, and legacies are most earnestly solicited.
"The Viscount Raynham, M.P., Treasurer.
