FIRST NOTICE to the NEPHEWS and NIECES
of MATTHEW CHERRY and BRIDGET CHERRY
merly Bridget Robins, deceased.—Whereas MATTHEW CHERRY,
late of Weston near Weedon, in the county of Northampton, and
formerly of No. 45, Great Peter-street, Westminster, brewer (who
died on the 19th day of November, 1861), by his will, dated the 2d day
of July, 1855, and proved in the Northampton District Court of Pro¬
bate on the 11th day of December, 1861, by Christopher Garrett, of
Tadmarton, in the county of Oxford, farmer (since deceased), Charles
Cherry, of Marston Saint Lawrence, in the county of Northampton,
farmer, and Mary Lovell, of Weston, near Weedon aforesaid, widow
(since deceased), the executors named in the said will, after bequeath¬
ing certain pecuniary legacies, did give and bequeath the residue of
his estate and effects unto the said Christopher Garrett, Charles
Cherry, and Mary Lovell, upon trust, to invest the same as therein
mentioned, and to pay the annual income thereof to his sister, the said
Mary Lovell, for her life, and after, her death to hold the trust fund
and the annual income thereof, upon trust for all and every the
nephews and nieces of the testator's laite father, Matthew Cherry, and
late mother, Bridget Cherry (whose maiden name was Bridget Robins)
who should come in and establish such relationship to the satisfaction
of his said trustees or trustee, within 12 calendar months after the de¬
cease of his said sister, Mary Lovell, and who should at that time be
resident in England, share and share alike; and the said testator there¬
by directed his said trustees or trustee to insert, or cause to be inserted,
six times in The Times newspaper, and six times in a Banbury news¬
paper, an advertisement calling upon such nephews and nieces of his
late father and mother to send in their claims to such relationship;
and, whereas, the said Mary Lovell died on the 11th day of September
instant.—Now, I, the undersigned, being the solicitor acting for and on
behalf of the said Charles Cherry, the surviving executor of the said
testator, do hereby require all persons claiming to be NEPHEWS and
NIECES of the said late father and mother of the said testator, on or
before the 11th day of September next, to send in to me, at my office,
in Banbury, in the county of Oxford, the particulars and evidence of
their claims to such relationship, and to establish such claims to the
satisfaction of the said Charles Cherry; and I further give notice, that
all claims which are not established to the satisfactiont of the said
Charles Cherry on or before the said 11th day of September next will
be absolutely forfeited. —Dated this 25th day of September, 1866.
JOHN FORTESC.
jolicitor to the said Charles Cherry.
