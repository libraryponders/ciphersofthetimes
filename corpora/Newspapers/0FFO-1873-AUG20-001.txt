THE Committee appointed at the City Board of
St. Petersburg for the building over the Neva of the permanent
Liteinaia Bridge, makes it generally known that of the 17 projects
offered to COMPETITION, anvertised in 1871, for the BUILDING of
a PERMANENT LITEINAIA BRIDGE over the river Neva, the
following three projects have been accepted as being worthy of a pre¬
mium:—The first, of 6,000 roubles, under the motto, Westminster, T.
Whitaker and Terret, Civil Engineers; the second, of 3,000 roubles,
project of the Engineer K. M. Ordish; and the third, of 1,500 roubles,
project under Lit. B. S. (Civil Engineer, Bronislaw Souhovetsky), pro¬
perty of M. Pouschlure and Co.
In regard to the 14 remaining projects, the parties to whom they
belong can receive them back from the said Committee either per¬
sonally or by post by sending their address.
Parties who come to receive the premium, as well as those who come
to receive back their project which has not been considered worthy of
a premium, are obliged to procure a certificate of identity, and a proof
that they are really the proprietors of the said projects.
