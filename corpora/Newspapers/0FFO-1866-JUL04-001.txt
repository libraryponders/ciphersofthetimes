TO Captain WEATHERBURN, ship Perey, 27th
June, 1866.—Dear Sir. After bringing home safely, and in ex¬
cellent health, from the other side of the world some 450 souls, I can¬
not disembark from the ship. Percy without expressing my grateful
THANKS to you, her master, for your kind attention and the anxiety
you have shown to meet my wishes and to add to the comfort of all
on board from the day the left wing of the 68th Light. Infantry
embarked at Auckland (12th March) to the present time. I and the
officers of the 68th Light Infantry beg you will accept of a piece of
plate which we desire to present to you as a testimonial of our regard.
Be so good as to convey to your officers my thanks for the exemplary
manner in which they have performed their duties, and for their at¬
tention to all on board. Your sailors also merit a good word, for I
have never seen quieter or better behaved seamen in my life. Accept
of my best wishes for your future welfare ; and believe me to remain,
yours faithfully,
(Signed) H. HARBOARD MORANT. Lieut.-Col.
68th Light Infantry.
