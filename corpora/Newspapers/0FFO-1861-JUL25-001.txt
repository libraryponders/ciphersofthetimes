LONDON RIFLE BRIGADE, Head-quarters,
July 24, 1861.—ENCAMPMENT at ALDERSHOTT.—Her
Majesty's Secretary of State for War having positively refused the
issue of tents, camp equipments, &c., for the use of the London Rifle
Brigade, the Lieutenant-Colonel Commanding regrets that he is
obliged to CANCEL all ORDERS published in reference to the pro¬
posed encampment of the Regiment at Aldershott on the 1st of August.
By order, A. T. EWENS, Captain and Adjutant.
LONDON SCOTTISH RIFLE VOLUNTEERS.
—Prize Shooting.—The TARGETS will be AVAILABLE THIS
DAY (Thursday), 25th, from 10 a.m., and on Friday and Saturday,
from 12 noon until dusk. Competition for all the prizes will continue
open until Saturday evening. Ammunition may be had on the ground.
Uniform must be worn. There will be a tent for refreshments. The
names of those in each company who are to fire for Lord Elcho's
Challenge Cup may be seen at the office on Thursday morning. In
addition to the prizes already announced, the Highland Society have
offered a prize of £25 (a cup or a watch).
The Company prize will not be fired this week.
The arrangements in connexion with them may be learned from
the respective Captains.
By order. S. FLOOD PAGE, Captain and Adjutant.
Head-quarters, July 25, 1861.
