CHARITY COMMISSION.—In the Matter of
JOHN HOBBY's CHARITY, for Imprisoned Debtors.—By
direction of the Board of Charity Commissioners for England and
Wales, notice is hereby given, that an ORDER is proposed to be made
by them after the expiration of one calendar month, to be computed
from the publication of this notice, having the following object,
namely, the ESTABLISHMENT of a SCHEME for the MANAGE¬
MENT of this CHARITY. The principal objects proposed to be
effected by the scheme are:—
291. To enable the Clothworkers' Company, who are the trustees of the
Charity, to expend out of the nccumuiations of surplus income in hand
a sum not exceeding £500, in improving the buildings of the Grammar
School at Sutton Valence, under their management.
2. To enable the Company to make yearly contributions to the funds
for the education of the poor in the parishes of Plumstead, Woolwich,
and Watford, where the property of the Charity is situate, and in

3. To provide that the remainder of the income of the Charity shall
be applied at the diseretion of the Clothworkers' Company, in aid of
sick or convalescent hospitals or dispensaries, or reformatory or in¬
dustrial schools, or other institutions for the cure or relief of the
physical maladies, or the moral or social evils affecting the poorer
classes of London and its neighbourhood.
A complete draft of the proposed scheme may be inspected each
week day during the next 15 days from the publication of this notice,
at the Clothworkers'-hall, Mincing-lane, between the hours of 10 and 4.
Any objections to the proposed Order, or suggestions for the modi¬
fication thereof, may be transmitted to the said Board, in writing,
addressed to their Secretary, No. 3, York-street, St. James's-square,
London, within 21 days next after the first publication of this notice.
—Dated this 1st day of August, 1868

PETROLEUM ACTS, 1862 and 1868.—The Metro¬
politan Board of Works hereby give notice, that an Act has just
passed to Amend the Act 25th and 26th Victoria, chapter 66 (1862), for
the SAFE-KEEPING of PETROLEUM. Among other things it is
provided—from and after the 1st day of February, 1863, the 3d section
of the Petroleum Act, 1862, shall be repealed, and that from that date
there may be annexed to any license to be granted such conditions as
to the mode of storage, as to the nature of the goods with which pe¬
troleum may be stored, as to the testing such petroleum from time to
time, and generally as to the safe keeping of petroleum, as may seem
expedient to the local authority Any petreleum kept in contraven¬
tion of this section shall be forfeited, and, in addition thereto, the
occupier of the place in which such petroleum is kept shall be liable
to a penalty not exceeding £20 a day or each day during which pe¬
troleum is kept in contravention of this Petroleum Acts 1862 and 1868,
or either of such Acts.
In the same Act there is a prohibition of sale of petroleum for pur¬
pose of illumination giving off an inflammable vapour at a temperature
of less than 100 degrees of Fahrenheit; and the temperature at
which petroleum gives off an infiammable vapour shall for the purposes
of the Petroleum Acts be tested in manner set forth in the schedule
to the Act.
And the Board hereby gives further notice, that all licenses for the
ensuing year must be applied for, as regards the Metropolitan Board
of Works, before the 31st day of December, 1868.
Any further information may be obtained at this office between the
hours of 9 and 4 o'clock.
