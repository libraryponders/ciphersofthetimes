IN the HIGH COURT of JUSTICE, Admiralty
Division.—1879, O. No 192.—Ships "Camilla" and "Amethyst."—
Whereas in an action of li itation of liability instituted in the said
High Court of Justice on behalf of the owners of the steam ship
Camilla, against William Tulley and others, the Judge of the Admi¬
raity Division of the said High Court did on the 8th day of July, 1879,
amongst other things, pronounce that in respect of loss or damage to
ships' goods, merchandize, or other things, caused by reason of the
improper navigation of the steam vessel CAMILLA on the occasion
of the COLLISION between the said steam vessel and the steam
vessel AMETHYST on the 9th day of December last, the owners of
the said steam vessel Camilla are answerable in damages to an amount
not exceeding £7,301 16s. (such sum being at the rate of £8 for each
ton of the registered tonnage of the said steam vessel Camilia, with¬
out deduction on account of engine room). This is, therefore to give
notice to all persons having any CLAIMS in respect of the LOSS or
DAMAGE caused as aforesaid that if they do not come in and enter
their claims in the said action on or before the 8th day of August,
1879, they will be excluded from sharing in the aforesaid amount.—
Dated this 16th day of July, 1879.
