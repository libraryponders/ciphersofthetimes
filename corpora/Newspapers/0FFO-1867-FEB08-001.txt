JER Majesty's Emigration Commissioners will, on
Thursday, the 14th instant, at 1 o'clock, receive TENDERS for a
Sis to convey passengers to MELBOURNE, Victoria, to be ready
on such day between the 1st and 11th of April next, both days inclusive
as the Commissioners may name.
The shipis to be ventilated on the plan of Dr. Edmunds, who will find
the necessary labour and materiais at a charge to be paid by the ship¬
owner of 1s. 6d. per ton register. This will free the vessel from any sub¬
sequent charge for the use of the apparatus.
The ship must, subject to the usual conditions, be furnished with Dr.
Normandy's, Messrs. Winchester and Graveley's, or Messrs. Chaplin
and Co.'s fresh-water distillery apparatus, and with a competent per¬
son to manage it.
If the ship be taken up to sail from Plymouth she must be moored in
the Catwater if required by the emigration officer; and if taken up to
sail from the Mersey she will not be allowed to embark her passengers
elsewhere than in the Birdenhead Docks.
Tenqers (form A No. 2, January, 1867), specifications of fittings, and
list of mess utensils, small stores, &c., may be had at the office of the
Commissioners, or of the emigration officers at London and Liverpool.
By order of the Board. R.B. COOPER, Assistant Secretary.
Government Emigration Board, 8, Park-street, Westminster, S.W.,
6th February, 1867.
