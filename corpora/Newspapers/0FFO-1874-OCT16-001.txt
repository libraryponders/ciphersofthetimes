TWENTY POUNDS REWARD.—Trinity-house,
London, 14th October, 1874. Whereas, on Saturday, the 10th of
October instant, at about 10.40 p.m., the weather being moderate, a
Ship, apparently American built, of about 1,400 tous burden, came
into COLLISION with the VARNE LIGHT SHIP, stationed in
Dover Strait, doing very extensive damage to the Light Ship, extin¬
guishing her light, and endangering the safety of the vessel, the lives
of the crew, and the navigation of the coast by other vessels:
And whereas the name of the ship, or of the Master in command
(who promised but did not render assistance), could not be ascertained
by the crew of the Light Ship;
The above roward will be paid to any person who will give such
INFORMATION to the Corporation of Trinity-house, of London, as
to the name of the vessel and her owners, and the Master in command
at the time, as may be suflicient to enable them to institute proceed¬
ings against the Master and owners, or either of them, under the
provisions of the Merchaut Shipping Acts.
Information to be forwarded to the undersigned,
ROBIN ALLEN, Secretary to the Corporation of
Trinity-house, London.
NOTICE.
Whereas steam and other vessels have, in moderate weather (as in
the above instance), by the culpable neghgence of the persons in com¬
mand, repeatedly come into collision with light vessels belonging to
this Corporation, doing considerable damage to the light ships and en¬
dangering the navigation of the coast for the time by other vessels,
attention is hereby called to the penalty provided by the 414th section
of the Merchant Enipping Act. 1854, as follows, viz.:—
"If any person willully or negligently commiss any of the following
offences (that is to say):—
"(1.) Injures any lighthouse, or the lights exhibited therein, or any

 Removes, alters, or destroys any light ship, buoy, or beacon;
i. (3.) Riies by, makes fast to, or runs foulof, anr light ship or buoy;
He shall, in addition to the expenses of making good any damage
so occasioned, incur a penalty not exceeding fifty pounds."
ROBIN ALLEN, Secretary.
