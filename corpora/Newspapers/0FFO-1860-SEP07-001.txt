AN APOLOGY.—A question having been raised
between me and Mr. George William Miller, of 35, Willow¬
walk, leather dresser and seller, in respect of my using language of
and concerning Mr. Miller, which has given pain to him and may be
injurious to his reputation as a respectable tradesman; without making
use of the exact language and words used, I hereby most unequivecally
express my regret in speaking so hastily as I did, and fully admit I did
not, as I ought to have done, reflect on the extent and meaning
thereof, and as I have been threatened with law proceedings I have
paid all charges and make this APOLOGY, to be published as my in¬
tention to counteract the consequences which may or might follow from
what I so improperly have said of and concerning the said Mr. Miller.
As witness my hand this 5th day of September, 1860.
GEO. CHEESEMAN, Neckinger-road, Bermondsey.
