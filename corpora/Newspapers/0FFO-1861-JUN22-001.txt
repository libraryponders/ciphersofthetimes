LLOYD's REGISTER of BRITISH and FOREIGN
SHIPPING.—Special Surveys on Iron ships.—The Committee,
attaching great importance to the careful periodical examination of
iron ships, desire to direct the attention of owners to the Rule, Sec. 17,
page 39, which prescribes that "every third year iron ships shall be
specially surveyed in dry dock or laid on blocks with both surfaces of
outside plating exposed."
Ships which have undergone the above examination will be noted
in the register book thus (t. s. 61); and if not submitted to such
triennial survey will be liable to have their character suspended.
By order of the Committee, GEO. B. SEYFANG, Secretary.
No. 2, White Lion-court, Cornhill, London, E.C., June 13, 1861.
