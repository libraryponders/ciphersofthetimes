CRYSTAL PALACE.—Easter Holydays (Second
Day). The ENTERTAINMENTS will be continued THIS
DAY (Easter Tuesday) at 12, and during the week, and will comprise the
American Old Folks Concert Company, in their costumes of 100 years
ago, who will give their characteristic performances, assisted by Miss
Emma J. Nicholls, the popular American vocalist; the inimitable
Mackney, in his celebrated delineations of negro character; Stead.
"the Cure," in his wonderful jumping and other songs; the Conrad
Brothers, in their extraordinary violin performances on the tight
rope ; the Orchestral Band of the Company will perform selections
of popular music: and there will be performances on the Great Festival
Organ at intervals. Display of the Upper Series of Fountains, at 3
o'clock. New Picture Gallery, Machinery in motion, and all the usual
attractions. The park and gardens in beautiful condition.
Open at 9. Admission, 1s.; children under 12, 6d.
Trains will run from London-bridge, the New Victoria Station at
Pimlico, and intermediate stations, as often as required.
