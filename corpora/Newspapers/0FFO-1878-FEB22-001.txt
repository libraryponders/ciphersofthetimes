CHARITY COMMISSION. In the Matter of Mrs.
CAROLINE MORRIS's FIREMAN's WIDOW'S CHARITY,
and in the Matter of The Charitable Trusts Acts 1853 to 1869.—By
direction of the Board of Charity Commissioners for England and
Wales, notice is hereby given that an ORDER is proposed to be made
by them after the expiration of one calendar month to be computed
from the first publication of this notice, APPOINTING TRUSTEES
of the above-mentioned Charity, and establishing a Scheme for the
future regulation and management thereof.
The following persons have been proposed for appointment as Trus¬
tees, namely:—The Chief Officer and four Superintendents of the
London Fire Brigade, together with James Thomas McDougall, of
No. 10. Mark-lane, in the City of London, Agricultural Chemist.
It is proposed to provide by the Scheme that the residue of the
clear yearly income of the Charity, after satisfying the opjects con¬
templated by the Founder, may be applied by the Trustees, in such
manner and subject to such regulations as they may from time to
time prescribe for the benefit of deserving firemen or firemen's
widows and children belonging to the London Fire Brigade.
Any objections to the proposed Order, or suggestions for its modifi¬
cation, may be transmitted in writing to the Secretary of the said
Commissioners, Whitehall, London, within 21 days next after the
first publication of this notice.
Dated this 4th day of February, 1878.
HENRY M. VANE, Secretary.
