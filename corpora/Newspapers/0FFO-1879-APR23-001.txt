TO Right Honourable and Honourable MEMBERS
of PARLIAMENT. John Clare, Inventor, Patentee, Con¬
structor, and Designer of the Climax of Metal Shipbuilding since
1853, adopted by the Admiralty, Appeals to Members of Parlament
for support in obtaining a Select Committee on his case. Vide
Hansard:—Mr. O'Donnell, M.P.'s, immortalizing speech, 14th March,
1878, and Mr. Biggar, M.P.'s, questions to Mr. Secretary Cross, 1877,
ratifying Mr. Clare's claims on the British nation in having "con¬
tributed materially to the present system of construction of vessels of
ar." Mr. Parnell, M.P., in Parliament, 1878; But for "Mr. John
Clare this question of iron shipbuilding would not have been so much
in advance for another 10 years." Mr. Roebuck, M.P., 1878 ; But for
"the fleet at the Dardanelles the Russian army would have been in
Constantinople." (Cheers.) Mr. Smith, First Lord of Admiralty, 5th
August, 1878 ; The fleet at the Dardanelles caused "the happy re¬
sult which we are now rejoicing at, of a peace with honour to this
country." (Hear, hear.) Mr. Clare, having spent, 25 of the best years
of his life developing sound standard principles for life-preserving
ships of metal, Appeals, on national grounds, for aid in purse and
person from the public, possessing principle and conrage to uphold
right, truth, and justice.
JOHN CLARE.
No. 52, Parliament-street, London, S.W.
