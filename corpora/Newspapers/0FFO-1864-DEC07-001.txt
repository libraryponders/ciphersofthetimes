PUBLIC APOLOGY to WILLIAM WEBBER,
Esq., 1, Sion-terrace, Tunbridge-wells, aKent, consulting surgeon.
—I. Henry Edwards, of Tunbridge-wells, retired butcher, a town com¬
missioner and house agent, hereby disclaim all ground whatever for
having imputed to Mr. Webber the crime of forging a will, and express
my sincere regret for having ever made use of any expression towards
that gentleman, imputing or tending to impute that he had done so
or that his conduct had to my knowledge been otherwise than just and
honourable, and beg to thank him (for his having agreed to stay the
legal proceedings he has commenced against me through his solicitor,
Mr. Thomas John Jerwood, of No. 17, Ely-place, Holborn, London, in
the Court of Exchequer of Pleas, in respect of the language I made use
of towards him at a public meeting held at the Town-hall on the 26th
day of August last (for the relief of the sufferers by a fire in the town),
and I consent to this APOLOGY being printed, published, posted,
and circulated as Mr. Webber may think proper, and agree to pay Mr.
Webber all the costs occasioned thereby, and all other costs, charges.
or expenses whatever which Mr. Webber has incurred in respect of
this matter, and I also agree to pay forthwith the sum of 10 guineas
for such charitable or public purpose as Mr. Webber may think pro¬
per. — Dated this 3d day of December, 1864.
HENRY EDWARDS, Frant-road, Tunbridge-wells.
Witness.—W. P. Trustram, Tunbridge-wells, Solicitor for the said
Henry Edwards.
