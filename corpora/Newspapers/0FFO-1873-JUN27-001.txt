CHARITY COMMISSION.—In the Matter of the
OLD BURIAL GROUND of the HAMBRO SYNAGOGUE,
situate at Horton, in the parish of St. Leonard's, Shoreditch, in the
county of Middlesex.—By direction of the Boald of Charity Commis¬
sioners for England and Wales, notice is hereby given that an Order
is proposed to be made by them, after the expiration of one calendar
month, to be computed from the first publication of this notice¬
1. Appointing Trustees of the above-mentioned Charity.
2. Giving directions, by way of scheme, for the future regulation of
the same Chariry.
The following persons have been proposed for appointment as
Trustees, namely:—Sir Anthony de Rothschild, of New-court, Lon¬
don Baronet; Sampson Lucas, of Gloster-square, Bayswater, in the
county of Middlesex, Esquire; Lionel Louis Cohen, of No. 27, Mon¬
tague-square, in the same county. Esquire; Baron Henry de Worms, of
South-lodge, South-place, Knightsbridge; Asser Henry Moses, of
No. 34, Westbourne-terrace, Esquire; Henry Aaron Isaacs, of 16, Bel¬
size-park-gardens, Esquire; and Joseph Michael Isaacs, of 21, Bel¬
size-park, Hampstead, in the same county, Esquire.
It is proposed that the said order shall direct by way of scheme that
the charity property shall be held and used as a burial ground, in such
manner and for such purposes as the Vestry or Council of the United
Synagogue shall, in accordance with the objects of the scheme con¬
tained in the United Synagogues Act, 33 and 34 Vic., c. 116, and con¬
sistently therewith direct, supject to all such powers, provisions, and
declarations as are in the said scheme expressly or impliedly con¬
tained and declared concerning the same.
