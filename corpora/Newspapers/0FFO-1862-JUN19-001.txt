WESTMINSTER-BRIDGE and KENNINGTON¬
ROAD TRAMWAY.—Several gentlemen having expressed
their opinion that the whole of the costs in relation to this prosecution
ought not to be borne by Woronzow Greig, Esq., of Surrey Lodge, who
commenced the legal proceedings purely upon public grounds, feeling
that no local Board had the power to grant permission to the laying
down of the rails, such power being vested only in Parlament; and
inasmuch as the tramway was found to be an obstructive and dangerous
nuisance, it is proposed to raise, by way of subscription, such a sum as
will pay (if not the whole) the larger proportion of the legal expenses.
SUBSCRIPTIONS will be thankfully received by Mr. Samuel Henton,
No. 7, Bridge-street, Lambeth.
