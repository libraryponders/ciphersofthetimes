ROYAL AGRICULTURAL SOCIETY's MEET¬
ING, 1863.—At a numerous and influentially attended MEETING
of the principal Gentry, Agriculturalists, and other Residents of the
county and city of Worcester, held at the Shirehall, on Saturday, the
14th September, 1861, to promote the holding of the Royal Agricultural
Society's Meeting for 1863 in Worcester,
Lord LYTTELTON (Lord Lieutenant of the county) in the Chair;
The following resolutions having been duly proposed and seconded,
were carried unanimously:—
Proposed by the Hon. W. Coventry, seconded by the Hon. F. Lygon,
M.P.;
That this Meeting is decidedly of opinion that the holding of the
Meeting
the Royal Agricultural Society at Worcester, in 1863, is cal¬
culated to raise the standard of cultivation and impreve the breed of
farming stock, to supply a powerful stimulus to agricultural progress
in the county, and generally to advance all interests connected there¬
with.
Proposed by Sir T. E. Winnington, Bart., M.P., seconded by J. S.
Pakington

That this Meeting cordially concurs in the effort now being made in
order to obtain the holding of this Meeting in Worcester, in 1863, and
pledges itself zealously to co-operate for that purpose in the raising of
a fund to defray the necessary expenses, and in bringing the claims of
Worcester prominently before the notice of the Council of the Royal
Aegre Soc.
Proposed by Sir E. A. H. Lechmere, Bart., seconded by the Rev. John
Pearson;
That this Meeting recognises and adopts the General and Working
Committees of the County and City previously appointed and act¬
ing for the promotion of the above object, and will exert itself to
strengthen their hands in every possible way.
Proposed by T. G. Curtler, Esq., seconded by A. H. Royds, Esq.;
That steps be immediately taken for organizing a subscription in the
several towns and parishes throughout the county, and that appplica¬
tions be issued to the agaiculturists and others in each parish, inviting
their co-operation, and soliciting contributions to the fund.
LYTTELTON, Chairman.
Proposed by the Mayor of Worcester, seconded by E. Evans, Esq.;
That the best thanks of this Meeting be given to the Lord Lieutenant,
for his kindness in calling the Meeting, and the ability and impar¬
tiality he has shown in presiding over it.
By order of the Working Committee,
Joint.
W. H. BENTLEY, Hon. Secs.
