BIRMINGHAM,
Sept. 6, 1866.—Messrs. R. W.
Winfield and Co.—Gentlemen.—As the author of a book entitled
the "Londoniad," containing a statement in reference to your firm,
which has no foundation in fact, and is libellous in its character, I
unhesitatingly acknowledge the injustice I have done you by its in¬
sertion, and tender you my most sincere APOLOGY. As the book
above referred to has been circulated in Birmingham and elsewhere,
I consent to your publishing this apology in the public newspapers,
and in such other manner as you may think proper; and I undertake
forthwith to deliver up to you all the copies of the book now in my
possession or power, and not to circulate any book, pamphlet, or payer
containing the libel complained of. I acknowledge your kindness
in staying legal proceedings on my making this public retractation and
apology and paying the costs of the action you have commenced against
me.—I am, gentlemen, yours faithfully, JAMES TORRINGTON
SPENCER LIDSTONE.—Witness, William Docker, solicitor, Bir¬
mingham.
