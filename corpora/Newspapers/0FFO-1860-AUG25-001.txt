BURIAL VAULT in the CHURCHYARD of the
PARISH of ST. JOHN ZACHARY, in the city of London.—
An order having been made by Her Majesty in Council, directing that
the coffins in the public vault in the above-mentioned churchyard be
laid down and covered with earth and powdered charcoal, and that the
entrance be filled up with earth and brickwork, notice is hereby given,
that from and after the expiration of one calendar month from the
date hereof, the work required to be performed by such order will be
forthwith effected, and the VAULT finally CLOSED. Any person or
persons, having any relations or friends interred in the said vault, and
whose remains he, she, or they may be desirous of having removed, are
required forthwith to give notice thereof to me, the undersigned, and
to proceed to obtain from the proper authority the necessary faculty for
that purpose. After the vault is closed under the above mentioned
order, no remains will, under any circumstances, be permitted to be
removed. —Dated this 16th day of August, 1860.
HORATIO GREGORY, Vestry Clerk,
Wexchandlers'-hall, London.
