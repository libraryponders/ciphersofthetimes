FRAUDS on GAS COMPANIES.—To the Pheenix
Gas Light and Coke Company, London.—Gentlemen.—I hereby
APOLOGIZE in this public newspaper for having knowingly connected
a surreptitious pipe to your supply service without a meter, you having
cut off the same owing to non-payment. By means of this I was enabled
to light up this house for sevensnights, I being unable to pay and you
being unable to ascertain the quantity of gas consumed. I have also
to express my gratitude for your lenity in not taking criminal pro¬
ceedings against me, as in consequence of my present bankruptey and
my not having yet obtained a certificate I have no money to pay a fine,
and I must have been imprisoned for a lengthened period.
I am, gentlemen, your very humble servant,
(Signed) SAML. C. WALLER, the Dover Castle, Little Surrey¬
street, Blackfriars-road, August 31, 1866.
