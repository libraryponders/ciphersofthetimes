LONDON HOSPITAL, Whitechapel-road, E., 8th
January, 1870.—Notice to Governors.—The wards of this Hospi¬
tal are now full, while the applications for admission of urgent extra
cases continue very pressing and numerous. It is therefore necessary
to request that Governors will suspend the issue of in-patient recom¬
mendations for the present, with a view to prevent disappointment on
the part of intending applicants for admission into the Hospital.
. J. NIXON House Governor and Secretary.
THE Rev. GEORGE C. BERKELEY, Vicar of
Southminster Essex, ventures to APPEAL to the Governors and
Sunscribers of the London Orphan Asylum for their VOTES in behalf
of ELLEN MARY TOMLINSON, age eight years, who is a candinate
at the forthcoming Election on the 24th inst. Mr. Tomlinson, a young
man in the prime of life, was managing clerk at a bank in Maldon,
and died, after only a few months' illness, last summer, leaving a widow
and six children most scantily provided for. Within the last month
Mrs. Tomlinson and her six childron have been visited with scarlet
fever in a most virulent form, of which three children have died, and a
fourth is not likely to recover. Mrs. Tomlinson is still very weak.
Proxies will be gratefully received by Mr. Berkeley; or Mr. Sewell
and Mr. W. Humpherys, of Maldon.—Southminster Vicarage, Jan.
10, 1870.
