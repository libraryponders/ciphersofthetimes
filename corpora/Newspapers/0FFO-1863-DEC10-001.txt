PROPOSED UNION of the LIBERTY of
CAWOOD WISTOW and OTLEY with the WEST RIDING
of YORKSHIRE.—Notice is hereby given, that it was resolved by
the Justices of the Peace for the West Riding of Yorkshire, assembled
at the Quarter Sessions of the Peace held at Leeds, in and for the said
Riding, on Tuesday, the 20th day of October last, that it seemed
expedient to the Justices of the Peace of the said Riding that the
union of the liberty of Cawood Wistow and Otley with the said West

under the Act of the 13th and 14th years of the reign of Her present
Majesty, chapter 105, intituled "An Act for Facilitating the Union of
Liberties with the Counties in which they are situate;" and it was also
further resolved and erdered, that a PETITION should be presented
to Her Majesty, praying that such union should take place ; and that
the further consideration of such position should be adjourned until
the General Quarter Sessions of the Peace, to be holden at Wakefield,
in and for the said Riding, on Tuesday, the 5th day of January then
next; and notice is also hereby given, that the further consideration
of the said petition will take place at the said last-mentioned Quarter
Sessions to be holden at the Court-house, in Wakefield aforesaid, on
Tuesday, the 5th day of January next, at the hour of 12 o'clock at noon.
— Dated this 1st day of December, 1863.
BENJAMIN DIXON, Deputy Clerk of the Peace
for the said West Riding of Yorkshire.
