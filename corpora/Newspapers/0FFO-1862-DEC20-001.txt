MAGENTA or ROSEINE DYE.—MEDLOCK's
PATENT.—Notice to Manufacturing Chymists. Dyers, and
others.—The cause of "Simpson v. Wilson," heard on the 10th inst, at
Guildhall, before the Chief Justice of the Court of Queen's Bench, re¬
mains undecided, ten of the jurymen were in favour of the plaintiffs-
but the remaining two being of a different opinion the cause must be
tried again. The plaintiffs are determined to do so at the carliest
possible period; they are also determined to prosecute any others
who may attempt to invade their rights in the meantime.
HENRY and FREDK. CHESTER, Newington-butts, S.,
Plaintiffs' Attorneys, 11th Dec. 1862.
ANILINE DYES.—GIRARD's PATENT for IM¬
PROVEMENTS in PREPARING COLOURING MATTERS
for DYEING and PRINTING.—The undersigned acknowledge having
INFRINGED this PATENT, by making and selling dyes manufac¬
tured according to Girard's process; and, in consideration of Messrs.
Simpson, Maule, and Nicholson withdrawing proceedings taken by
them against me for so doing, I hereby express my regret for having
thus invaded their rights, as sole vendors of these dyes, and declare
that I have discontinued to make or sell such dyes, and will not do so
during the term of the above mentioned patent. —Dated this 28th day
of November, 1862.
EDW. KEIGHLEY.
