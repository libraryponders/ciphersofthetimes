IN the HIGH COURT of JUSTICE, Admiralty
 Division.—(1877 G., No. 244.)—Ships WATERLOO and STELL¬
ING.—The General Steam Navigation Company v. Joicey and others.
—Whereas, in an action instituted in the High Court of Justice on
behalf of the General Steam Navigation Company against James
Joicey and others, a Judge of the Admiralty Division of the said
Court did, on the 8th day of August, 1277, amongst other things, pro¬
nounce that, in respect of loss or damage to ships, goods, merchandise.
or other things, caused by reason of the improper navigation of the
steam vessel Waterloo, on the occas on of the collision between the
said steam vessel and the steam vessel Stelling, on the 3rd of Jonuary,
1877, the owpers of the said steam vessel Waterloo are answerable in
damages to an amount not exceeding the sum of £4,115 13s. 8d. (such
sum being at the rate of £8 for each ton of the registered tonnage of
the said steam vessel Waterloo, without deduction on account of
engine room), exclusive of interest and costs. This is, therefore, to
tive notice to all persons having any CLAIM in respect of the LOSS or
DAMAGE caused as aforesaid, that if they do not come in and enter
their claims in the said action on or before the 5th day of November
next ensuing, they will be excluded from sharing in the aforesaid
amount.—Dated this 17th day of August, 1877.
