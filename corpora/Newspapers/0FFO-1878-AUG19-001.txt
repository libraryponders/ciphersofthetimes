CHARITY COMMISSION.—In the matter of the
crity known as Dean CLARKES TRUST for the, AUG¬
MENTATION of 10 SMALL LIVINGS, and for the Renair of the
Cathedral Church of St. Paul, London, founded by the Will of Wil¬
liam Clarke, D.D., dated 22d April, 1679, and in the matter of the
Charitable, Trusts Acts, 1853 to 1869.—By direction of the Board of
Charity Commissioners for England and Wales, notice is hereby given,
that an ORDER is proposed to be made by them after the expiration
of one calendar month, to be computed from the first publication of
this notice—
Removing and discharging, at their own request, the Right Honour¬
able John William, Lord Rayleigh, of Ferlingham Place, in the county
of Essex; Thoma Harvey Bramston, of Skreens, in the same county,
Esquire, a Lieutenant-Coionel in Her Majesty's Regiment of Grena¬
dier Guards; William Michol Tufnell, of Hatfield Place, in the same
county, Esquire; and Arthur Joliffe Tufnell, of Langleys, Great
Waltham, in the same county, Esquire, from being Trustees of the
abovementioned Charity; and
Giving directions by way of scheme for the future administration of
the said Charity. It is proposed that the Order shall direct by way of
scheme, that upon the transfer into the name of the Official Trustees
of Charitable Funds of the sum of £10,000 Consolidated £3 per Cent.
Annuities, to provide for the augmentation of the benefices now en¬
titled to the benefits of the Charity, the Trustees may surrender the
existing lease to the Dean and Chapter of St. Paul's, who hoid the
estate comprised therein subject to the obligation imposed upon them
by an Order of Her Majesty in Council, dated 13th August, 1872, to
apply all the net income thereof in perpetuity to providing for the re¬
pair of the said Cathedral Church of Saint Paul;
And appointing the said Dean and Chapter to be the Trustees for
the administration of the fund, as set apart to satisfy the trusts of the
said will, for the augmentation of poor livings.
Any objections to the proposed Order, or suggestions for its modifi¬
cation, may be transmitted, in writing, to the Secretary of the said
Commissioners, Whitehall, London, S.W., within 21 days next after
the first publication of this notice. —Dated this 13th day of August,
1878.
HENRY M. VANE, Secretary.
