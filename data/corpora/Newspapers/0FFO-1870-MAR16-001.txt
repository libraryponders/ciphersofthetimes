timated their intention to institute proceedings against me, the
undersigned Joseph Camplin Prosser, of Devenden-vicarage, near
Chepstow, in the county of Monmouth, Clerk in Holy Orders, for
having received into my house and taken the care and charge of a
lunatic without having first obtained, in compliance with the provi¬
sions of the Act of the 8th and 9th Vict., cap. 100, being an Act for
the regulation of the care and treatment of lunatics, an order of re¬
ception and medical certificates; and whereas, in consideration of the
acknowledgment and apology hereinafter set forth, the said Com¬
missioners have, under the special circumstances of my case, consented
to stay the prosecution, now I, the said Joseph Camplin Prosser do
hereby ACKNOWLEDGE that I have VIOLATED the LAW by re¬
ceiving into my house, and taking the care and charge of a lunatic,
without having first obtained the necessary order and medical certifi¬
cates; and I do hereby express my deep concern and regret for having
so done, and do thus publicly apologize for the same, and I do solemnly
promise not again to offend in like manner. —Dated this 14th day of
March. 1870.
JOSEPH CAMPLIN PROSSER.
