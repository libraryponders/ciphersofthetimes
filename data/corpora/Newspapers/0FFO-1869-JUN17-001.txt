WHITEHALL.—Whereas, the Queen having been
pleased to grant unto the Baron de BLISS, of Brandon-park,
Suffolk, Her Royal license that he and his issue (in compliance with
his late uncle's will) might take the name and arms of Bliss, and
further commanded that such concessions should be void unless such
arms should be duly recorded in Her Majesty's College of Arms, which
been recorded accerdingly; and whereas Col. Carlo Antonio
Barreto, of the kingdom of Spain, by his will, dated 3d day of January,
1867, gave and bequeathed unto the said Baron de Bliss, his heirs and
assigns, for ever, all his estates, vineyards, plantations, lands, and all
other his property in the kingdom of Spain, and in all other king¬
doms; but he made such gift upon the express condition that he took
and used his surname, and notified the adoption of his name in as
public a manner as he could in England, and in all other kingdoms in
which he held estates; and, in compliance with such testamentary
injunctions in the said will, the said Baron de Bliss notifies that he has
ADOPTED the SURNAME of BARRETO in England, and in all
other kingdoms in which the tostator possesses estates.
