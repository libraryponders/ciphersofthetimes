LONDON RIFLE VOLUNTEER BRIGADE.—
April 9th, 1862.—SPECIAL NOTICE.— All members entitled to
join the brigade for the Brighton Review under Brigade Order No. 7,
31st March, can obtain their drill and railway tickets at Head-quarters
between the hours of 10 a.m. and 4 p.m., until Monday, the 14th inst.
inclusive ; and at Guildhall after drills and parades until Saturday
next. The railway tickets are of two classes, the first available for
Saturday or Sunday, the latter for Monday, both returnable on Mon¬
day or Tuesday mornings.
"All ranks are informed that the railway arrangements as published
by the Brighton Railway Company must be strictly adhered to.
Ammunition, particulars as to issue, will be published in Monday's
orders.
Times for parade on the 21st will be published on receipt of order

The commanding officer trusts that on Saturday next there will be
a strong muster of the brigade, as on this parade Captain Warde, the
Leint.-Colonel elect, will be introduced to the regiment and take com¬
mand for the day.
By order,
A. T. EWENS, Capt. and Adjutant,
LONDON SCOTTISH RIFLE VOLUNTEERS.—
No. 1. Tickets for Brighton, on Easter Monday, may be had at
Head-quarters (1s. 6d. each), on Saturday, and following days, until
noon on Thursday next, after which hour all tickets not disposed of
must be returned to the Railway Company, and members not provided
with tickets will have to pay the ordinary fare.
No. 2. Members going to Brighton must provide their own provisions:
none but the regimental haversack will be permitted to be carried in
the ranks. This may be had, price 2s., at Edmiston's, 5, Charing-cross,
by giving three days' notice there.
No. 3. Ammunition will be supplied with the railway tickets.
No. 4. The hour of Parade on Easter Monday will be advertised
in The Times and Daily Telegraph on Thursday, the 17th, and Satur¬
day, the 19th.
No. 5. All members going to Brighton on Easter Monday are par¬
ticularly requested to attend the parade on Saturday, the 19th, at
Head-quarters, at 4 p.m. There will be no firing on that day,
No. 6. On Friday (this day), the 11th, members are invited to come to
the Range, after 2 p.m., to join the working party at earthwork. No
uniform. Tools provided.
By order S. FLOOD PAGE, Capt. and Adjt.
Head-quarters, 8, Adelphi-terrace, W. C., April 9, 1862.
