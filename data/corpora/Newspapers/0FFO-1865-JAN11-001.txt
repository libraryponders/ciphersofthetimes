of George Michael Papayanni, of Liverpool, in the county of Lancaster,
and others, the owners of the screw steam ship or vessel Amalia, as
plaintiffs, against the owners of the screw steamship Marie de Brabant,
and against the owners of the cargo laden therein, and others, for
the purpose of having the amount of their, the said plaintiffs', liability
determined in respect of the loss or damage caused to the said vessel
Marie de Brabant, and to any goods, merchandise, or other things
whatsoever, by reason of the improper navigation of the said vessel
Amalia, whereby a collision occurred on the 15th day of May, 1863,
between the said vessels; and also for distribution of the said amount
rateably among the persons entitled to participate therein; the Judge
of the said Court did, on the 15th day of November, 1864, among other
things, pronounce that in respect of loss or damage to ships, goods,
merchandize, or other things, caused as aforesaid, the owners of the
soid steam vessel Amalia are answerable in damages to the amount of
£14,600 and no more, being at the rate of £8 for each ton of the gross
tonnage of the said steam vessel Amalia, without deduction on account
of engine room: This is therefore to give notice to all persons having
any CLAIMS in respect of the LOSS or DAMAGE caused as afore¬
said, that if they do not come in and enter their claims in the said
cause on or before the 1st day of February, 1865, they will be excluded
from sharing in the aforesaid amount. —Dated this 10th day of Decem¬
