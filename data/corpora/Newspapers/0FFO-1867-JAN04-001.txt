LONDON STREET REFORM SOCIETY.
President—Sir WILLIAM FRASER, Bart., M.A., F.S.A.
On the 13th of June, 1865, it was admitted unanimously in the House
of Commons that the condition of London as regards paving, lighting,
and cleansing was disgraceful. No improvement has taken place.
The traffic of London becomes every day more unmanageable. The
public vehicles are inferior to those of provincial and continental

This Society has been formed to stimulate public attention; to
collect, condense, and make known facts; to point out defects in the
law: to enforce provisions at present neglected; and to watch Bills in¬
troduced into Parliament on these subjects.
The London Street Reform Society is not pledged to support any
specific change in the government of the metropolis.
Those who may wish to join the Society are requested to write to
J. S. Halv. Eso., Hon. Sec., L.S.R.S., 88, St. James's-street, S.W.
