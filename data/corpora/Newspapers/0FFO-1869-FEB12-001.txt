HOLY LOCH OYSTER and MUSSEL FISHERY.
—Notice is hereby given, that on the 1st day of February, 1869,
an ORDER was settled and made by the Board of Trude, under "The
Sea Fisheries Act, 1868," for the ESTABLISHMENT and MAINTEN¬
ANCE by James Hunter, Esq., of Hafton, of a several OYSTER and
MUSSEL FISHERY at ARDNADAM, in the Holy Loch, Firth of
Clyde, in the county of Argyll: and that provision is made by the said
order for the fohowing objects:—
1. To authorise the said James Hunter, his heirs and assignees, to
establish and maintain a several oyster and mussel fishery on parts of
the foreshore and bed of the Holy Loch, situate within the united parish
of Dunoon and Kilmun, in the county of Argyll, containing an area of
307 imperial acres, or thereabouts, and bounded as described in the
order and delineated and coloured red on the plan therein referred
to and deposited at the Board of Trade.
2. To confer on the said James Hunter, his heirs and assignees, a
right of several oyster and mussel fishery within the limits described
in the oreter.
3. To provide for the marking out of the limits of the said several
hishery, and of the limits of an excepted part of the foreshore opposite
the village of Sandbank, and for giving notice of such limits in the
event of the marks being obliterated, as prescribed in the order.
4. Te provide that no buildings, erections, embankments, or other
works, except as therein mentioned, shall be commenced or executed
without the cousent of the Board of Trade, and that accounts shall be
rendered to the Board of Trade of capital, expenditure, and income,
and oysters and mussels sold.
5. Saving rights of salmon fishing in the Holy Loch and the river
Meikle Echaig; saving certain rights of way mentioned in the order;
and saving rights under "The Crown Lands Act, 1866."
6. To provide that the order shall continue in operation for 60 years
from its confirmation by Act of Parlament.
Copies of the order and plan have been deposited for public inspec¬
tion, free of charge, at the Custom-houses of Glasgow and Greenock, and
at the office of the Union Bank of Scotland in Dunoon; and copies of
the order will be furnished by Mr. John Millar, at the said office, and
by the agents named below, at the price of 1s, each, to all persons apply¬
ing for the same.
Dated this 11th day of February, 1869.
W. F. SKENE and PEACOCK, W.S., Edinburgh.
