TO Mr. GEORGE BARHAM, Express Country
Milk Company (Limited), 28, Museum-Street, Bloomsbury.—
Sir.—You having consented, at my solicitation, to abandon your
prosecution against me upon a charge of stealing a churn of milk,
your property, from the Euston-square Railway Station, on the 10th of
February last, I have, at your suggestion, this day paid the sum of £15
into the Poor-box of the Marylebone Police Court. I have also to
express my sincere thanks to you for having consented to abandon the
prosecution. I furthermore declare that the defence sot up for me.
that you were actuated by a spirit of animosity, caused by a rivalry of
trade, was and is without foundation, and that I am sorry for the
same. I also authorize you to advertise this letter, at my expense,
twice in any three London newspapers you may select.
DOYour obliged Servant.
JOHN HOPKINS, 34. Goding-street, Vauxhall.,
