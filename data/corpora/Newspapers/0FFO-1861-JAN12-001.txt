ST. ANNE, (Limehouse), Middlesex.—The Church¬
wardens of the above parish hereby give notice that it is the in¬
tention of the Right Hon. Sir George Cornewall Lewis, Baronet, one of
Her Majesty's principal Secrotaries of State, to recommend to Her
Majesty in Council, that for the purpose of preventing the vaults under
the church of St. Anne, Limehouse, from becoming or continuing
dangerous or injurious to the public health, an order should be made
for the adoption of the following measures, viz.:—That all the coffins
beneath the said church be embedded in fresh earth, mixed with char¬
coal, and enclosed by brickwork or concrete in an airtight manner.—
Dated this 10th day of January, 1861.
Church-row, Limehouse, E.	A. A. WALTER, Vestry Clerk.

NOTICE to the HOLDERS of UNEXPIRED REMITTANCE
CERTIFICATES for PASSAGES to NEW SOUTH WALES.
THE Holders of Passage Remittance Certificates,
issued by the Colonial Government of New South Wales since the
1st January, 1860, who have not yet communicated with the Emigration
Board, are requested forthwith to SEND IN their CERTIFICATES,
stating at the same time whether they wish to emigrate or not, in
order that steps may be taken either to provide passages for those who
wish to emigrate, or to enable the depositors in the colony to obtain a
return of their deposits in the case of those who do not wish to emi¬
grate.
By order of the Board,
JOHN WALPOLE, Assistant Secretary.
