CASTLE BAYNARD WARD.—At a WARD¬
MOTE, held this day, at Mr. Johnson's Room, 4, Sermon-lane,
Doctors'-commons, before the Worshipful Sills John Gibbons, Esq.,
Alderman of the Ward, for the Election of a fit and proper person to
be of the Common Council of the same Ward, for the remainder of the
current year, in the room and stead of Sills John Gibbons, Esq., who
held such office of Common Councilman, but who has been elected
Alderman of the same Ward.
Moved by Mr. Jennings, seconded by Mr. Bishop, and unanimously
resoled;
That George Roger Longden, Esq., cititen, and spectacle maker, be
elected a Common Councilman for this Ward for the remainder of the
current year.
Moved by Mr. Procter, seconded by Mr. Lavell, and unanimously

That this Wardmote desires respectfully to tender to Sir Henry
Muggeridge, Knight, its cordial thanks for the faithful, zealous, and
efficient manner in which, for nearly 10 years, he represented this Ward
in the Court of Aldermen, for the liberal support and patronage ex¬
tended by him at all times to the charitable institutions of the Ward,
and for his uniformly courteous and gentlemanly demeanour to all its
inhabitants. This Wardmote also earnestly hopes that, relieved from
the cares and duties of an onerous office, his health may be restored.
and that many years of domestic happiness may be vouchsafed to him
and to his amiable lady and family.
That the foregoing resolution be transcribed, and emblazoned,
framed, glazed, and presented to Sir Henry Muggeridge.
Moved by Mr. Deputy Farrar, seconded by Mr. Longden, and unani¬
mously resolved
That the best thanks of this Wardmote be offered to Sills John
Gibbons, Esq., the Alderman of the Ward, for his courteous conduct,
this day, in presiding over the Wardmote.
Resolved. That the foregoing resolutions be advertised in the usual
dally papers, and the City Press.

J. S. NEWBON Ward Clerk.
