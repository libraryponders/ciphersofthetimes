CLICQUOT's CHAMPAGNE.—Counterfeit Brand.
—Injunction.—It having been recently discovered that certain
persons were offering for sale several parcels of spurious and inferior
wines, in bottles of which the corks are branded with a counterfeit of
the brand of the firm of VEUVE CLICQUOT PONSARDIN, an
INJUNCTION was, on the 13th day of February, 1863, granted by His
Honour the Master of the Rolls in the suit of "Ponsardin and others
v. Stear and others," restraining the defendant Stear, his servants and
agents, from selling or offering for sale, or in any way dealing with the
said wine, and restraining the defendants, Messrs. Joseph Barber and
Co., of Brewer's-quay, from parting with any of the said wine which is
now in their possession, and for which they have issued warrants.
And a further injunction was, on the 18th day of the said month
of February, also granted by his Honcur the Master of the Rolls
in the suit of "Ponsardin and others v. Sir Samuel Morton
Peto, Bart, and others," to restrain them from parting with
another parcel of 149 cases of the same wine now lying at the
Victoria London Docks, or any part of such last mentioned wines.
The whole of the wines in question were (with others) imported in
December last in the sailing ship Gazelle, from Dunkirk, in cases
marked VT with B under, entered by Comyn, and the warrants or some
of them in the name of one H. Bernard. All persons are hereby cautioned
against buying or advancing money upon the warrants for any wine
with such counterfeit brand, and any persons to whom the same may
be offered for sale, or as security for advances, are requested to com¬
municate immediately with Mr. Robert Selby, 31, Eastcheap, the plain¬
tiff's sole agent for Great Britain and its dependencies, or with me.
J. WILSON NICHOLSON, Plaintiff's Solicitor, 48, Lime-street.
WHEREAS GEORGE GARRARD ABRAHAM
BOULTON (otherwise George Boulton) late of 11, Vittoria-place,
Stoke Newington-road, Middlesex, gentleman (who died in the year 1827),
by his last will and testament, dated the 18th day of July, 1827, gave
and bequeathed to Henry Booker, Elizabeth Cousins (formerly Booker),
and Caroline Booker £150 each; to his wife's sister, Ann Bacon, widow,
£100 ; and to her son, Thomas Bacon, £100 ; and to her three other
children, Ann Bacon, George Bacon, and James Bacon, £50 each; to
Mary Porter, wife of John Golden Porter, for her separate use,
£100 ; to Joseph Boulton Porter, £200; George Richard Manwaring,
the plaintiff, £500; and Richard Manwaring, £300 ; legacies in the
said will mentioned payable on the death of his widow, who died in
1862; and whereas, by an Order of the High Court of Chan¬
cery, in a cause "George Richard Manwaring against James Bacon,"
an inquiry is directed to ascertain whether any, and if any, which of
the LEGATEES named in the said testator's will whose respective
legacies have not been paid are dead, and, if any of them be dead, then
when they respectively died, and who was or were their respective per¬
sonal representative; and also whether any, and if any, which of the
said unpaid legatees had assigned, or mortgaged, or incumbered their
respective legacies, and if so, to whom, and in what manner; and
whether any, and if any, which of the said unpaid legatees had become
bankrupts, and if so who were the assignees of their respective estates.—
All persons, therefore, claiming to be such legatees, the representatives
of such as are dead, and also all persons claiming to be assignees,
mortgagees of or incumbrancers on the said legacies or assignees
of such of the said legatecs as have been bankrupts, are, by their solici¬
tors, on or before the 20th day March, 1863, to come in and prove their
claims to the said legacies, or any part thereof, at the chambers of the
Vice-Chancellor Wood, 11, New-square, Lincoln's-ian, Middlesex; or, in
default, they will be peremptorily excluded from the benest of
the said order. Thursday, the 25th day of March, 1863, at 12 o'clock
at noon, at the said chambers, is appointed for hearing and adjudica¬
tion upon the said claims. —Dated this 2d day of March, 1863.
HY. LEMAN, Chief Clerk.

LIFEBOAT SERVICES.—The Committee of the

public for ASSISTANCE to enable them to meet the continued heavy
demands on the Institution's 123 lifeboat establishments. During the

bankers, and by the Secretary, Mr. Richard Lewis, at the Institu¬
tion, 14, John-Street, Adelphi.
