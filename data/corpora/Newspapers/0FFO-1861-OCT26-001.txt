IN Her MAJESTY's COURT of PROBATE.—In
the Goods of WILLIAM CLARKE, deceased.—Notice is hereby
given, that a CITATION, dated the 17th day of May, 1861, has been
issued out of Her Majesty's Court of Probate, directed to Ann
Eudale, John Clarke, and Margaret Carter, by which it appears that
by an affidavit of Robert Ellis, of Fleet, in the county of Lincoln,
sworn the 4th day of May, it appears that William Clarke, late of
Gedney, in the county of Lincoln, deceased, died on the 1st day of
March, 1861, intestate, a bachelor, without a parent, leaving the said
Ann Eudale, John Clarke, and Margaret Carter, and Mary Burton,
Betsy Speechley, and Emma Clarke, spinster, his natural and lawful
brothers and sisters him surviving, and that the said Mary Burton,
Betsy Speechley, and Emma Clark have renounced all their right, title.
and interest to the estate and effects of the said William Clarke, and
that the said Robert Ellis is a creditor of the said deceased; and the
said citation is to command the said Ann Eudale, John Clarke, and
Margaret Carter that within eight days after service thereof on them
they are to enter appearance, and accept or refuse letters of adminis¬
tration of the estate of the said William Clarke, or show cause why the
same should not be granted to the said Robert Ellis, a creditor of the
said deceased; and notice is hereby further given that, in default of
their so appearing, the Registrars of the said Court will proceed to grant
letters of administration of the said estate to the said Robert Ellis.
H. L. STRONG, Registrar.
