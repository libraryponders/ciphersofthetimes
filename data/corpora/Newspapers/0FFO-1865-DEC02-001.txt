JER Majesty's Emigration Commissioners will, on
Thursday, the 7th of December, at 1 o'clock, receive TENDERS
for a SHIP to CONVEY PASSENGERS to SYDNEY, New South
Wales, to be ready on such day between the 22d and 31st of January
next, both days inclusive, as the Commissioners may name. The ship
is to be ventilated on the plan of Dr. Edmunds, who will find the
necessary labour and materials at a charge, to be paid by the ship¬
owner, of 1s. 6d. per ton register. This will free the vessel from any
subsequent charge for the use of the apparatus. The ship must, sub¬
ject to the usual conditions, be furnished with Dr. Normandy's or
Messrs. Winchester and Graveley's fresh water distilling apparatus,
and with a competent person to manage it. Seven clear lay days for
embarcation and 14 days for disembarcation will be required. If the
ship be taken up to sail from the Mersey she will not be allowed to
embark her passengers elsewhere than in the Birkenhead Docks.
Tenders (form D. No. 25, January, 1865), specifications of fittings, and
list of mess utensils, small stores, &c., may be had at the office of
the Commissioners, or of the Emigration Officers at London and
Liverpool.
By order of the Board.
R. B. COOPER, Assistant-Secretary.
