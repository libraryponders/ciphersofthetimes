CITATION by EDICT.—The Honourable Sir John
Henry de Villiers, Knight, Chief Justice, and the other Judges
of the Honourable the Supreme Court of the Colony of the Cape of
Good Hope, do hereby make known:—That whereas Mary Fletcher
Kohler (born Hutchinson), of Cape Town, Wife of William Kohler,
has, on the 30th day of August, 1877, petitioned the Honourable the
Supreme Court for leave to sue by edict her husband, the said William
Kohler, now or lately residing in New York, in the United States of
America, for restitution of conjugal rights; and on his failing to
comply with that, then for dissolution of the bonds of marriage by
redson of malicious desertion, and for the custody of the children born
of the said marriage
And whereas the said William Kohler has been duly summoned on
the 30th of August, 1877, by Edictal Citation of this Honourable
Court to appear on the 19th day of December, 1877, before the said
Honourable Court, then and there to answer his said wife, Mary
Fletcher Kohler, and, to show cause, if any, why by reason of his un¬
lawful and malicious desertion from her since the month of May,
1866, he shall not by judgment of this Honourable Court be ordered
forthwith to return to and live and cohabit with her as her lawful
husband:
And whereas the said William Kohler has made default and has
not appeared according to the said citation, whereupon the said Mary
Fletcher Kohler requested the benefit of the default, with the profit
thereof, which were granted, and leave given to file her interdict.
And whereas, on the 12th day of February, 1878, on action brought,
this Honourable Court granted her a decree for the restitution of con¬
jugal rights against the said William Kohler, on the grounds of
malicious desertion and ordered him to return to her on or before the
1st day of August, 1878, which said order he has not obeyed, and has
refused or neglected so to do
And whereas, upon the application to this Honourable Court, on
the 5th August, 1878, by the said Mary Fletcher Kohler for leave to
sue her husband, the said William Kohler for the dissolution of the
bonds of Marriage by veason of his malicious desertion, and of his
failing to comply with and obey the said order of this Honourable

Now this Court, having taken the application of the said Mary
Fletcher Kohler into consideration, and having granted the same ac¬
cordingly, by these presents doth SUMMON the said WILLIAM
KOHLER, now or lately of No. 670, Hudson-street, New York
United States of America, to appear before the said Supreme Court
at Cape Town, on the Twentieth day of November, 1878, at ten
o'clock in the forenoon precisely, either in person or by proxy, to
show cause, if any, why by reason of his unlawful and malicious de¬
sertion as aforesaid, and by reason of his failure to comply with and
obey the said order of this Honourable Court to return to her, the
bonds of marriage now subsisting between them, shall not be dis¬
solved, and she be declared entitled to the custody of the children
born of the said marriage.
Thus done and granted in the Supreme Court of our said Colony, at
Cape Town, on the 5th day of August, 1878, in the forty-second year
of our reign. J. C. B. SERRURIER, Registrar of the Supreme.
Court.
C. H. VANZYL, Plaintiff's Attorney, De Lettre Chambers,
Church-square Cape Town.
