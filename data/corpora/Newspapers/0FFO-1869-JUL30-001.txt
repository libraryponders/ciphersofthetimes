No. 4.927.—Whereas in a cause instituted in the High Court of Admi¬
ralty of England on behalf of the London, Belgium, Brazil, and River
Plate and Royal Mail Steamship Company (Limited), the owners of
the steamship or vessel City of Buenos Ayres ar plaintiffs,
against the owners of the steamship Bismarck, and the owners of the
cargo lately laden therein and the master and crew of the said vessel
proceeding for their private effects on board thereof, and against al
and every other person or persons whatever interested in the said
vessel Bismarck, or the cargo or effects laden therein, or having any
right, claim, or interest whatever with reference to, or arising out of a
collision which occurred on or about the 19th day of December, 1866,
between the said vessels City of Buenos Ayres and Bismarck, for the
purpose of having the amount of the said plaintiffs' liability deter¬
mined in respect of the loss or damage caused to the said vessel Bis¬
marck, and to any goods, merchandise, or other things whatsoever,
by reason of the improper navigation of the said vessel City of Buenos
Ayres, whereby the said collision occurred; and also for distribution
of the said amount rateably among the persons entitled to participate
therein, the Judge of the said Court did, on the 15th day of June,
1869, among other things, pronounce that in respect of loss or damage
to ship's goods, merchandise, or other things caused as aforesaid, the
owners of the said steam vessel City of Bnenos Ayres are answerable
in damages to an amount not exceeding £10,514, such sum being at
the rate of £8 for each ton of the registered tonnage of the said steam
vessel City of Buenos Ayres, without deduction on account of engine
room. This is therefore to give notice to all persons having any
CLAIM in respect of the loss or damage caused as aforesaid, that if
they do not come in and enter their claims in the said cause, on or be¬
fore the 15th day of September, 1869, they will be excluded from shar¬
ing in the aforesaid amount. —Dated this 12th day of July, 1869.
