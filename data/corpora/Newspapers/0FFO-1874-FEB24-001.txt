MEN who have held LIVINGS or CURACIES in the County
of SALOP.—The late William Henry, Kinnaird Gibbons, formerly of
Harley, in the county of Salop, but latterly of the Old Lodze, in the
County of Surrey, Esquire, having by his will left a conaiderable sum
of money, the interest of which is to be applied for the benefit of the
widows and chiliren of clersymen who have held livings or curacies in

FIT of this CHARITY, are requested to send their names and ad¬
dresses to Messrs. Young, Jones. Roberts, and Hale, 2, St. Mildred's¬
court. Poultry, London. Applicants (being widows) must specify their
ages and their present income or means of subsistence, as also the
livings or curacies held by sheir husbands, and the annual value of
such livings or curacies. In the case of children the names and ages
of all must be given, and the same particu ars as to the livings or
curacies held by their fathers.
