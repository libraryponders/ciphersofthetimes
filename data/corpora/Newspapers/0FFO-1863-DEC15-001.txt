at Jasienica, in Galicia, containing, besides a sum in bank notes, the
following objects of value:—45 Galician Railway Shares (Carl Lud¬
wigsbahn) with Coupons, the first of which payable on the 1st July,
1864, viz.—Nos. 13162 to 13164, 30349, 60055, 74181, 74182, 95926 to
95947, 96479 to 96494; eight Estate Disencumbrance Bonds of the dis¬
trict of Cracow, with the newly-issued Coupons belonging thereto, viz.—
Nos. 63, 250, 562, of 10,000 florins each, Nos. 78, 261, 429-30, of 5,000
florins each, No. 8524, of 1,000 florins one necklace, of 24 brillants of
pure water, all of equal size, each weighing 2 carats, and set in gold in
quadrangular shape ; one rivière, of 60 fine white brillants, of two
carats each, and set in silver, each set numbered; one chain, of 117
brillants, the middle ones weighing three to four carats, the others
falling off in size towards both ends; one solitaire, of seven to 10
carats, set à jour, as a clasp for pearls; one row of pearls, 142 larger
ones, and 26 smaller ones on both ends, with one emerald clasp of
considerable size, richly set with brillants; several long gold breast
pins, with pearls of the shape of pears hanging down; three large
sapphires of extraordinary beauty, set in such a manner that each of
them can be used as a clasp, and that all three (joined by golden clasps)
form a clasp of five parts, each sapphire severally is set with a double
row of brillants, and the whole of superior beauty; one School Medal
of the Cracow University, silver gilt, with the inscription "Maurycy
Mann;" five ducats, of Warsaw colnage of 1831, with the Polish cagle;
six silver coffee spoons, marked "A. K.," and several rare dollars and
medals. The public is cautioned not to purchase the above shares,
bonds, and jewels, the necessary steps having been taken to cancel the
bonds and shares. In event of any of these articles being offered for
sale, the public is requested to inform, immediately, either Count
Joseph Zaluski, at Jasienica, in Galicia, the owner of the stolen pro¬
perty; or Mr. Anton Hoelzel, banker, at Cracow. Any information
that may result in the discovery of the 1 rpetrator of the theft or the
recovery of the stolen objects, and the conviction of the thief, shall be
duly REWARDED.—Cracow, December, 1863.
