METROPOLITAN CLUB.—The noblemen and
gentlemen who have agreed to co-operate in establishing the
above named club are informed that it is intended shortly to commence
business operations. The Metropolitan Club will, to some extent,
remedy the insufficiency of club accommodation, and it will facilitate
intercourse among philanthropists and gentlemen interested in the pro¬
motion of social science. Members will be guaranteed against all
liabilities beyond the annual subscription and the entrance fee, which
for the first 200 members, will amount to three and five guineas respec¬
tively. For particulars apply, by letter, to the Secretary.
The Marquis TOWNSHEND, Chairman of Committee.
HERBERT FRY, Secretary, pro tem.
