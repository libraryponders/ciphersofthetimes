MICHAEL CLARKE, deceased. — Second Notice.—
Whereas Michael Clarke, late of Corley, in the county of War¬
wick, farmer, deceased, by his will, dated the 14th day of August, 1863,
directed his trustees to pay and divide one moiety of his estate unto
and equally among such of the brothers and sisters of his late mother
(whose maiden name was Hannah Huskins) as should be living at the
time of his decease, and the children of such of them as should be then
dead, and should present themselves to his said trustees within three
years from the day of his death, but not otherwise; and whereas the
said Michael Clarke died on the 15th day of October, 1865, and the said
period of three years will expire on the 15th day of October next:;
Now notice is hereby given, that all PERSONS ENTITLED to PAR¬
TICIPATE in the before-mentioned MOIETY of the ESTATE of the
said MICHAEL CLARKE, under the disposition thereof contained in
the said will, are hereby required, on or before the 15th day of October
next, to forward particulars of their claims (stating their relationship
to the mother of the said Michael Clarke) to Messrs. Woodcocks, Twist.
and Son, of Bayley-lane, in the city of Coventry, the solicitors to the
trustees; and notice is hereby also given, that all persons so entitled
who shall neglect or omit to make such claim on or before the said 15th
day of October next, will be excluded from participating in the said
moiety of the said estate ; and that after the said 15th day of October
next the said trustees will proceed to a distribution of the said moiety
of the said estate among the parties entitled thereto, having regard
only to the claims of which they shall then have had notice.—Dated
this 14th day of August, 1868.
WOODCOCKS, TWIST, and SON, Solicitors, Coventry.
