WHEREAS the Commissioners in Lunacy lately
commenced a prosecution against me, the undersigned Thomas
Hookham Silvester, of Tunbridge-wells, in the county of Kent, Doctor of
Medicine, for having received into my house, at Tunbridge-wells afore¬
said, and taken the care and charge of a patient as a lunatic, without
having first obtained, in compliance with the provisions of the Act of the
8th and 9th Victoria, cap. 100, being an Act for the Regulation of the
Care and Treatment of Lunatics, an order of reception and medical cer¬
tificates; and whereas, in consideration of the acknowledgment, and
apology hereinafter set forth, the Commissioners have, under the special
circumstances of my case, consented to stay their prosecution;—Now 1.
THOMAS HOOKHAM SILVESTER, do hereby acknowledge that I
have violated the law, by receiving inte my house and taking the care
and charge of a patient as a lnnatic, without having first obtained the
necessary order and medical certificates, and I do hereby express my
deep concern and regret for having so done, and do thus publicly
APOLOGIZE for the same, and I do solemnly promise not again to
offend in like manner. —Dated this 5th day of March, 186
THOMAS H. SILVESTER.
