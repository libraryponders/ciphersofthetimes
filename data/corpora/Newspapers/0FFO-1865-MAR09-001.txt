PUBLIC FISHERY of the THAMES.—The Com¬
THAMES ANGLING PRESERVATION
SOCIETY APPEAL for increased FUNDS, to enable them to employ
wholly-paid keepers in lien of the present subsidized staff. The receipts
of the Society are now £300. An additional income of £200 is required.
To assist the Society the Board of Thames Conservancy have erected
fish ladders at Teddington and Moulsey weirs. Netting is abolished.
Other improvements are contemplated.
Fish hatching is carried on at Hampton, and thousands of salmon,
trout, and other fish are annually turned into the river.
The Committee appeal to anglers, residents on the banks of the
Thames, and the general public, this Society being the only one which
provides the healthy and gratuitous recreation of angling to thousands
of the working classes of the metropolis.
