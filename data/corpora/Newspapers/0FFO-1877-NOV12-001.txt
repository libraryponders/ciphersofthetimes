TO MANUFACTURERS of IRON GOODS,
Merchants, and others. Whereas we, HENRY ROGERS, SONS,
and Co., carrying on business in London, Wolverhampton, and other
places, have lately ascertained that certain unscrupuious persons have
endeavoured to deceive the trade by effering Axles bearing the name
"Rogers, London " of a very inferior make, but intended to repre¬
sent the Patent Mail Axles supplied by us to the trade, Notice is
hereby given that in future all Axles supplied by the said Henry
Rogers, Sons, and Co., will, as they have hitherto been, be marked in
full, "Henry Rogers, Sons, and Co., London," and as an additional
security will have added the Trade Mark of the said firm, viz, a Fiag
(the Union Jack) surmounting a Crown with the initials "H. R. S. &
Co." underneath, and also a consecutive number on each Axle, for the
purposes of identification. And notice is hereby further given, that
in case any manufacturer or other person shall stamp, impress, or
affix on or to any Axies not being the manulacture or merchandise
of us, the said Henry Rogers, Sons, and Co., our said trade marks, or
any colourable imitation thereof, or in any case any merchant or other
person shall export or sell any such Axies, not being such merchandise
so marked as aforesaid, proceedings will be immediately commenced
against such manufacturer merchant, or other person, to restrain him
or them from such wrongful acts as aforesaid, and to recever damages
in respect thereof. And notice is hereby further given, that crimial
roceedings may be taken in respect of snch wrongful acts.—Henry
Rogers, Sons, and Co., Wolverhampton, 1st November, 1877
