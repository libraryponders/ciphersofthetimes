THE GRESHERNISH OYSTER and MUSSEL
FISHERY.—Notice is hereby given, that the Board of Trade ha
settled and made an ORDER for the ESTABLISHMENT and MAIN¬
TENANCE, by John Robertson, residing at Greshernish, Isle of
t ye, of a SEVERAL or EXCLUSIVE OYSTER and MUSSEL
FISHERY, in Loch Greshernish, Isle of okye, in the county of Inver¬
neas. The fishery grounds are as follows, viz.:—All those parts of the
foreshore and bed of Loon Grosherniso, situate within the parish of
Durinish, Isle of Skye, in the county of Inverness, containing an area
of 167 acres or thereabouts, and bounded as follows, that is to say, on
the west and south by the lands of Greshernish and Coislater, and
partly by the lands of Edinbane, on the east by the said lands of Edm¬
bane, extending northwards from the southern extremity of the loch
to a point on the shore of the loch 6,100 links or thereabouts, and on
the north and north-east by an imaginary line drawn across the loch
as follows, namely, from the point last mentioned 1,000 links or thereby
due west, thence in a north-westerly direction to a point distant about
3,000 links, and thence in more westerly lurection to a point distant
3,000 links or thereby on the western shore of the loch, forming the
north-western extremity of the fishery.
The Order confers on the said Jonn Robertson, and his heirs and
assignees, a right of several oyster and mussel fishery within the limits
above mentioned. It requires that no buildings, erections, ombank¬
ments, or other works shall at any time be commenced or executed
within the limits above described without the previous sanction and
approval in writing of the Board of Trade. It aiso requires the said
John Robertson, and his heirs and aseignees, to render certam accour
to the Board of Tradc, and provides for the inspection of the fishery,
and of the books and documents of the said John Robertson, and his
heirs and assignees, connected therewith.
The frder is to continue in operation for 60 years from its confirma¬
tion by Act of Parl-ament, and no longer.
The Order, which is dated 10th January, 1872, and an Admiralty
chart of the locality, aleng with rolative plan snowing the linits of thn
fishery, can be seen at the Custom-nouses of Kyleakin, stornoway, and
Oban, and also at the office of Donald Mackenze, solicitor, Portree.
Isle of Skye, for one calendar month from the date of this advertise¬
ment, between the hours of 10 in the forenoon and 4 in the afternoon.
free of charge; and copies of the Order may be obtained at the place
last mentioned at the price of 1s. each.
