NORTH-EAST METROPOLITAN AUDIT DIS¬
TRICT.—Poplar Union.—I, the undersigned, Thomas Wrake
Ratcliff, Attorney at-Law, Auditor of the above named district, do
hereby give notice that I have appointed the AUDIT of the AC¬
COUNTS of the POPLAR UNION, and of the parishes therein con¬
tained, for the half-year ended 29th September, 1873, to commence on
Wednesday, the 5th day of November, 1873, at 1 o'clock in the after¬
noon, at the Board Room of the Poplar Workhouse, when and where
all persons who by law are bound to account at such audit are re¬
quired to attend, and to submit all books, documents, bills, and
vouchers, containing or relating to the accounts or to moneys assessed
for, and applicable to the relief of the poor.—Dated the 21st day of
October, 1873. THOS. W. RATCLIFF, District Auditor.
NEW LOCK and WEIR on the THAMES, near
Richmond.—Notice is hereby given, that a PUBLIC MEETING
of the Inhabitants of Richmond, Twickenham, Isleworth, Petersham,
and neighbourhood will be held in the Large Room of the Richmond
National Schools, Eton-street, Richmond, on Thursday, the 23d Octo¬
ber instant, to protest against the serious injury inflicted on the above.
localities by the refusal of the Thames Conservancy to take any steps
to maintain the river in a useful state for boating and commercial
purposes; also to consider what course should be taken in Parliament
or otherwise to remedy the decayed and decaying state of the river and
generally to devise measures for its protection.
The chair will be taken at 8.30 p.m. precisel
