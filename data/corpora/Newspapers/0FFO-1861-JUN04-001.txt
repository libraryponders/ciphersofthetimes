District of Coblenz, on the Rhine.—Particulars of dress in refe¬
rence to the advertissement in The Times, of the 30th inst., received from
Mons. De Rodenberg, the King's Procurator-General:—"Chemise cam¬
bric, the upper hem forming a running string with eyelets, and small and
even cords passing through the centre, in the centre of the chemise and
below the cyelet-holes the initials "A. H., 36," embroidered in Gothic
characters, in relief, half an inch long. 2. A night-gown of fine white
dimity, collar turned down, 2 ft. 3 inches, with white mother of-pearl
buttons; some remains of a fine material, with brown and white stripes
(jaconot musiin), in the white stripe is a small winding white line, with
red spots. In the vicinity of the body have been found the remnants
of a petticoat, 3 ft. 2 inches long, it is composed of fine white dimity,
striped, the same material as the night-gown—on the upper edge, which
is an inch and a half broad, with white riband strings, are embroidered
in white letters, 2 lines, in relief, and in large characters of the Ger¬
man printed alphabet, the initials "M. R. 6." The bottom hem is
finished with a cord in linen thread. The fine quality of the materials
and the elegant make of all those articles indicate that the victim be¬
longed to a rich class. Information to C. F. Field, late Chief of the
Detective Police of the Metropolis.—J. Pollaky, Superintendent foreign
department, Private Inquiry-office, 20, Devereux-court, Temple.
