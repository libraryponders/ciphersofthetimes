ONE HUNDRED POUNDS will be given for such
authentic INFORMATION as will lead to the discovery of a
YOUNG OFFICER, now missing. He was last seen in the main
street of Tenby, S.W., on the night of the 1st of last month (February.
1876), dressed in brown tweed and brown felt hat. He is about 5ft. 6,
age 34 years, noo brown hair, small moustache, and small whiskers,
linen marked J. L. S. He had with him a silver half chronometer,
and probably three of his own cheques on a London banker. He is
supposed to be lying ill and insensible somewhere in brain fever.
Address Captain Brook, Milford-house, Tenby; or Mr. Barker, Surrey
County Police, Guildford.
