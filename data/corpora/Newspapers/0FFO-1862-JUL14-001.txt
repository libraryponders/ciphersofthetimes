NATIONAL MOVEMENT in ITALY.—J. SALE
BARKER, Esq., will deliver TWO LECTURES, at the Marylo¬
bone Literary and Scientific Institution, on "The Rise and Progress
of the National Movement in Italy," on the evenings of Thursday, the
17th and 24th of July. The lecture will commence each evening at
half-past 8 o'clock precisely. Reserved seats, 2s. 6d. ; unreserved seats
and gallery, 1s. The proceeds of the lectures to be devoted to the
Garibaldi Fund. Tickets to be had at Mr. Westerton's library, Knights¬
bridgo; Mr. Effingham Wilson's, Royal Exchange; offices of the Gari¬
baldi Italian Unity Committee, 10, Southampton-street, Strand; and
the Marylebone Institution, 17, Edwards-street, Portman-square.
