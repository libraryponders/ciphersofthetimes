ELECTRIC LIGHT.—Messrs. Wells and Company, Comn
cial Iron and Marble Works, Shoreditch, London, have to inform
their friends and the public that they are temporarily restrained by
injunction from exhibiting the electric light, owing to a dispute as to
patent rights in respect to the Gramme duplox machine. They are,
however, prepared to receive and answer all INQUIRIES as to this
particular system of lighting, and also as to several other systems,
which they are prepared to supply, and which are not subject to any
dispute. —October 18th. 1878.
