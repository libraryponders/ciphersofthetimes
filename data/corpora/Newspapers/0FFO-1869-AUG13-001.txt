BUCCLEUCH MUSSEL FISHERY.—(Frith of
Forth.)—Notice is hereby given, that the Baard of Trade has
settled and made an ORDER for the ESTABLISHMENT and MAIN¬
TENANCE by his Grace Walter Francis, Duke of Bucclench and
Queensberry, of a several or exclusive MUSSEL FISHERY in those
parts of the bed of the Frith of Forth lying in the parishes of Cru¬
mond and North Leith, and county of Edinburgh, containing
avea
of 5.403 imperial acres, or thereabouts, and bounded as follows, viz:—
On the north by a straight line drawn due east and wost through the
Mickrie Stone till it intersects the eastern and western boundaries here¬
inafter mentioned; on the east by a straight line drawn due north
from the east end of the old village of Newhaven till it intersects the
northern boundary hereinbefore mentioned; on the west by a hne
drawn due north from the shore at Cramond, passing along the east
side of Cramond Island, and projected northwards till it interseets the
northern boundary hereinbefore mentioned; and on the south by the
line of low water of ordinary spring tides on the shore of the Frith of
Forth between the eastern and western boundaries above mentioned.
The Order confers on the said Duke of Bucclench and Queens'erry, and
his heirs and successors, a right of several Mussel Fishery within the
limits above mentioned. The Order, which is dated 31st July, 1869,
and an Admiralty chart of the locality, showing the limits of the
fishery, can be seen at the Custom-houses of Leith and Granton, and
also at the rooms of Messrs. Cay and Black, 65, George-street, Edin¬
burgh, for one calendar month from the date of this notice, between
the hours of 10 in the forenoon and 4 in the afternoon, free of charge:
and copies of the Order may be obtained at the place last mentioned,
at the price of 1s. each.—Dated the 11th day of August, 1863.
JOHN GIBSON, Junior, W.S., 12, Charlotte-street, Edin¬
urgh, Agent of the said Duke.
