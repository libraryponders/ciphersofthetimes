THE portion of the PARISH of STEPNEY which
is not dis-membered from the Mother Church comprises up¬
wards of 20,000 souls, and being situate in the east of London, their
poverty from want of employment is notorious. Not a day elapses
but my door is assailed by from 40 to 50 applicants for relief in the
way of food, babylinen, coals, and hospital tickets, which it is im¬
possible for me to relieve but most painful to refuse. I now APPEAL
to a generous public to as -ist me in the existing emergency. A record
will be kept for inspection of the mode in which this assistance has
been distributed, with the names of recipients, and whatever sums may
be sent will be acknowledged in groups in The Times from time to
timne.
R. LEE, M.A., Rector.
Stepney Rectory, Dec. 14th, 1868.
