NYSTERIOUS MURDER. —Whereas, on or about
the 22d of March the BODY of a FEMALE was discovered
under a heap of stones, in a declivity of the Rheinecker Berges, between
Brohl and Niederbreisig-on-the-Rhein, together with various articles
of dress, as advertised in The Times of the 9th inst.; and, whereas,
from further information received it appears that the person so killed
by a gunshot which traversed the breast, lungs, and back, was a
stranger and a traveller. The remains of the clothing being that of a
nightdres, which leads the authorities to the supposition that the person
was murdered in her bed, and afterwards removed to the locality men¬
tioned. Initials on the linen, of the German printed alphabet,—
"M. R., 6," and in Gothic, "A. H., 36." The relatives of any female
missing in this country about the period named, and who has not since
been heard of, will be good enough to send information to Ch. Frede¬
rick Field, late Chief Inspector of the Metropolitan Detective Police;
or to De Rodenberg, the King's Procurator-General in Coblents.—
Ign. Pollaky, Superintendent private inquiry office, 20, Devereux-court,
Temple.
