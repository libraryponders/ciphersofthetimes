IN the HIGH COURT of ADMIRALTY of
ENGLAND.—The DAPHNE and MOULIN.—No. 1,999. — Whereas
in a cause instituted in the High Court of Admiralty of England, on
behalf of John Temperley and others, as plaintiffs, the owners of the
sailing ship or vessel Daphne, against the owners of the ship or vessel
Moulin, and of the cargo lately laden on board thereof, and others,
for the purpose of determining the amount of their, the said plaintiffs'
liability in respect of the loss or damage caused to the said ship or
ressel Moulin, and the goods, merchandise, or other things whatsover
on board thereof, by reason of the improper navigation of the said
vessel Daphne, whereby a collission occurred on the 7th day of August,
1862, between the said vessels, and also for the distribution of the said
amount rateably among the persons entitled to participate therein, the
Judge of the said Court did, on the 5th day of April, 1864, among other
things, pronounce the owners of the said vessel Daphne to be answer¬
able in respect of the loss or damage caused as aforesaid, to the extent
of £5,664, and no more, exclusive of interest, the same being an aggre¬
gate amount equal to £8 for each ton of the registered tonnage of the
said vessel Daphne; This is, therefore, to give notice to all persons
having any CLAIM in respect of the loss or damage caused as aforesaid
to the said vessel Moulin, or to the goods, merchandise, or other things
whatsoever on board thereof, that if they do not come in and enter
their claims in the said cause, on or before the 31st day of May next
ensuing, they will be excluded from sharing in the aforesaiad sum.—
Dated this 14th day of April 1864.
H. A. BATHURST, Assistant Registrar.
