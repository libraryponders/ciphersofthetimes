SEA.-CAUTION.—To Parents and Guardians.—
On the 14th April, 1877, William Beanjoy (alias Brett), a Member
of a Firm styling itself Brett and Company, was proceeded against
before the Lord Mayor for having, witheut the necessary licence from
the Board of Trade, supplied an apprentice for the British Ship Slieve
tloom, in violation of the Provisions of Section 147 of the Mer¬
chant Shipping Act. 1854, and was conticted and fined £5 and costs.
Parents and Guardians are informed that if they wish to send a boy to
sea they should communicate with the Superintendent at the Mercan¬
tile Marine Office at any port in the United Kingdom, or be carsful
to ascerain whether the person (other than the owner or master or
their duly appointed agent) undertaking to find a ship for a boy
possesses a licence from the Board of Trade. By order of the Board

April, 28, 1877.
THOMAS GRAY, an Assistant Secretary.
