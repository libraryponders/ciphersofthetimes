plication having been made to the Governors of the Bank of
England to direct the payment of One Dividend on the sum
£69 Long Annuities, heretofore standing in the name of JANE
FELL, of Tavistock-street, Covent-garden, spinster, deceased, and

same, unless some other claimant shall sooner appear and make out

MILNER GIBSON TESTIMONIAL.—At a
MEETING at Fendall's Hotel, on the 19th of July, in pursu¬
ance of Resolutions moved and seconded by Sir J. V. Shelley, M.P.
Sir Charles Douglas, M.P.; Mr. Cobden, M.P.; Mr. J. White, M.P.;
and Mr. W. N. Ashurst, it was determined to invite a public collection
towards a FUND for a TESTIMONIAL to the Right Hon. T.
MILNER GIBSON, to commemorate the final Abolition of all Taxes
on Literature and the Press.
Chairman of the Committee, Mr. William Ewart, M.P.; Treasurer,
Mr. Robert Chambers; Secretary, Mr. John Francis.
Arrangements are in progress for the formation of local Com¬
mittees in Dublin, Edinburgh, Manchester, Birmingham, Liverpool,
Newcastle-upon-Trne, Ashton-under-Lyne, &c.
Subscriptions, limited to £1, can be sent to Messrs. Drummond and
Co., Charing-cross; or to the Treasurer, Robert Chambers, Esq.,
No. 47, Paternoster-row, London, E.C.; or paid to the Collector, Mr.
Thomas Sanderson..By order of the

JOHN FRANCIS, Hon. Sec.
VEST MIDDLESEX RIFLES.—AMUSKETRY
CLASS, of 12 consecutive mornings, will commence on Monday
next, August 6. Muster at Hanover-gate, Regent's-park, at 6.40 a.m.
A Class will also commence the same day for 12 consecutive evenings.
Muster at Hanover-gate, at 7 p.m.
At the termination of the above classes one other will commence, to
consist of four mornings and four evenings, and the latter class will
conclude the musketry course for the year 1861.
The prize meeting of the Corps will take place early in October. The
prizes will be open for competition to those members only who have
passed through a course of musketry this year.
By order. (Signed) T. W. BRIDGMAN, Captain Instructor.

made his last ascent at the Crystal Palace the great focas of
attraction now are those charming and delightful Gardens at Anerly.
The St. Clond of London, successfully revived by A. Hinton. Palace
return tickets are available at the Anerly Station. The band plays every
Monday, Wednesday, and Saturday evenings, on the platform, from7
till 11. Admission, during those hours, 6d. ; at all other times these
beautiful gardens are open free. The hotel and tavern departments are
complete in their arrangements. Really good dinners, well served,
with good wine, good attention, and moderate charges can always be
ensured at Anerly Tavern, which can be verified by more than 100 trade
dinners, charitable festivals, wedding parties, and pic-nics, who have
patronized this favourite retreat during the last two months. N.B. An
extra night on Tuesday, August the 6th.
