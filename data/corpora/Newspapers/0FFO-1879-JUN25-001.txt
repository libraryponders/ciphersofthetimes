METROPOLITAN DRINKING FOUNTAIN and
CATTLE TROUGH ASSOCIATION, supported entirely by
voluntary contributions.
Offices—Victoria-house, Victoria-street, Westminster.
President—His Grace the Duke of WESTMINSTER, K.G.
Treasurer—Samuel Gurney, Esq., F.R.G.S., &c.
This is the only Society which provides free supplies of water for
man and beast in the streets of London, 86 new fountains and
troughs have been erected during the past 12 months making 786
now at work in London. On the 10th of this month 10,130 human
beings drank at six of the Society's fountains, and 11,285 horses at six
of the Society's troughs in 24 consecutive hours besides oxen, sheep,
and dogs. Funds are urgently needed to enable the Committee to
continue and extend the work, and they earnestly solicit liberal con¬
tributions in support of this simple scheme for the amelioration of
animal suffering and the promotion of habits of temperance amengst
our itierant and working popuiation. CONTRIBUTIONS may be
paid to the Bankers, Messrs. Ransom, Bouverie, and Co.; Messrs.
Barclay, Bevan, and Co. ; or to the General Manager and Secretary,
John Lee, F.R.G.S.
