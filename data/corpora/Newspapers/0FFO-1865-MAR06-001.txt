PUBLIC FISHERY of the THAMES.—The Com¬
ee of the THAMES ANGLING PRESERVATION
SOCIETY APPEAL for increased FUNDS, to enable them to employ
wholly-paid keepers in liou of the present subsidized staff. The receipts
of the Society are now £300. An additional income of £200 is required.
To assist the Society the Board of Thames Conservancy have erected
fish ladders at Teddington and Moulsey weirs. Netting is abolished.
Other improvements are contemplated.
Fish hatching is carried on at Hampton, and thousands of salmon,
trout, and other fish are annually turned into the river.
The Committee appeal to anglers, residents on the banks of the
Thames, and the general public, this Society being the only one which
provides the healthy and gratuitous recreation of angling to thousands
of the working classes of the metropolis.

ALL SAINTS, POPLAR, Middlesex.—In conse¬

quence of the frequent complaints made to the Vestrymen of
the very untidy state of the churchyard of this parish, caused by the
decay of several of the tombs and graves therein, notice is hereby given,
that all persons having an INTEREST in the VAULTS, Tombs, and
Graves in the churchyard of this parish are hereby required by the
said Vestrymen to put such vaults, tombs, and graves in proper repair,
otherwise the vestrymen will, on the expiration of three months from
this date, order the levelling of all such vaults, tombs, and graves as
may at that time remain out of repair.
By order of the Vestrmer.
WILLIAM G. CEELY, Clerk to the Vestrymen.

DUC de MONTEBELLO's (CHAMPAGNES.—
CAUTION.—Whereas an INJUNCTION in Chancery has been
obtained in the cause "Duc de Montebello v. Gemmer," restraining
the defendant and all others from selling or parting with sundry cases
of Rhenish wine, imported from Rotterdam, containing bottles bearing
imitation Duc de Montebelio labels and corks, and further prohibiting
the importation or sale of all wines bearing such imitation labels or
corks; and whereas, in the cause "Duc de Montebello v. Jebens,"tried
in the Tribunal Correctionnel of Havre, a parcel of 250 cases, containing
3,000 bottles of wine, bearing imitation Duc de Montebello labels and
corks, seized by the French Customs' authorities (the said spurious wines
having been seat from Germany and Havre to be thence shipped to
England, and thus, through the colourable importation from a French
port, to avoid detection on arrival here), were adjudged to be confis¬
cated; all persons are hereby cautioned against dealing with spurious
Champagnes bearing imitation Duc de Montebelle labels and corks.
