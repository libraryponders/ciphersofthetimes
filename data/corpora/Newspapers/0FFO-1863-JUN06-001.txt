NASTER of the ROLLS.—Mr. Disraell, fo. 49,
Regr.—Thursday, the 28th of May, 1863. —Between the Risca
Coal and Iron Company and James Edward Coleman, official liqui¬
dator of the said Company, plaintiffs; Evan Herbert Lioyd, Frederick
Wollaston, and Sir Alexander Beaumont Churchill Dixie, Baronet,
defendants.—Upon motion this day made unto the Right Honourable
the Master of the Rolls by counsel for the plaintiffs, who alleged that
the plaintiffs have filed their bill in this Court against the defendants,
which was afterwards amended, and that it appears by the affidavit of
George Henry Sawtell, filed the 27th of May, 1863, that the defendant,
Evan Herbert Lloyd, hath been within the jurisdiction of this Court
within two years next before the filing of the said bill, and that the
said defendant is beyond seas, and that there is just ground to believe
that the said defendant, Eyan Herbert Lloyd, has gone out of the
realm to avoid the consequence of the calls made upon him in con¬
nexion with the liquidation of the said Company, and upon reading
the said affidavit His Honour doth order that the defendant, EVAN
HERBERT LLOYD, do APPEAR to the plaintiffs' bill on or before
the 29th of June next, and the plaintiffs are to cause a copy of this
order, together with a notice thereof to the effect set forth at the foot
of the 10th of the consolidated general orders, rule 6, in that behalf, to
be inserted in the London Gazette, The Times, and Galignani's Messen¬
ger newspapers within 14 days from the date hereof.
Notice.—Evan Horbert Lloyd, take notice, that if you do not ap¬
pear, pursuant to the above order, the plaintiffs may enter an appear¬
ance for you, and the Court may afterwards grant to the plaintiffs such
rellef as they may appear to be entitled to on their own showing.
