DISTRICT MEDICAL OFFICER, St. Pancras,
Middlesex.—The Guardians of the Poor of this Parish will on
Thursday, the 29th August instant, elect for APPOINTMENT a
TEDICAL OFFICER for No. 4 District. Salary £110 per annum.
The medical officer is required to attend at King's-road or elsewhere
as may from time to time be determu od by the Guardians, for one
hour at least daily (Sundays excepted). Drugs and medicines are pro¬
vided by the Guardians, and a dispenser is appointed for the Dis¬
pensary.
Candidates must possess the qualifications prescribed by the Local
Government Board. The officer appointed will be required to reside
within the district and otherwise to conform to the orders of the Local
Government Board. Applications (which must be made on forms pro¬
vided for the purpose, to be obtained at this office), accompanied by
diplomas and other evidences of qualifications, must be sent to me
not later than 10 a.m. on Monday, the 26th August instant. Candi¬
dates are expected to be in attendance at the Vestry-hall at 3.30 p.m.
on the day of election, but no travelling or other expenses will be al¬
lowed. Age not to exceed 45 years.
By order,
DANIEL FILDEW, Clerk to the Guardians.
Guardians offices, Vestry-hall, Pancras-road, N. W.,
6th August, 1878.
