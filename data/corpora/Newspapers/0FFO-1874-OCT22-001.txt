TWENTY POUNDS REWARD.—Trimty-house,
London, 14th October 1874.—Whereas, on Saturday, the 16th of
October instant, at about 10.40 p.m., the weather being moderate, a
ship, apparently American built, of about 1,400 tons burthen, came
into COLLISION with the VARNE LIGHT SHIP,stationed in Dover
Strair, doing very extensive damage to the Light Ship, extinguishing
her light, and endangering the satety of the vessel, the lives of the
crew, and the navigation of the coast by other vessels;
And whereas the name of the ship, or of the master in command
(who promised but did not render assistance), could not be ascertained
by the crew of the Light ship, the above reward will be paid to any
person who will give such information to the Corporation of Trimty¬
house, of London, as to the name of the vesset and her owners, and the
master in command at the time, as may be sufficient to enable them
to institute proceedings againtt the master and owners, or either of
them, under the pravisions of the Merchant Stupping Acts.
Information to be forwarded to the undersigned.
ROEIN ALLEN, Steretary to the Corporation of Trinity¬
houre, London.

other vessels,
