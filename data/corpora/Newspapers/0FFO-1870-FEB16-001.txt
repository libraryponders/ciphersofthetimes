FIFE.— ABERDOUR MUSSEL FISHERY
(FIRTH of FORTH).—Notice is hereby given, that the Board
of Trade has settled and made an Order for the Establishment and
Maintenance by the Right Henourable Sholto John Watson Douglns.
Earl of Morton, of a several or exclusive Mussel Fishery in those parrs
of the foreshore and bed of the Firth of Forth, situate within the
parishes of Aberdour and Burntisland, in the county of Pife, and con¬
taining an area of 4.440 acres, or thereabouts, and bounded as follows:
—On the west by an imaginary line drawn due south from the west
top of the lands of Cuttlehill, called the Old Muir, and passing through
the northernmost point of St. Colm's Abbey, in the Island of Incheolm,
until it interseets an imaginary straight line drawn due west from the

east from the point of intersection above -mentioned to the Mickry
Stone, and thence by another imaginary straignt line drawn from the
Mickry Stone to the nortn-west point of the Island of Inchcolm until
it intersects the eastern boundary hereinafter mentioned; on the east,
emgumsaem¬
bour of Burntisland until it intersects the southern boundary herein¬
before mentioned; and on the north, by the line of high-water of ordi¬
nary spring tides on the shore of the Firth of Forth between the castern
and western boundaries hereinbefore mentioned.
The Order confers on the said Earl of Morton, and his heirs and
successors, a right of several or exclusive mussel fishery within the
limits abovementioned. It also requires that no buildings, erections.
embankments, or other works shall at any time be commenced or ex¬
ecuted within the hmits above described without the previous sauction
and approval in writing of the Board of Trade. It also requires the
said Earl, and his heirs and successors, to render certain accounts to
the Board of Trade, and provides for the inspection of the fishery, and
of the books and documents of the said Earl, and his heirs and suc¬
cessors connected therewth.
The Order is to coutinue in operation for 60 years from its confirma¬
tion by Act of Parlament, and no lenger.
The Order, which is dated 19th October, 1869, and an Admiralty
chart of the locality, showing the limits of the fishery, can be seen
at the Custom-houses of Berntisland and Kirkcaldy, and also at the
rooms of Messrs. Cay and Back, 65, George-street, E.Enburgh, for one
calendar month from the date of this notice, between the hours of 10
in the forenoon and 4 in the afternoon, free of charge; and coples of
the Order may be obtained at the place last mentioned, at the price of
1s. each. —Dated the 26th day of October, 1860.
