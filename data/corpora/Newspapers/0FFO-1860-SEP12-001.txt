and in behalf of CHARLES MELLISOH (who died at Berhampore on
the 19th of April, 1857), cornet in the East Indian Army, son of the
deceased Dietrich Mellisch, of London, formerly a captain of horse in
the English service, viz.:—a. Two Royal Bavarian State Bonds, at 41
per cent, interest, of 1,0004., together 2,0001. ; b. One Royal Bavarian
Stafe Bond, of the Military Loan of 1855, at 4 per cent. interest, of
100f. ; c. One Royal Bavarian State Bond, at 4 per cent. interest
amounting to 100fl.; d. Four bank notes, at 2 per cent, interest, of
the total amount of 315fl. 26k., exclusive of the interest due thereon;
e. In ready-money, 10fl. 28k.: lastly, f. Outstanding dues, but disputed,
stated at 2,000fl. The probable heirs of the above-mentioned Charles
Mellisch are :—1. His mother, Emilie, born Baroness of Baumbach, of
Carlerphe the former widow of Mellisch, and at present the wife of
Frederic Wingfield, Esq., of Graham's-town; and 2. His only sister,
Anny Emily Mellisch, born at Calcutta, in the East Indies, and after¬
wards in a boarding school at Mannheim, on the Rhine. Inasmuch as
no legal claim has been hitherto instituted here to the property above
specined, and as the present abode of the heirs is unknown, this present
public citation is herewith issued, to the effect, that within six
months from the date of his publication, the due and proper claims
thereunto be made, either in person or by proxies duly and legally
anthorized for that purpose ; or, that, in the contrary case, the same
will be considered derelict, and handed over to the Royal Bavarian
Exchequer. The property is provisionally placed in the hands of a
hrustee appointed to that purpose, and the administration thereof
duly provided for.
