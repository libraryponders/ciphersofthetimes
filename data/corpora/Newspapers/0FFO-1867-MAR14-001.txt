Yarmouth, in the county of Norfolk, but afterwards of the
hamlet of Heigham, in the county of the city of Norwich, Esquire, by
his will, dated 28th September, 1864, and proved in the District Regis¬
try of the Court of Probate at Norwich, on the 6th day of March, 1867,
inter alia bequeathed as follows:—"I give and bequeath to my friend
Thomas W. Dufrene, formerly of Brussels, but now or lately residing
at corner of Tenth and Chesnut streets. Philadelphia, the sum of one
hundred and fifty pounds, free from legacy duty, but inasmuch as
am uncertain whether the said Thomas W. Dufrene is living or dead, I
direct my executors hereinafter named to insert an advertisement
twice in The Times London newspaper and twice in one of the Phila¬
delphia newspapers, to be selected by my executors, calling upon the
said Thomas W. Dufrenc to claim his legacy and prove his identity at
an address in London to be mentioned in such advertisement, and I
declare that if the said Thomas W. Dufrene shall not claim his legacy
and prove his identity within the space of twelve calendar months next
after the last of such advertisements shall appear in The Times news¬
paper the legacy shall be forfeited and shall sink into and form part of
my residuary estate hereinafter disposed of." Now, therefore, the
said THOMAS W. DUFRENE is required to take hotice, that unless
he shall CLAIM his LEGACY and PROVE his IDENTITY to the
satisfaction of Robert John Child, of No. 11, Old Jewry-chambers.
London, Esquire, on or before the 4th day of April, 1868, his said
legacy will be forfeited and the same will be paid over by the testator's
executors to the residuary legatees.
