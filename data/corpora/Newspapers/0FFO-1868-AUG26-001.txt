POLICE NOTICE.—Dogs.—30 and 31 Vict., cap¬
134, sec. 18.—By virtue of the Metropolitan Streets Act, 1867, and:
as to "the Metropolis" as defined by the said Act (with the exception
of the city of London), that is to say, as to all parishes and places for
the time being within the jurisdiction of the Metropolitan Board of
Works, I, the Commissioner of the Police of the Metropolis, give
notice, that I do hereby REQUIRE that, from and after the 31st day
of August next (on which day the notice already issued by me will ex¬
pire), until the issuing by me of a further notice, EVERY DOG while
in the streets and not led by some person be MUZZLED in such a
manner as will admit of the animal breathing and drinking without
obstruction.
And further notice is hereby given, that, from and after the said 31st
day of August next, until the issuing by me of such further notice as
aforesaid, any dog found loose in the streets without such muzzle, as
above described, will be taken possession of by the police, and detained
until the owner has claimed it, has provided a proper muzzle, and paid
all expenses connected with such detention. Any dog which has re¬
mained in the possession of the police for three clear days without the
owner claiming the same, and paying all expenses incurred by its de¬
tention, may be caused by me to be sold or destroyed.
as wvitness my hand this 20th day of August, 1868.
RICHARD MAYNE, the Commissioner of Police of the Metropolis.
Metropolitan Police-office, 4, Whitehall-place.
