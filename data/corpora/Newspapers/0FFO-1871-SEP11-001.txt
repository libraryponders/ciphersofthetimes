S. E.—Wish you had written sooner; came on
purpose. Expensive and inconvenient to change now ; think it
unnecessary, but will do as requested, Engaged on 30th; would 23d
or 5th do? Answer as soon as possible. Plans unsettled now; pro¬
ably leave here, but continue to address London; letters will be
forwarded immediately, when absent. Pray write often. Kindest
wishes. Be hopeful. Shall hear again soon.
DEAREST DICK.—I am now perfectly calm, and
you may trust me most implicitly. I have not said a word about
the matter to any one ; why then be so angry? Ill and broken-hearted
as your silence makes me. I have kept all my sorrow to myself. See
death named in advertisement of Sept. 7th. Letters on the subject
wait you. Write kindly and very soon to yours faithfully and
affectionately.

MALMON FISHERY ACTS, 1861, 1865.—Notice is
hereby given, that the Special Commissioners for English Fisheries
will hold a COURT at the Guildhall, Tavistock, on the 24th day of
October next, at 11 o'clock a.m., and at such Court will proceed to in¬
quire into the legality of all fishing weirs, fishing mill dams, and fixed
engines situate in the Tamar Fishery District, and all persons interested
in such engines are required to attend at such Court from day to day
until discharged, in order that a decision may be made by the said
Commissioners with respect to the removal of such fishing weirs, or
lixed engines, or the alteration of such fishing mill dams, or that such
other order may be made by them in the premises as the facts of each
case appear to warrant.
THOS. F. BRADY, Secretary.
Dated at 8, Parliament-street, Westminster, this 24th
day of August, 1871.
