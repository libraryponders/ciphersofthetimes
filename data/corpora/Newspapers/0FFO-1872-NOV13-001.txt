THAMES CONSERVANCY.—Fisheries. — Notice
is hereby given, that the Conservators of the River Thames
propose to make the following BYE-LAW for the REGULATION of
the FISHERIES in the RIVER THAMES, from the City Stone, near
Staines, to Cricktade, so far as relates to ANGLING during a part of
the fence season:—
The Conservators of the River Thames, in exercise of the powers
and authority vested in them by the "Thames Conservancy Acts,
1857 and 1864," and the "Thames Navigation Act, 1866," and of every
other authority them hereunto in anywise enabling, do order and di¬
rect as follows, that is to say:—Notwithstanding the eighth Bye-Law
passed under the seal of the Conservators of the River Thames, the
14th day of June, 1859, and allowed by Her Majesty in Council at the
Court held at Windsor on the 11th day of November, 1869, for regu¬
lating (atnongst other thingst the Fisheries in the River, Thames,
whereby it is proviced shat the fence season in the upper river for pike
and all other river fith, except salmon, salmon trout, and trout, shall
be the period between the 14th of February in each year and the 3ist
day of May following, it shall be lawful to fish for and take by
angling, but not otherwise, pike and other river tish, except saimoh,
saimon trout, and trout, between the 14th of February and the 1st of
March in every year.
Any objection to, or representation respecting the above proposed
Byo-Law shouid be sent to or left with the Secretary of the Conser¬
vators, at No. 41, Trinity-square, Tower-hill, on or before the 14th day
of December next.
