PHILIP THOMAS, deceased.—Hen-at Law and
Next of Kin Wanted.—The CHILD or CHILDREN, or other
Descendants, or the Heir-at Law of PHILIP THOMAS, one of the
sons of Daniel Thomas, formerly of Colby Mill, in the parish of Wiston,
in the county of Pembroke, miller, deceased, and brother of William
Thomas, of Pembroke Dock, in the said county of Pembroke, foreman
of the Royal Dockyard at that place, deceased, who was the father of
William Thomas, late of No. 8, Queen-street west, Pembroke Dock
aforesaid, surgeon (who died on the 25th December, 1862), may HEAR
of SOMETHING to his or their ADVANTAGE by applying to either
of us, the undersigned. Any person giving information as to the said
child or children, or other descendants, or the heir-at-law will be libe¬
rally rewarded for his trouble. The said Philip Thomas would, if he
were now living, be about 100 years old. At the age of 17 years or
thereabouts he left Colby Mill, and went to Picton Castle, Pembroke¬
shire, where he was employed in the stables of the first Lord Milford.
In the course of a year or two afterwards he was taken by Lord Milford
to London, and remained for some time in his service. He married a
widow, and while still young died in London. At the death of the said
Philip Thomas there were living two sons of his wife, but it is not
known whether they were the issue of that or of her previous mar¬
riage. Nothing has been heard of the widow of the said Philip Thomas,
or of her said sons, for the last 60 years or thereabouts. — Dated the 14th
day of December 1863.
WILLIAM PHILLIPS, 4, Sise-lane, London, Agent for George
Parry, of Pembroke Dock, Pembrokeshire, Solicitor for James
Hancock, the Administrator of the said William Thomas,
surgeon, deceased.
