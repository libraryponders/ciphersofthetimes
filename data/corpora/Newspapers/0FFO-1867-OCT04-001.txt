METROPOLITAN BOARD of WORKS.—Metro¬
polis Improvement Act, 1863, 26th and 27th Vic¬ cap. 45.—
Notice is hereby given, that in accordance with the provisions of the
abovenamed Act of Parliament, the REMAINS of all PERSONS IN¬
TERRED in the CHURCHYARD of ST. ANDREW-BY-THE¬
WARDROBE, St. Benet, Paul's-wharf, St. Mary Magdalen, St.
Nicholas Cole Abbey, and St. Nicholas Olave, in the city of London,
and the Memorial Tablets or Monuments erected therein, will
shortly be REMOVED. The heirs, executors, administrators, or
friends of any persons interred in the said churchyards will
have the option of removing their remains, as well as any
tablets or monuments erected to their memory, under proper
supervision, to any other churchyard or consecrated burial-ground,
provided that the expense of a single removal shall in no case
exceed £10; and that notice of such intended removals, and the
burial certificates of the person or persons to be removed, be addressed
to Mr. John Pollard, Clerk of the Metropolitan Board of Works, at
their office, Spring-gardens, on or before the 31st day of October, 1867,
in which case the expense of such removals will be borne by the
Metropolitan Board of Works. All human remains, as well as
all memorial tablets or monuments, concerning the removal of which
no notice as above described have been received on or before the 31st
day of October, 1867, will forthwith be carefully removed, under proper
supervision, to the Great Northern London Cemetery, in the parish of
East Barnet, county of Hertford, and there re-interred, and the monu¬
ments re-erected by the Metropolitan Board of Works.
By order, JOHN POLLARD, Clerk of the Board.
Office of the Board, Spring-gardens, S.W., Sept. 30, 1867.
