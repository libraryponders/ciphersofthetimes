the ESTABLISHMENT and MAINTENANCE by the OYSTER
MERCHANTS' COMPANY (Limited), of a several OYSTER
and MUSSEL FISHERY at EMSWORTH, in the Estuary of the
Emsworth Channel, in the counties of Sussex and Southampton.
1. The Cyster Merchants' Company (Limited), in this Order called
the Company, shall be the undertakers of the fishery mentioned in

2. The following are the Company's fishery grounds under this Order
(as shown on plans deposited at the Board of Trade and marked "Ems¬
worth Fishery Order, 1870,") namely, all those parts of the bed of the
Emsworth Channel lying below low-water mark at ordinary neap tides.
being situate within the parishes of Hayling North, in the county of
Southampton, and West Thorney, in the county of Sussex, or one of
them, containing an area of 45 acres or thereabouts, and bounded as
follows:—On the north by an imaginary line drawn from Wicor or
Vicar Point due west to Swear Deep., Torr, and on the south by
another imaginary line drawn from the northern bank of Upper
Markers Rythe, at the point where it debouches into the said Ems¬
worth Channel, to the northern bank of Tiobs Rythe on the west side
of the same chanuel.
3. This Order confers on the Company a right of several oyster and

4.The limits of the said several fishery shall be marked out by per¬
pendicular booms or poles, placed on the line of low water of ordinary
neap tides, at intervals of not more than 100 yards, or in such other
manner as the Board of Trade may direct.
5. In the event of the marks mentioned in the last foregoing article
being obliterated by storm or otherwise, they shall be replaced by the
Company, and notice of the said limits may be given to fishermen,
dredgermen, and other persons, as follows: that is to say,
1 By notice boards or placards placed in conspicuous places at the
entrances of limsworth Channel and Chichester Harbour, at Emsworth.
Havant, and Hayling-bridge.
(2 By advertisements in one or more local newspapers, and in the
London Siuppinng and Mercantile Gazette.
6. No builamas, erections, embankments, or other works, other than
the marks mentioned in Clause 4 of this Order, shall at any time be
commencel or executed within the limits above described, without the
previous sanction and approval in writing of the Board of Trade.
7. The Company shall render to the Board of Trade such accounts of
their capital, expenditure, and income, and of all oysters and mussels
sold by the Company, in such form and at such times as the Board of
Trade may require; and shall allow the Board of Trade, or any person
appounted by the Board of Trade, to inspect the fishery and all books
and documents in their possession relating thereto, and shall give to
such Board or person all such infermation relating thereto as they or
he may require.

9. This Order shall not be taken as a consent to the surrender of any
rights, interests, powers, authorities, or privileges transfeered to the
management of the Board of Trade by "The Crown Lands Act,
1866.
10. This Order may be cited as "The Emsworth Fishery Order,

1870."
(Signed) C. CECIL TREVOR, an Assistant-Secretary to the
Board of Trade, 26th July, 1863.
