PERKIN's PATENT MAUVE DYE.—86, St.
Vincent-street, Glasgow, January 24. 1863.—Dear Sirs, —Refer¬
ing to your complaint that the mauve paste lately sold by us has been
ascertained by scientific analysis to be an infringement of the patent
granted to your Mr. Wm. Henry Perkin, on the 26th day of August,
1856, we beg to say that the paste sold by us has not been of our own
manufacture, or sold by us as such, but has been supplied to us by
Messrs. Petersen and Sichler, of St. Denis, as their agents who gave us
their written assurance that the paste did not infringe your patent.
Since learning from you that you were prepared to prove the infringe¬
ment, we have again communicated with Messrs. Petersen and Sichler
offering them the opportunity of trying the question of infringement
at their own expense. This offer they have not accepted, and we have
therefore agreed to pay you the sum of £245 8s. 4d., being the amount
agreed to be accepted by you as in full of your claims against us in con¬
sequence of our having acted as agents for the said Petersen and Sichler
in the sale of the said paste. We also readily give you our undertaking
not to deal further in the article in question, and we have handed over
to you all the paste we had on hand.
We remain gentlemen, yours obediently
JAS. DALE and Co.
Messrs. G. T. Perkin and Sons, Greenford-green, Sudbury, Middlesex.

BROAD-STREET WARD.—25th February, 1863.
—At a COURT of WARDMOTE, held this day at the Ward
School-house, London-wall, before the Right Worshipful Sir JOHN
MUSGROVE, Bart., Alderman of the Ward,
Mr. Davis Sims was unanimously elected a Common Councilman of
this Ward for the remainder of the year, in the room of Sydney Hedley
Waterlow, Esq., now Alderman of the Ward of Langbourn.
Resolved unanimously, That this Wardmote desires to express to
Sydney Hedley Waterlow, Esq., its best thanks for the zeal, ability,
and liberality with which he has for upwards of seven years faithfully
served this Ward as one of the representatives in the Court of Common
Council, and to assure him, on his elevation to the honourable position
of Alderman of the Ward of Langhourn, he carries with him from this
Ward the highest respect and esteem of its inhabitants, with their best
wishes for his health and happiness: that the same be fairly tran¬
scribed, signed by the Ward Clerk, and presented to Mr. Alderman
Waterlow.
That the best thanks of this Wardmote be given to the Right Worship¬
ful Sir John Musgrove, Bart., Alderman of the Ward, for his able and
impartial conductin presiding over the proceedings of this Wardmote.
That the foregoing resolutions be advertised in The Times, Morning
Advertiser, Daily Telegraph, Standard, and City Press.
DAVID STUART, Ward Clerk.
