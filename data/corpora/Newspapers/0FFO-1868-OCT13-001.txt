IN the HIGH COURT of JUDICATURE at
BOMBAY Ordinary Original Civil Jurisdiction.—In the Matter
of the Indian Companies Act, and in the Matter of the ORIENTAL
INSURANCE SOCIETY in LIQUIDATION.—Upon hearing
Mr. Peile, attorney for Bhimice Pursholum Cooverjee Rustomjee
Mody and Cursetice Stormusice Chinoy, the Liquidaters of the
Oriental Insurance Company, and upon reading the affidavit of
Cursetjee Stormusice Chinoy, one of such liquidators, sworn in this
matter on the 10th day of July instant, and annexed hereto, I do
order and direct that all persons having CLAIMS upon the said
ORIENTAL INSURANCE SOCIETY do send in statements of such
claims, in writing, to the said Liquidators, at their office, Victoria¬
buildings, Parsee, Bazar-street, Bombay, being the registered office of
the Oriental Insurance Society in Bombay, on or before the 1st day of
December next, after which time the said Liquidators shall be at
liberty to adjust and eatisfy the claims then sent in; and further.
that after all such claims so sent in shall have been so adjusted and
satisfied the said Liquidators shall be at liberty to distribute the
surplus assets (if any) of the said Oriental Insurance Society among
the shareholders thereof; and I do further direct that this, my
order, shall be advertised in London in the London Gazette and
Times newspapers, and shall also be advertised in the Bombay Go¬
vernment Gazette and in two English and two native newspapers in
Bombay, and in onc public newspaper at each of the following places
—viz, Calcutta and Hongkong. —Dated this 10th day of Scot. 1868.
Signed. JOSEPH ARNOULD.
Signed, HEARN, CLEVELAND, and PEILE, Attorneys for
the Liquidators of the Oriental Insurance Society
