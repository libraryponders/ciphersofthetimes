CHARITY COMMISSION.—In the Matter of the
CHARITY called The SCHOOL for BOYS situate in the parish
of ERITH in the County of Kent.—By the direction of the Board of
Charity Commissioners for England and Wales notice is hereby given
that an ORDER is proposed to be made by them after the expiration
of one calendar month to be computed from the first publication of
this notice having the following objects namely
Removing and discharging Frances Selena Culling Hanbury Widow
Robert Hanbury Esquire The Honorable and Reverend William Henry
Fremantic Henry Prederick Bowker Esquire and Arthur George
Burrows Esquire with their respective assents from the Office of Go¬
vernor of the above mentioned Charity,
Vesting the legal estate in the premises now or lately occupied for
the purposes of the Boys School in the Official Trustee of Charity
Lands and his successors In trust for the said Charity and Establish¬
ing a scheme in variation of the subsisting scheme for the administra¬
tion of the said Charity.
A scheme has been proposed providing for the appointment and suc¬
cession of Governors of the said Charity and That the said
vernors
shall be at liberty with the sanction of the said Board to effect the sale
of the School buildings and their site and appurtenances belonging to
the said Charity and which are no longer required for their original
purpose to the School Board of Erith with the intent that the pur¬
chase money to arise from such sale may be invested and the dividends
thereon applied in the payment of Scholarships for deserving boye of
the Middle and Lower Classes attending any well conducted Public
Elementary School at Erith.
Any objections to the proposed Order or suggestions for its modid.
cation may be transmitted is writing to the Secretary of the said Com¬
missioners No. 8 York Street St. James's Square London within 21
days next after the first publication of this notice.
Dated this second day of April 1878.
HENRY M. VANE Secretary.
