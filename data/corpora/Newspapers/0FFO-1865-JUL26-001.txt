BREECH-LOADING ARMS.—Memorandum in
explanation of the advertisement "To Gunmakers and others,"
June 21, 1865.
Length.—I. By length of barrel 39" it is to be understood that the
two following lengths are to be the same as those of the service Enfield
rifle with the 39" barrel.
(1.) Length from butt plate to trigger.
(2.) Length from butt plate to muzzle.
Ramrod.—II. Every arm will be required to have a ramrod, but the
ramrod is not to be included in the fittings referred to in Section 8. It
will be used chiefly as a cleaning rod, as expressed in Section 2, No. 6,
and may be lighter than the service ramrod.
Weight.—III. The limits of weight for the barrel, viz. 4 1bs. to 5 1bs.,
are intended to include any part of the breech mechauism which is
attached to, and detachable with, the barrel.
Bayonet.—IV. Competitors are not required to supply bayonets with
the arms, but the arms are to be prepared to take a bayonet of the ser¬
ice pattern, except in respect to the size of the socket, and they must
he fixed in the same way.

No. 8. Tallow, or any other animal fat, is inadmissible.
VI. There will be no objection to any competitor bringing forward
two descriptions of ammunition, but no more.
