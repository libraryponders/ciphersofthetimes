Matter of Mrs. MARGARET CAMPBELL, deceased. —Whereas,
Margaret Campbell, of Bligh-Street, Sydney, in the colony of New
South Wales, widow and relict of Robert Campbell, late of the same
place, Esquire, deceased, made her last will at Sydney aforesaid, dated the
28th December, 1868, and left the said colony for England in the be¬
ginning of the year 1859, and afterwards arrived there and resided for
a short time at Richmond, in the county of Surrey, and afterwards at
the Hotel Bristol, and at No. 4. Rue d'Astoy, Paris, and died at the
Hotel Chatham, Paris, on the 25th day of April, 1864. It is not known
whether the said Margaret Campbell made any codicil to her said will
or not after leaving the said colony. The executors named in the will
hereby offer to any solicitor, proctor, notary, or other person who may
have made any CODICIL to her said WILL, upon its being handed to
her executors, or giving satisfactory information relative thereto, such
a sum as will fully and liberally reimburse them for all their expenses
and trouble in the business. Information to be forwarded to Messrs.
Wilde, Rees, Humphry, and Wilde, 21, College-hill, London, solicitors
for the executors.
