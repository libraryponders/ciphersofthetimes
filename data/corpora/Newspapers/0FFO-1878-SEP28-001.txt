THE Worshipful COMPANY of TURNERS of
London, according to their custom, invite COMPETITION for
PRIZES this year in HAND TURNING in HARD and SOFT
WOOD, Pottery, and Diamond Cutting and Polishing. The silver
medal and the freedom of the Company will be the first prize in the
wood and diamond cutting and polishing competition; the freedom of
the Company and a sum of money will be given for the best piece of
bottery "thrown;"and silver medals for the best article of pottery
thrown and turned," and "turned or shaved." In addition, the
Company will (subject to the consent of the Court of Aldermen) ob¬
tain the freedom of the City of London for those competitors who
gain the freedom of the Company. Bronze medals and certificates of
merit, as well as sums of money, will be awarded in the diseretion of
the judges, according to gradations of merit.
All specimens in wood and pottery must be delivered free at the
Mansion-house, and those in the diamond competition at Messrs.
Hunt and Roskell's, 156, New Bond-street, marked "Turner's Com¬
pany," during the week ending October 5th.
The specimens will be on view at the Mansion-house on the follow¬
ing days, October 6th, 9th, and 10th, from 11 to 4. Admission by
ticket only, to be obtained from the Hon. Secretary; and the prizes
will be distributed by the Right Hon. the Lord Mayor at the Mansion¬
house, on Thursday, October 10th, at 11.30 a.m.
Further particulars can be optained on application to the Hon. Sec¬
retary, R. L. Loveland, 4, Hare-court, Temple.
