NOTICE.—SUPREME RESOLUTION of the
PERUVIAN GOVERNMENT about the BORAX of IQUIQUE.
—Lima, 28th May, 1874.—In view of the document, and having
conched on the 12th inst, the contract of consignation ce'ebrated
between the Government and Don Federico Frerant, for the exporta¬
tion of Borax or Borate of Lime to Europe, which is produced in the
province of Tarapaca, and having in consideration that this system
has produced exignous resul's to the Public Treasury, and that it is
necessary that the Government protects the industry as far as the
Faculties permit—declares that the Borax or Borate of Lime, which
is produced in the province of Tarapaca, or any other place in Pern,
is a free export, subject only to the payment of 10 soles per metrical
ton of 1,000 kilogrammes, for duties of exportation, which will be paid
at the Custom-house of Iquique until the Government gives another
order, remaining bound to this disposition the 10,000 quintals that the
ex-contractor Frerant has accumulated.
Signed by the President,
JOSE M. TIRADO.
Published by the Piscal Inspection of Pern in Europt; L. M.
Barrera, Fiscal Inspector, 58, Rue Monceau, Paris; and at the Peru¬
vian Consulate-General in London, 18, Bedford-square.
