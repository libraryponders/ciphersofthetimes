London (Golden-lane and Petticoat-square, &c.) Improvement Scheme
Confirmation Act, 1877.—The Commissioners of Sewers of the City
of London do hereby give notice, that the final award of Sir Henry
Arthur Hunt, in the matter of the above-mentioned Act and Scheme,
has been deposited at the Home Office, and that a copy thereof has
also been deposited at the Sewers office, Guildhall.
All persons CLAIMING to have any RIGHT to or INTEREST in
the LANDS, or any of the lands, compensation for which is ascer¬
tained by the said award, must deliver to the) Solicitor to the Com¬
missioners of Sewers of the City of London, Mr. A. J. Baylis, Church¬
court-chambers, Old Jewry, on or before the 1st day of October,
1878, a succinct statement in writing of the nature of such claim,
together with a short abstract of the title on which the claim is
founded.
And further that any abstract in the case of a person claiming a
fee simple interest in the land must commence 20 years previous to
the date of the claim, unless an absolute conveyance of the land shall
have been made within 20 years and more than 10 years prior to such
date, in which case the abstract will commence with the conveyance.
The Commissioners will pay all reasonable costs incurred with re¬
spect to every such statement and abstract.
Dated this 22d day of August, 1878.
HENRY BLAKE, Clerk to the Commissioners of Sewers
