Resolved unanimously.—That the thanks of this Common Hall are
due, and that the same be now tendered to the Right Honourable Sir
Thomas White, Knight, Lord Mayor, for the exceedingly satisfactory
manner in which he has met all requirements of his office, and ful¬
filled the important functions appertaining to the Mayoralty.
The Common Hall fully recognises his courteous and efficient presi¬
dency when occasion has called the Livery together in the exercise of
their rights and privileges. This Common Hall also records its high
appreciation of his Lordship's conduct at all times, alike in the ad¬
ministration of justice, the support of the cause of charity, and the
dispensing of hospitality. Especially would this Common Hall con¬
gratulate the Lord Mayor upon the remarkable growth, day by day, of
the voluntary fund inaugurated at the Mansion-house for the relief of
the sufferers by famine in India, a result which must form one of the
pleasantest associations of his year of office.
Resolved unanimously—That the best thanks of the Livery, in
Common Hall assembled, are due, and are hereby presented to Simeon
Charles Hadley, Esquire, Alderman, and William Quartermaine East,
Esq., for the able and efficient manner in which they have during the
past year fulfilled the arduous and important duties which appertain
to the office of Sheriffs of London and Sheriff of Middlesex, and for
the energy, zeal, and courtesy displayed by them, not only in the
discharge of those duties, but in upholding the honour and dignity of
the office, and the rights and privileges of the citizens of London.
