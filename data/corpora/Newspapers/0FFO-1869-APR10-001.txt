MARGARET WATSON, deceased. —Notice.—
Whereas Margaret Watson, late of High-hill, in the parish of
Scaleby, in the county of Cumberland, spinster, deceased (who died on
the 1st day of April, 1869), by her will did devise and bequeath certain
real and personal estate upon trust for her brother, James Watson (of
whom she states in her said will that she had had no tidings for up¬
wards of 22 years), in case he should at any time before or within 12
calendar months after her decease appear in England and personally
make claim before, and establish his identity to the full satisfaction of,
the trustees of her said will; Notice is hereby given to the said
JAMES WATSON (who was formerly of Carlisle, in the county of
Cumberland, grocer), before or within 12 calendar months from the
said 1st day of April, 1869, to appear in England and personally make
claim before and establish his identity to, the full satisfaction of
William Atkinson, of Aglionby, in the said county of Cumberland,
yeoman; Fergus Watson, of Great Corby, in the same county, yeo¬
man; and John Sutton, of Woodhead, in the same county, yeoman,
the trustees named in the said will of the said Margaret Watson, de¬
ceased. —Dated the 6th day of April, 1869.
HENRY DOBINSON, Solicitor to the said Trustees.
