POLICE PERJURY CASE.—At a Public Meeting
held in the St. Pancras Vestry-hall on the 8th February last, H.
J. Slack, Esq., in the chair, the following resolution was carried una¬
nimously:—"That the thanks of this Meeting are due and presented
to Mr. John Ivory for his firmness and perseverance in the prosecution
of this case, and that the generous manner in which he took the whole
expense upon himself is deserving of the highest commendation, and
should be recognized by the public: and, with this view, that a Com¬
mittee be appointed to collect SUBSCRIPTIONS to INDEMNIFY
Mr. IVORY in the amount of his expenditure." The Committee ap¬
pointed by the above resolution have to acknowledge the receipt of
£128 19s. 6d. already subscribed, still leaving a balance of £121 0s. 6d.
due to Mr. Ivory. Subscriptions will be thankfully received by W.
Shaen, Esq., Treasurer, 8, Bedford-row, W.C.; James Beal, Esq.,
Chairman, 209, Piccadilly; Mr. J. Kenny, Hon. Sec., 275, Euston¬
road; or Mr. John Ivory, 53, College-place, Camden-town.

CLICQUOT's CHAMPAGNE.—Caution to the
Wine Trade.—Whereas circulars have recently been issued to the
wine trade in Scotland purporting to be signed by Edmund Brace and
Co., of 21, Quality-street, Leith, and 9, Exchange-square, Glasgow, in
which the said Edmund Brace and Co. describe themselves as agents
for "Clicquot's" Champagne, and state that they have lying in Glas¬
gow a parcel of "Clicquot," which they offer for sale at 50s. in bond
(copies of which circulars are in my possession), Notice is hereby given,
that the said Edmund Brace and Co. have no connexion, either as
agents, or in any way whatever, with Messrs. Werlé and Co. (succes¬
sors of V. Clicquot, Ponsardin), of Rheims, the sole proprietors of the
brand, and producers of the wine known in this country as Madame or
Widow Clicquot, and that no genuine wine of that brand can be fairly
sold in this country at the price quoted; and the trade are hereby
CAUTIONED against attempts to mislead, and are requested to give
information respecting the sale of any wine purporting to be "Clic¬
quot," in bottles, whereof the corks are not branded "V. Clicquot, P–
Werlé"—or which bear a brand fraudulently imitating, or only colour¬
ably differing from that brand, to me, at my office in London; to
Messrs. Symington, Miller, and Stewart, 119, St. Vincent-street, Glas¬
gew (my sub-agents for Scotland and the north of Ireland): or to J.
Wilson Nicholson, Esq., solicitor, 48, Lime-street, London, E.C.
S. B. J. SELBY, 124, Fenchurch-Street, E.C., Sole Agent for
Messrs. Werlé and Co., successors of V. Clicquot, Ponsar¬
din, for the United Kingdom, and all its Dependencies and
Colonies except Canada.
Witness to Mr. Selby's Signature.—J. Wilson Nicholson.
