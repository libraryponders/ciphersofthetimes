DUC de MONTEBELLO's CHAMPAGNES.—
CAUTION.—Whereas an INJUNCTION in Chancery has-been
obtained in the cause "Duc de Montebello v. Gemmer," restraining
the defendant and all others from selling or parting with sundry cases
of Rhenish wine, imported from Rotterdam, containing bottles bearing
imitation Duc de Montebello labels and corks, and further prohibiting
the importation or sale of all wines bearing such imitation labels or
corks; and whereas, in the cause "Due de Montebello v. Jebens," tried
in the Tribunal Correctionnel of Havre, a parcel of 250 cases, contain¬
ing 3,0 bottles of wine, bearing imitation Due de Montebello labels and
corks, seized by the French Customs authorities (the said spurious wines
having been sent from Germany and Havre to be thence shipped to
England, and thus, through the colourable importation from a French
port, to avoid detection on arrival here), were adjudged to be confis¬
cated; all persons are hereby cautioned against dealing with spurious
Champagnes bearing imitation Duc de Montebello labels and corks.
TAKLIN and TAYLER, 156, Fenchurch-street, E.C.,
Solicitors to the Plaintiff.
R. SYMONDS and SON, 3, Ingram-court, 167, 168, Fen¬
church-street, E.C., Agents to the Plaintiff.

MOP TRADE.—SEVENTY POUNDS REWARD.
—Whereas several persons and firms have been selling inferior
English hops as a superior description of East Kent hops, and inferior
foreign hops as a superior description of Bavarian hops, and whereas
the said persons and firms have unlawfully and fraudulently applied,
or caused to be applied, to the wruppers of the said hops trade marks
which they knew to be forged, and counterfeited trade marks, or a
false description of the place where the said hops have been produced,
notice is hereby given that a reward of £50 will be paid to any person
giving such INFORMATION as shall lead to a conviction, under the
sections of the Merchandise Marks Act of 1862, which declares it to be
a misdemeanour to forge a trade mark, or falsely to apply a trade
mark, whether genuine or forged, to an inferior article, or falsely to
apply a trade mark, whether genuine or forged, to any case, wrapper,
&c., in which an inferior article is intended to be sold, punishable by
the criminal courts, on indictment, with fine and imprisonment; and
notice is hereby also given, that a reward of £20 will be paid to any
person giving such information as shall lead to a conviction under the
sections of the aforsaid statute, which empower Justices of the Peace
to impose a penalty equal to the value of the article, and £5 in addi¬
tion, upon any person who shall sell, utter, or expose for sale, or for
any purpose of trade, any article with a forged trade mark, or with a
genuine trade mark used falsely and wrongfully, or with such forged
and false trade marks on the wrappers covering the same, &c., which
empower the said Justices to impose a penalty of £5 upon any person
who shall sell, utter, or expose any article upon which, to his know¬
ledge, has been put a false description of the place where it has been
produced. Apply to Mr. Anthony Carr, solicitor, 25, Rood-lane, Lon¬
don, E.C.
