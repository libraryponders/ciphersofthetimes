CAUTION.—To Bankers, Bill Brokers, and others.
—Whereas BILLS of EXCHANGE or PROMISSORY NOTES
been and may be put into circulation by Mr. Daniel Pearson,
purporting to be accepted or made by Messrs. Lloyd, Pearson, and
Lloyds, or the Madeley Coal and Iron Company of Madeley, in Stafford¬
shire; and whereas the partnership between Messrs. Lloyds and Mr.
Daniel Pearson has been duly dissolved, and an injunction has been

from drawing, accepting, endorsing, giving, or negotiating any bill of
exchange or promissory note in the name of the Madeley Coal and
Iron Company, or in the name of Lloyd, Pearson, and Lloyds, or in
any other name whereby the Messrs. Lloyds might be rendered liable
all persons are therefore cantioned against dealing with any such bills
of exchange or promissory notes; and it is requested that information
of any attempts to negotiate the same may be immediately given to
Messrs. R. and S. Mullens, solicitors, 7, Poultry, London, E.C.—
London, 18th March, 1863.

ST. STEPHEN, Coleman-street.—At a GENERAL
VESTRY of the above parish, very numerously attended, holden
in the Vestry Room of the Church, on Tuesday, 7th April, 1863,
JOHN WEBSTER, Esq., Senior Churchwarden, in the chair,
It was unanimously resolved, That this Vestry regards with con¬
siderable apprehension the attempt on the part of the Government to
unite the city police with the metropolitan police force, thereby tending
very much to deprive the citizens of a long held and highly cherished
right of self-government. By request of the Vestry,
THOS. JONES, Vestry Clerk.
No. 1, King's Arms-yard, Coleman-street.
