NOTICE and CAUTION to the NOBILITY
Gentry, and Public generally.—Whereas, the GUNMAKERS'
COMPANY of London have reason to believe that many guns and
pistols, principally of foreign manufacture, the barrels of which have
never been proved, as with a view to the safety of the public is re¬
quired by "The Gun Barrel Proof Act, 1855," and that such unproved
small arms are frequently used by sportsmen, in ignorance of the fact
that they have not been submitted to the test of soundness and safety,
an omission which experience has proved may at any moment endanger
the lives of those who use them; All persons are therefore requested
to take notice that such unproved small arms are liable to seizure
under the above Act, and are also CAUTIONED against selling, pur¬
chasing, or possessing any guns, pistols, or othersmall arms, the barrels
of which have not been duly proved and marked as proved as directed
by the said Act.
By order of the Mgast.
ardens, and Assistants,
GEO. RUTHERFORD, Clerk of the Gun¬
makers Company.

TO the ROYAL MALL STEAM PACKET COM¬
PANY, 55, Moorgate-street.—Gentlemen,—We beg to express
our regret that in the month of April last we caused to be shipped on
board your steamship Seine a package of drugs, addressed to Dr.
Sealey, Barbadoes, which contained within it a box with two bottles of
muriatic and nitric acid, and that we did not give, at the time of
despatching the package, notice that its contents were of a dangerous
character, as required by the provisions of the Merchant Shipping Act,
and we beg to APOLOGIZE for the neglect. We are now aware that
such a package is dangerous if shipped with other cargo,
You having intimated to us that we are liable for penalties for ship¬
ping acids without giving notice of the fact that the package contained
them, we have to thank you for waiving proceedings against us upon
our making this apology, which you may use in such manner as you
may think right.
are, Gentlemen, your obedient servants,
