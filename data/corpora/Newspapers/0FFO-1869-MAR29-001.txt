WHEREAS the Commissioners in Lunacy have inti¬
mated their intention to institute proceedings against me, the
undersigned James Morant, of Douglas-villa, Waverley-road, Southsea,
in the county of Hants, house decorator, for having received into my
house, at Southsea aforesaid, and taken the care and charge of a
lunatic without having first obtained, in compliance with the provisions
of the Act of the 8th and 9th Vict., cap. 100, being an Act for the Regula¬
tion of the Care and Treatment of Lunatics, an order of reception and
medical certificates; and wherea in consideration of the acknowledg¬
ment and apology hereinafter set forth the said Commissioners have
under the special circumstances of my case consented to stay the pro¬
secution;—Now I, the said James Morant, do hereby ACKNOWLEDGE
that 1 have VIOLATED the LAW by RECEIVING into my house
and TAKING the CARE and CHARGE of a LUNATIC without hav¬
ing first obtained the necessary order and medical certificates; and I
do hereby express my deep concern and regret for having so done, and
do thus publicly apologize for the same, and I do solemnly promise not
again to offend in like manner. —Dated this 27th day of March, 1869.
