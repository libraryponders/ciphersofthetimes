THAMES CONSERVANCY.—Pursuant to the
Thames Conservancy Act. 1864, section 31, notice is hereby
given, that the Conservators of the River Thames, in exercise of the
powers vested in them by the Thames Conservancy Acts, 1857 and
1864, propose to make the following BYE LAWS::
I. Every steam dredger moored in the River Thames shall between
sunset and sunrise exhibit three bright light, from globular lanterns of
not less than eight inches in diameter, the said three lights to be placed
in a triangular form and to be of sufficient power to be distinctly viiable
with a clear atmosphere on a dark night at a distance of at least one
mile, and to be placed not less than six feet apart on the highest part
of the frame work athwart ships.
2. Any person committing any breach or in any way infringing this
bye law, or the bye laws passed under the Corporate Seal of the Con¬
servators of the River Thames, the 18th day of April, 1864, shall be
liable to a penalty of, and shall forfeit a sum not exceeding, £5, which
said penalty shall be recovered, enforced, and applied according to the
provisions of the said Thames Conservancy Acts.
All persons desiring to make objections to, or representations re¬
specting the proposed bye laws, must make such objections or re¬
presentations, in writing or otherwise, on or before the 20th day of
May next, and a special meeting of the said Conservators will be held
at their office, No. 41, Trinity-square, Tower-hill, at 10.30 o'clock in the
forenoon, on the 10th day of May next, to afford persons an oppor¬
tunity of personally making to them any objection or representation
respecting the proposed bye laws. —Dated the 6th day of April, 1869.
BARTLE J. L. FRERE, 28, Lincoln's-inn-fields, Solicitor
of the Conservators of the Thames.
