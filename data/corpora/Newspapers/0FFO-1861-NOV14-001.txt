BANK of ENGLAND.— Unclaimed Stock.—Appli¬
cation having been made to the Governors of the Bank of
England to direct the re-transfer from the Commissioners for the Re¬
duction of the National Debt of the sum of £435 10s. Reduced Three
per Cent. Annuities, heretofore standing in the name of MARIA
POWELL, late of Lower Clapton, and formerly of Hackney, spinster,
deceased, and which was transferred to the said Commissioners in
consequence of the dividends thereon not having been received since
the 5th April, 1851; Notice is hereby given that, on the expiration
of three months from this date, the said Stock will be Transferred,
and the Dividends thereon paid, to David Powell and Charles Powell,
the executors, who have claimed the same, unless some other claimant
shall sooner appear and make out his claim thereto.
ALLOWANCE of SPOILED STAMPS in the
CITY of LONDON.—In order to meet the convenience of persons
residing at the east end of London who may have occasion to claim an
allowance of spoiled stamps, the Commissioners of Inland Revenue have
arranged that, from and after the 2d of next month, attendance shall
be given at their office in Gresham-house, Old Broad-street, on five
days in each week, instead of three, for the purpose of making such al¬
lowances—viz, on Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays, for the allow¬
ance of sea policy stamps, and on Tuesdays and Thursdays for other
stamps. The attendance will be from 11 to 2 o'clock.
THOMAS SARGENT, Sec.
Inland Revenue, Somerset-house, Nov. 12, 1861.

NORTH-WEST METROPOLITAN AUDIT
DISTRICT.—Parish of St. Luke, Chelsea.—I, the undersigned
J. H. Mitchiner, Auditor of the above named District, do hereby give
notice, that I have appointed the AUDIT of the ACCOUNTS of the
PARISH of ST. LUKE, CHELSEA, for the half-year ended the 29th
of September, 1861, to commence on Thursday, the 28th day of
November instant, at 10 o'clock in the forenoon, at the Workhouse,
Arthur-street, Chelsea, when and where all persons who by law are
bound to account at such audit are required to attend, and to submit
all books, documents, bills, and vouchers containing or relating to the
accounts or to moneys assessed for, and applicable to, the relief of the
poor. —Dated the 13th day of November, 1861.
J. H. MITCHINER, District Auditor.
