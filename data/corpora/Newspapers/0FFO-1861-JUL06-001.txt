TEBBUTT.—Whereas, Christopher Tebbutt, of
Frome Selwood, in the county of Somerset, by his will, dated the
8th of July, 1820, and the 2d of November, 1826, in the Prerogative
Court of Canterbury, directed that after the deaths of Ann Edwards
and Mary Sweetman, his freehold messuage and premises in Long¬
row, Frome Selwood, called the Spread Eagle Inn, should be sold, and
the money arising therefrom equally divided between all the children
then living of his three brothers and sister, John. Job, William, and
Mary Tebbutt; and whereas, Mary Sweetman survived Ann Edwards.
and died in July, 1859, and the said messuage and premises have since
been sold; notice is hereby given, pursuant to the Act of Parliament
passed in the 22d and 23d years of the reign of Her present Majesty, in¬
tituled "An Act to further Amend the Law of Property and to re¬
lieve Trustees," that all persons CLAIMING under the said will as
CHILDREN of the said JOHN, JOB, WILLIAM, and MARY
TEBBUTT, or otherwise, are required to send the particulars of their
claims to the undersigned, as solicitor for Benjamin Tebbutt, of
No. 34. Langham-street, Langham-place, in the county of Middlesex,
the administrator, with the will annexed "de bonis non" of the said
Christopher Tebbutt, at his office as below, on or before the 2d day of
September, 1861, after which date the said administrator, having regard
only to the claims of which he shall then have had notice, will proceed
to apply the money arising from the sale in accordance with the direc¬
tions of the will, and for the money so applied he will not be liable to
any person of whose claim he shall not then have had notice. —Dated

GEORGE CAREW, 45, Bloomsbury-square, Lend.
