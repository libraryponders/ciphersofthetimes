SALMON FISHERY ACTS, 1861 to 1876. — West
Cumberland.—Notice is hereby given, that it is the intention of
the Right Honourable Richard Assheton Cross, one of Her Majesty's
Principal Secretaries of State, one month from the date hereof, to
issue a CERTIFICATE under his hand defining the limits of
the rivers Mite. Esk. Irt, Calder and Ehen, and forming the said
rivers into a FISHERY DISTRICT, as follows:—So much of the
said rivers Mite, Esk. Irt, and Calder, and their tributaries, as lies
within the county of Cumberland; so much of the river Ehen and its
tributaries as lies below the Weir, at the foot of Ennordale Lake, be¬
longing to the trustees of the town and harbour of Whitehaven; the
estuaries of the said rivers; so much of the coast as lies between the
North Head Lighthouse, near St. Bee's Head, on the north, and
Haverig Point, on the south, being the northern boundary of the
Fishery District of the rivers Kent, Bela, Winster, Leven, and Duddon;
all rivers flowing into the sea between the said points and being
within the said county ; the space between ordinary high and low
water mark (nereinafter termed the seashore) between the said points
and the sea adjoining to the seashore between the said points for the
distance of three miles seaward from ordinary low water mark.—
Home Office Whitehall, May 21st, 1879.
