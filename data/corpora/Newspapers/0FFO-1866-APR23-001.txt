GRAMMAR SCHOOL MASTERSHIP.—The
Governors of the Free Grammar School of Queen Elizabeth, in
Cranbrook, are about to ELECT a MASTER to succeed the late Rev.
a
There is an excellent school-house. The Master's stipend is £100
a year. He is permitted to take boarders on his own terms, and to
receive from foundation boys £1 1s. per quarter for instruction in
writing, arithmetic, modern history, and geography; and £1 1s. per
quarter for mathematics. There is an Exhibition of £30 a year for
foundation boys to one of the Universities. The late Master had
about 50 boarders, at 40 guineas per annum each, and 20 foundation
boys.
The Master must be a Graduate of Oxford or Cambridge, between
25 and 50 years of age. Preference will be given to candidates expe¬
rienced in school tultion.
Applications addressed to the Governors, under cover to the Rev.
T. A. Carr, Vicarage, Cranbrook, to be forwarded before the 1st of
May next ; upon receipt of which a copy of the rules for the govern¬
ment of the school, with a series of questions to be answered by can¬
didates (and returned to the Governors accompanied by testimonials)
will be transmitted to each applicant.
Due notice will be given of the day of election. The Master to enter
upon his duties by Midsummer next.
No private application to either of the Governors will receive atten¬
tion; on the contrary, any endeavour thus to influence will prejudice
a candidate.
Testimonials are not to accompany applications, but are to be with¬
held till answers to the Governors' questions are transmitted.
Cranbrook, Kent, 29th March, 1866.

be prepared to receive boarders, and able to instruct the junior classes
