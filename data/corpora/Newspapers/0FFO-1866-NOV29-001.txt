and Taf, and their tributaries, as is situate within the counties of Car¬

also the estuaries of the said rivers, and so much of the coast as lies
between Worms Head in the county of Glamorgan, and St. Gowan's

between the said points, and being within the said counties, as laid
down in the annexed map deposited in the office of the Clerk of the

given, that it is the intention of the Right Hon. Spencer
Horatio Walpole, one of Her Majesty's Principal Secretaries of State,
to GRANT CERTIFICATES, under his hand, forming inte fishery
districts the RIVERS SEIONT, Gwrfai, and Llyfni; the Wharfe, Nidd,
Ure, Swale, and Ouse; and the river Medway, as hereinafter defined:
and that the limits of the said rivers for the purposes of the said Act
shall be defined to include Seiont, Gwriai, and Llyfni, so much of
the rivers Seiont, Gwriai, and Llyfni, and their tributaries, as is
situate within the county of Carnarvon, and the rivers Alaw, Cefni, and
Braint, and their tributaries, situate in the Island of Anglesea, and
also the estuaries of the said rivers and so much of the coast as lies
between the western boundary of the stream running into the Leven
Sands at Aber and the north-west boundary of the fishery district of
the river Dovey at Criccieth, both in the county of Carnarvon, and in¬
cluding the whole coast of Anglesey, and all streams flowing into the
sea between the said points, and being within the said counties.
Wharfe, Nidd, Ure, Swale, and Ouse.—So much of the rivers Wharfe,
Nidd, Ure and Swale, and their tributaries, as is situate within the
North, East, and West Ridings of the county of York, and also so
much of the river Ouse and its tributaries within the said county as lies
west of its junction with the river Derwent.
Medway.—The river Medway, with its tributaries, situate within the
counties of Kent, Surrey, and Sussex, and also the estuary of the said
river, and so much of the coast as lies between the northern boundary
