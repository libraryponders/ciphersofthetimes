THE EPPING FOREST ACT 1878. Whereas the
Conservators of Epping Forest are required to make a
REGISTER of COMMONER of EPPING FOREST Notice is hereby
given that all Owners and Occupiers of lands and tenements lying
within the ancient boundary of Epping Forest as set forth in the
perambulation made by authority of Parliament in the 17th year of
King Charles the First are required to send in to me their claims to
have their names inserted on such Register in the formeet forth in
the Schedule hereto—copies of which Schedule may be obtained at the
Office of the City Salicitor Guildhall London. Such Claims must be
sent in on or before the 1st day of October next, and the Conservators
are entitied to require reas nable proof of the title of each Claimant.
The followins provisions of the Act apply to such Register:—
"The Register shall be made in two parts. Patt 1 being for the
Northern Forest Parishes namely—Epping, Theydon Bois. Loughton,
Waitham Holy Cress. Chingford and Chigwell ; and Part 2 being for
the Southern Forest Parishes, namely—Woodford, Walthamstow,
Leyton, Wanstead, Little Ilford, and West Ham.
"Where a Commoner is under age or of unsound mind, or a
married woman, his or ber guardian, committee or husband shall be
entitled to be on the Register in his or her place.
"Where several persons are joint tenants co-parceners or tenants in
common in respect of rights of common they shall in respect thereof
be considered as one ; and such one of them only shall be entered on
the Register as the majority of them select in writing or in case they
are equally divided, or decline to select, then as the Conservators
select.
The Conservators will give notice after the claims are sent in, when
the Register is ready for inspection and of the days appointed by
them for correcting the Register.	T. J. NELSON City Solicitor
