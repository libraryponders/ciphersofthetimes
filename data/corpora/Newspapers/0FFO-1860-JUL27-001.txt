BRITISH SYRIAN RELIEF FUND.—A Com¬
mittee has been formed with the view of raising a sum of money
for the relief of the sufferers by the late unhappy events in Syria. Re¬
lief will be afforded without regard to sect or party, through a Com¬
mittee at Beyrout, presided over by Her Majesty's Consul-General.
Donations have been promised by Viscount Palmerston, K.G., Viscount
Stratford de Redeliffe, G.C.B., the Turkish Ambassador, Earl Gran¬
ville, K.G., Lord John Russell, M.P., the Earl of Shaftesbury, Lord
Overstone, Baron Lionel de Rothechild, M.P., Messrs. Spartall and
Co., Messrs. Heywood, Kennard, and Co., H. E. Gurney, Esq., &c.
Further subscriptions are requested, and also donations of provisions,
clothing, medicine, tents, &c., which will be sent out free of expense.
Depots in the city and at the west end will shortly be announced.
Many thousands of persons are without food and sheltey
A first instalment of £1,000 has been announced by telegraph to-day
to the Consul-General at Beyrout. Messrs. Saunders, Otley, and Co.,
who have been instructed from Beyrout to receive subscriptions for the
same object, have agreed to write out to their friends advising them to
merge in the Committee presided over by H.B.M. Consul-General. All
London bankers are requested and authorized to receive subscriptions
to the fund.COMMITTEE.

SATURDAY EARLY CLOSING MOVEMENT.
—At a PUBLIC MEETING of Tradesmen favourable to Early
Closing on Saturdays, held at St. James's-hall, 10th July, 1860,
Mr. PEARCE (of Halling, Pearce, and Stone's) in the chair,
Resolutions were unanimously agreed to approving the plan for
closing shops on Saturday afternoons, at 4 o'clock in the winter and 5 in
the summer months, to commence on the 1st day of September next.
Alarge number of leading houses having agreed to the above, and resolved
to begin on the 1st of September next, all employers who feel an interest
in this subject, and desire to join in it, are requested to forward ther
names forthwith to WILLIAM LOCKE, 127, Regent-street, W.
Subscriptions to be paid to Bank of London, Charing-cross, and
Threadneedle-street.

vites friends of the Volunteer Movement to favour this Corps, by fur¬
nishing DESIGNS for the obverse and reverse of a GOLD CHAL¬

peted for at the Annual Prize Meeting of the Corps. The large medal

free inspection of other corps who may desire such ald. Designs or
