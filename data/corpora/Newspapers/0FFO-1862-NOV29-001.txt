ONE HUNDRED POUNDS REWARD.—
Whereas, it having come under the notice (of Her Majesty's
Government that  LITTLE GIRL, named ELIZABETH HUNTER,
usually called "Betty Hunter," was DECOYED AWAY from Green
Man's-lane, Frog-lane, Lower-road, Islington, on Sunday evening,
March 30, by a decent-looking man, and has not since been heard of,
Sir George Grey hereby authorizes a reward of £50 to any one giving
such information as shall lead to the conviction of the offender, and
he will advise Her Majesty's pardon to any accomplice or accomplices
giving such information. And a further reward of £50 will be paid
on application to J. B. Talbot, Secretary of the Society for the Pro¬
tection of Young Females, 28, New Broad-street, city, to any party
giving such information as shall lead to the recovery of the child and
the conviction of the offender. Elizabeth Hunter is of pale com¬
plexion, has light hair and eyes, and a large scar on one of her cheeks.
Persons feeling sympathy with her broken-hearted parents are re¬
quested to forward their kind contributions to the Rev. D. Wilson,
Vicar of Islington, Barnsbury-park; the Rev. J. M. Ward, Incumbent
of St. Stephen's, 49, Canonbury-square, Islington; Rev. Louis Stan¬
ham, 54, Gibson-square, Islington; or Mr. E. Miles, 15, Liverpool¬
street, Bishopsgate-street, city.
