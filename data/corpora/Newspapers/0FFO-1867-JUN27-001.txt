POLICE PERJURY CASE.—At a Public Meeting
held in the St. Paneras Vestry-hall on the 8th February last, H.
J. Slack, Esq., in the chair, the following resolution was carried una¬
nimously :—"That the thanks of this Meeting are due and presented
to Mr. John Ivory for his firmuess and perseverance in the prosecution
of this case, and that the generous manner in which he took the whole
expense upon himself is deserving of the highest commendation, and
should be recognized by the public; and, with this view, that a Com¬
mittee be appointed to collect SUBSCRIPTIONS to INDEMNIFY
Mr. IVORY in the amount of his expenditure." The Committee ap
pointed by the above resolution have to acknowledge the receipt of
£128 19s. 6d. already subscribed, still leaving a balance of £121 0s. 6d.
due to Mr. Ivory. Subscriptions will be thankfully received by W.

road; or Mr. John Ivory, 53, College-place, Canden-towr.

DENERS.—THREE PREMIUMS of £100. £50, and £25 respec¬
tively will be given for the best THREE DESIGNS for LAYING¬
LOUT and UTILIZING 200 acres of LAND, partly for building and
the remainder as a park for purposes of recreation, in Hayling Island,
Hampshire, immediately to the east of Southsea.
It is intended that the park shall include two cricket grounds, with
appropriate buildings for players and visitors, to cost not more than
£600 ; and also an archery ground, sufficiently spacious for archery
meetings on a large scale, with buildings for members and visitors, to
cost not more than £400.
The competitors will be required to devote special attention to the
nature, description, and situation of the houses to be erected either
surrounding or in the park, and to furnish an estimate of the probable
ground-rents to be realized.
The designs to which the premiums are awarded to become the pro¬
perty of the proprietor of the estate. All designs to bear a motto or
device, and to be accompanied by a sealed envelope bearing on the
outside a corresponding mark, and, within, the name and address of the
competitor.
A general plan of the site, with further particulars, may be obtained
on and after Monday, the 24th inst., on application to Mr. H. R.
Trigg, surveyor and builder, South Hayling, Havant, Hants, to whom
the designs are to be forwarded on or before Monday, the 29th day of
July next, and the premiums will be paid to the successful competitors
on the 31st day of August next, by
