land to direct the re-transfer from the Commissioners for the Reduc¬

shall sooner appear and make out his claim thereto.

CACTORY ACT EXTENSION BILL.—At a
MEETING of LONDON PUBLISHERS and BOOKBINDERS,
held at Freemasons' Tavern, on Tuesday, June 18, SAMPSON LOW,
sen., Esq., in the Chair, the following resolutions were passed unani¬

That this Meeting, while not opposing wholly the Government plan
for regulating the hours of labour of women and young persons, con¬
sider that the Bills now before Parliament require modification to
meet the peculiarities of the bookbinding trade, and think that the
terms proposed to Mr. Tremenhere form a fair basis for the arrange¬
ment, and this Meeting pledges itself to cordially support a committee
in urging upon the House the acceptance of those terms.
That Messrs. Bone, Burn, Darton, Edmonds, Ecles, Leighton, Simp¬
son, Straker, Thorburn, Watson, and Zachnsdorf be appointed a Com¬
mittee to carry out the last fresolution, and that they be empowered
to take such steps as in their judgment rhey may consider proper for
that purpose.
That this Meeting considers that a proposition to abolish Sunday
labour should be included in the Bill,
