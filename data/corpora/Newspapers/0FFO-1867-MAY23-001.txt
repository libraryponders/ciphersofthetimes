NORTHAMPTON FREE GRAMMAR SCHOOL.
—The Trustees of the above School are now desirous to proceed
to the APPOINTMENT of a HEAD MASTER. Under the scheme
of the Court of Chancery regulating the School it is provided that the
Head Master shall be a member of the Church of England, and Gra¬
duate of an English university or of Trinity College, Dublin. He will
be entitled to half the capitation fee of £4 on each foundation scholar,
and is to be entitled to take boarders to the number of 20, or, with the
consent of the Trustees, a larger number.
Copies of the scheme, which contains full information as to the
salary and income of the Master, may be had on application to Messrs.
Hensman, Northampton, solicitors to the Board. Applications to be
sent in to Messrs. Hensman on or before the 24th day of June next.
