THAMES MARINE OFFICERS' TRAINING
SCHOOL SHIP will be OPENED for the reception of boys, in
July, after the Midsummer holydays.
The Lords of the Admiralty have been pleased to give Her Majesty's
50-gun frigate Worcester, which will be moored in the Thames, and
called the Thames Marine Officers' Training Ship.
The general object will be to educate boys for the sea-faring life,
and to give them such instruction as will fit them for their profession
before they enter upon the actual duties of a sailor, and such as will,
with practical experience at sea, enable them to become thorough
masters of their profession.
The services of the most able instructors in all branches of education
will be procured, and great care taken to impart to the boys a therough
sound English education, which will embrace geometry, trigonqetry,
navigation, nautical astronomy, mechanics, steam-engine marine sur¬
veying, and chart drawing.
French will be taught to all.
Nautical instruction will be given by an able commander and his
officers, and all boys will be exercised in the duties of a first class ship.
Boys only that are intended for the sea will be admitted, and from

Terms of admission, 35 guineas per annum.
