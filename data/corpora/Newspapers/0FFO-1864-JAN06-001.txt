LEONARD FLINT.—Whereas by an Order of the
High Court of Chancery, made in a cause of "Fearnside v.
Flint," and dated the 3d day of November, 1863, it was ordered that
an inquiry be made whether Leonard Flint in the pleadings in the said
cause named is living or dead, and, if dead, whether he died during the
lifetime of George Flint, the testator in the same pleadings named, or
after his decease, and if the said Leonard Flint is dead, whether or not
he died a bachelor, and whether or not intestate, and who was his heir¬
at-law and customary heir at the time of his death, and who is now his
heir at-law and customary heir. The said Leonard Flint was for¬
merly of Market Weighton, in the county of York. On the 29th day
of February, 1840, the said Leonard Flint left England by the ship
Silas Richards for New York, in the United States of America, and
arrived there on the 2d day of April, 1840. The said Leonard Flint is
believed to have intended to proceed to Newark, in the State of Ohio.
in the said United States of America. At the time of leaving England
the said Leonard Flint was possessed of real estate at Market Weighton
aforesaid. The said LEONARD FLINT, if living, or, if dead, his
wife or children (if any), or any other persons claiming to be interested
in his said real estate, are required to COMMUNICATE with
Messrs. Shackles and Son, solicitors, Hull, or Mr. H. S. Redpath, soli¬
citor, 27, Walbrook, London, for the purpose of establishing his, her,
or their identity, or of proving his death, and her or their title to the
said real estate, on or before the 25th day of March, 1864.—Dated the

H. SYME REDPATH, 27, Walbrook, London, Agent for
Shackles and Son, Hull, Yorkshire.
