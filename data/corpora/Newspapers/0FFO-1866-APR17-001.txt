CAMDEN ESTATE PROTECTION COMMIT¬
TEE.—At a MEETING of the Leaseholders and Residents of the
Camden New-town Estate, held on the 10th April.
M. A. GARVEY, Esq., in the Chair,
It was unanimously resolved.
That two constables be permanently employed for the protection of
the estate, and for the preservation of its privacy.
The duties of the constables to be the exclusion from the streets and
squares of all tramps, vagrants, and impreper characters, to suppress
street eries and other noises, and to keep the gates closed against all
through traffic, while offering perfect freedom of ingress and egress to
the residents on the estate and to all traffic directly connected with
them.
A letter of authority from the Marquis Camden was read appointing
the Chairman of the Committee his representative in these matters, and
authorizing him to prosecute trespassers in his own name.
RICHARD RICHARDSON, Chairman of Committee.
JOHN DUNCAN, Hon. Secretary.
