WILLIAM SNOWDON.—TEN POUNDS RE¬
WARD.—Whereas, by a Decree of the High Court of Chancery,
made in a cause of "Dunn v. Snowdon," and dated the 23d day of
November, 1861, it was ordered that an inquiry be made whether
WILLIAM SNOWDON, in the bill filed in the said cause mentioned,
is LIVING or DEAD, and if dead when he died. The said William
Snowdon was formerly of West Herrington, in the county of Durham
and was by trade a carpenter and joiner. For several years (in and
prior to the year 1847) he was employed on the works of the South¬
Eastern Railway Company, in the neighbourhood of Dover and Folkte
stone, Kent. He was last seen at West Herrington aforesaid on the 1st
of January, 1848, and, for a short time previously, had been living at
Hetton, in the county of Durham. The said William Snowdon if living,
or (if dead) his legal personal representative, is required to communi¬
cate with Messrs. S. J. and H. Dale, solicitors, North Shields, North¬
umberland, or Mr. H. Syme Redpath, solicitor, 27, Walbrook, London,
for the purpose of establishing his identity, or of proving his death and
the title of the legal personal representative, on or before the 31st day
of January, 1862. In case the said William Snowdon be dead £10 re¬
ward will be paid to any person furnishing evidence of the death of
the said William Snowdon. —Dated the 18th day of December, 1861.
H. SYME REDPATH, 27, Walbrook, London, Agent for
S. J. and H. Dale, North Shields, Northumberland.
