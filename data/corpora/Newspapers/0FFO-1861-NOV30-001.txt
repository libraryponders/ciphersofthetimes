Kaye, plaintiff, and James Metcalfe, defendant.—Whereas His
Honour the Vice-Chancellor Sir Richard Torin Kindersley, Knight, on
the 21st day of November instant, granted unto me, the above named
John Kaye, an injunction in this cause to restrain the above named
defendant, James Metcalfe, from preparing or selling any pills under
the designation of "WORSDELL's PILLS," or "Worsdell's Vegetable
Restorative Pills," and from printing or causing to be printed, or
using any handbills, covers, or wrappers in and for the sale of pills
having the name of Worsdell printed, written, or stamped thereon,
other than handbills, covers, or wrappers furnished by the plaintiff, and
further in any manner using the name of Worsdell in or about the
sale of pills other than pills prepared by the plaintiff; and from
selling any pills (other than pills prepared by the plaintiff) in boxes,
covers, wrappers, or any other things bearing any imitations with or
without colourable variations only to the boxes covers, and wrappers
used by the plaintiff in the sale of Kaye's Worsdell's pills, or any
boxes, covers, or wrappers, or other things which shall represent or
have the appearance of representing the pills sold by the defendant as
"Worsdell's Pills," or "Kaye's Worsdell's Pills," or "Worsdells
Vegetable Restorative Pills," or "Kaye's Worsdell's Vegetable Restora¬
tive Pills." I do hereby give notice, that proceedings will be im¬
mediately commenced against all persons preparing or selling pills
under the designation aforesaid, or in using handbills, covers, or
wrappers, in and for the sale of pills having the name of Worsdell
printed, written, or stamped thereon, other than pills of my own pre¬
paration, and handbills, covers, or wrappers supplied by me.—Dated
this 22d day of November, 1861.
JOHN KAYE, of Prospect-hall, Woodford, Essex, and also of
No. 22, Bread-street, London, Sole Proprietor of the recipe
for the preparation of Worsdell's Vegetable Restorative Pills.
