BANK of ENGLAND.—Unclaimed Dividends.—
Application having been made to the Governors of the Bank of
England to direct the payment of one dividend on the sum of
£1,176 5s. 11d. Consolidated Three per Cent. Annuities, and one divi¬
dend on £1,568 18s. 9d. like Annuities, heretofore standing in the
names of CHARLOTTE MACNAB, widow, and CHARLES DAY
MORSON, Esq., both of Bytown, Upper Canada, and which dividends
were paid over to the Commissioners for the Reduction of the
National Debt, in consequence of the first thereof having re¬
mained unclaimed since the 5th January, 1854;—Notice is hereby given,
that, on the expiration of three months from this date, the said Divi¬
dends will be paid to the said Charlotte Macnab and Charles Day
Morson, who have claimed the same, unless some other claimant shall
sooner appear and make out his claim thereto.
ROYAL YORK HOTEL, Margate.—In our publi¬
cation of September 3 the following unfounded statement
appeared:—"There was a complaint against Robert Wardell, of the
York Hotel, but he did not appear to apply for a renewal of his
licence." We express REGRET that a STATEMENT so untrue
should have been published. The reporter employed, being a stranger
to the dirtrict, was ignorant of the respectability of the York Hotel
and its proprietor, Mr. Wardell. We have ascertained that Mr.
Wardell's licence was renewed as a matter of course. We are exceed¬
ingly annoyed that it should have appeared in the Thanet Advertiser,
and take this opportunity to express our sincere regret that it did, as
it was unwarranted and untruthful.—Thanet Advertiser office, Rams¬
gate, September 8, 1864.
