EPPING FOREST COMMISSION.—The Epping
Forest Commissioners hereby give notice that, by an ORDER
dated the 30th day of October, 1872, made in pursuance of the autho¬
rity given to them for that purpose by the "Epping Forest Amen-ment
Act, 1872," they have ordered that, until the expiration of the Session
of Parliament next after they shall have made their final report under
the "Epping Forest Act, 1871," or until further order, all persons who¬
soever shall be, and they are by the said Order prohibited from com¬
mitting any waste, injury, or destruction of or to the trees growing upon
the waste lands of the said Forest within the Manor of Loughton, and
which lands are disbinguished on the plan annexed to the said Order by
the colour green (excepting such portions of the said waste lands, and
so coloured green, as are known by the names of Monke-woods and the
Loughton kisce); and all persons affected by the said order are to be
at liberty to apply to the said Commissioners as there may be ocea¬
sion.
Copies of the said Order and plan ase deposited for inspection at the
office of the Commissioners, No. 6, Old Palace-yand, Westminster; the
Castle Hotel, Woodtord; the Crown Hotel, Loughton; and the Cock
Hotel. Epping.
Dated this 1st day of November, 1873.
T. P. WILKINSON, Clerk to the Epping Forest Commissioners.

waste lands of the said forest, and mnclosures of waste lands of the said
forest as hereinafter mentioned, so far as they have been ascertained

inquiries already made by them. In such plan inclosures of waste

missioners, No. 6, Old Palace-yard, Westminster; the Castle Hotel,
