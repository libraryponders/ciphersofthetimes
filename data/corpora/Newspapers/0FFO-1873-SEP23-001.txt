NOTICE. —Whereas Her Majesty, by Warrant, under
Her Royal Signet and Sign Manual, bearing date the thirtieth
day of August, one thousand eight hundred and thirty seven, was
graciously pleased to give and grant unto John Bruce Bruce (thereto¬
fore John Bruce Knight), then calling himself John Bruce Pryce of
Duffryn, in the parish of Aberdare, and of Duffryn, in the parish of
Saint Nicolas, both in the county of Glamorgan, Esquire (since de¬
ceased), Her Royal License and Authority that he might thenceforth
continue to use the surname of Pryce in lieu of that of Bruce and bear
the arms of Pryce quarterly with those of Bruce and Knight, and
also that his eldest son, John Wyndham Bruce, then calling
himself, John Wyndham Bruce Pryce (also since deceased), might con¬
tinue to use the surname of Pryce in addition to and after that of
Bruce, and bear the arms of Pryce, quarterly with those of Bruce and
Knight, and that the said surnames and arms might in like manner be
borne and used by the issue of the said John Wyndham Bruce Pryce.
And whereas the said John Wyndham Bruce Pryce afterwards discon¬
tinued the use of the said surname and arms of Pryce, and used the
name and arms of John Wyndham Bruce only. Now I the under¬
signed, Alan Cameron Bruce-Pryce (heretofore known as Alan Came¬
ron Bruce), of Duffryn, in the parish of Saint Nicolas, in the
said county of Glamorgan, Esquire, being the eldest son of the above¬
mentioned John Wyndham Bruce, deceased, and the grandson of the
abovementioned John Bruce Pryce, deceased, do hereby give notice
that I have (in pursuance of Her Majesty's said Royal License and
Authority) ASSUMED, do resume, and shall hereafter continue to
use the SURNAME of PRYCE, in addition to and after that of
Bruce, and to bear the arms of Pryce quarterly with those of Bruce
and Knight.—As witness, my hand, this tenth day of September, one
thousand eight hundred and seventy three.
ALAN CAMERON BRUCE PRYCE.
