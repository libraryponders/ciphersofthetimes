COUNTY of MIDDLESEX to WIT.—The Con¬
tagious Diseases (Animals) Act, 1878.—TYPHOID FEVER of
SWINE—Carcases of Diseased Animals.—At a Meeting of the Exe¬
cutive Committee of the Local Authority for the County, held at the
Sessions-house, Clerkenwell, on Monday, the 17th day of February,
1879, it was.
aResolved and Ordered, That the following RULES and REGULA¬
TIONS shall have effect and be in force on and after the 20th day of
February, 1879, with regard to the carcases of swine which have died,
or have beon slaughtered while suffering from typhoid fever
1. The carcase of any animal which has died, or has been
slaughtered while suffering from typhoid fever shall not be moved
from the premises where the animal died, or was slaughtered, with¬
out a licence for that purpose under the hand of a District Inspector
of the Local Authority, and thea only for the purpose of burial, under
his direction and in his charge.
2. The carcase of every animal which has died, or has been
slaughtered as aforesaid, shall be buried by the owner in its skin, as
soon as possible, in some proper place, to be approved of by the In¬
spector. The skin shall be first so slashed as to ne rendered useless,
and the carcase shall be covered with a suflicient quantity of quick
lime, or other disiniectant, and with not less than six feet of earth.

By order of the Executive Committee,
NICHOLSON, Clork of the Peace.
Sessions-house, Clerkenwell, 17th February, 1879.
