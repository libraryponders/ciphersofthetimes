SALMON FISHERY ACT. 1865,
The LIMITS of the FISHERY DISTRICT of the RIVER WYE

So much of the river Wye and its tributaries as is situate within the
counties of Radnor, Brecknock, Hereford, Gloucester, and Monmouth,
and also so much of the estuary of the river Severn on its western side
as lies between Lydney Pill, in the county of Gloucester, and Collister
Pill, in the county of Monmouth, commencing from the middle of a
line drawn from the said Lydney Pill to Clapton Pill, in the said
county of Gloucester, as laid down on the map deposited in the office
of the Clerk of the Peace for the county of Hereford. Ido hereby
certify that the fishery district above described is duly formed by me.
Given under my hand this 18th day of January, 1866.


G. GREY.
