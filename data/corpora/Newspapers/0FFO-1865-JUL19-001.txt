CANCER HOSPITAL, London and Brompton
established for the exclusive treatment of the indigent suffering
from cancer.—The Hospital has now been in operation 14 years, during
which time it has received under its care 5,753 cases. There is accom¬
modation for 30 in-door patients; but the income of the Charity is not
equal to maintain that number. The out-door cases continue to average
500 constantly under treatment. Poor persons suffering from cancer are
admitted as out-door patients on their own application, and are seen
at 167, Piccadilly, on Tuesday,
Thursday, and Friday, at 2 o'clock, and
at Brompton, on Wednesday, at 3 o'clock. Those wishing for admission
to the Hospital must attend at the London establishment, 167, Picca¬
dilly, on Tuesdays, at 2 b'clock; or at Brompton, on Wednesdays,
at 3 o'clock. The recommendation of a subscriber takes precedence.
