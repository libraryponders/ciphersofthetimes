INJUNCTION in CHANCERY against the MER¬
CHANTS and MANUFACTURERS POCKET DIRECTORY of
LONDON, 1868, and MERCHANTS and MANUFACTURERS'
DIRECTORY oF LONDON, 1863.—Notice is hereby given, that in a
suit, wherein John Stuart Crosbie Morris, as proprietor of the Business
Directory of London, is plaintiff, and William Ashbee and others, as
proprietors of the said Merchants and Manufacturers
Directory, are
defendants, the Vice Chancellor Sir G. M. Giffard has this day
GRANTED an INJUNCTION, restraining the defendants, their
agents, and servants, from further printing, publishing, and selling the
above directory, published by the defendants, on the ground that it is
a piracy of a great part of the said Business Directory, and the Vice¬
Chancellor ordered the defendants to pay all the costs of the suit. All
booksellers and others are hereby warned against selling any directory
published by the defendants, containing any matter pirated from the
plaintiff's directory, and the general public are hereby warned against
purchasing the same.—Dated this 10th day of November, 1868.
Signed, BENHAM and TINDELL, 18, Essex-Street, Strand,
Plaintiff's Solicitory.
