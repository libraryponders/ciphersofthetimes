NOTICE to WILLIAM ROSWELL.—If
WILLIAM ROSWELL, carpenter, son of Thomas Roswell,
late of Telscombe, in the county of Sussex, farmer, deceased, will
APPLY to Messrs. Blaker and Son, solicitors, Lewes, he will hear of
something to his advantage. He was last reliably heard of in Dart¬
mouth-street, Westminster, about Christmas, 1843. A report has been
circulated that he was lodging in Octavius-street, Deptford, Kent,
about three years since, and was then working in Deptford as a car¬
penter or shipwright. There is a considerable sum of money now in the
Court of Chancery belonging to the said William Roswell (if alive), and
which sum will, under the direction of the Court, be paid to his legal
personal representative unless the said William Roswell is proved to
be alive. It is intended to make application to the Court of Probate
for letters of administration to the effects of the said William Roswell,
on the assumption that he has died intestate, and with a view to re¬
ceive the money out of Court, and to distribute it among the parties
entitled thereto as if he were dead. A Reward of Five Guineas will
he paid to any person who will first give either Messrs. Blaker and Son.
or their agent, Edmund Byrne, Esq., solicitor, 3, Whitehall-place, Lon¬
don, such information as will lead either to the discovery of the said
William Roswell, or satisfactory proof of his death.—Lewes, Sussex,
January, 1869.
