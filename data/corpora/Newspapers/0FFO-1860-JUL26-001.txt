HAMPSTEAD.—At a numerous MEETING of
the LICENSED VICTUALLERS of Hampstead, held at Mr.
Kirby's, the William the Fourth Tavern, July 24th.
Mr. HAWARD, of the Flask Tavern, in the chair.
It was moved by Mr. Eagle, seconded by Mr. Maltby, and carried
unanimously:—
That in consequence of the increased duty upon spirits, the trade
is compelled to make a proportionate increase in the retail price, and
this meeting decides upon raising 1d. per quartern upon all British and
foreign spirits and cordials.
WARD of FARRINGDON WITHOUT.—
Tuesday, July 24, 1860.—At a MEETING of the inhabltant
honseholders of this Ward, held at the Sessions-house, Old Bailey, in
pursuance of a requisition from the Ward Committee, appointed to
promote the establishment of meat and poultry markets on the north
of Long-lane, Smithfield, in connexion with the Metropolitan Railway;
Sir JAMES DUKE, Bart., M.P., Alderman of the said Ward, in the
chair.
A Report from the Committee upon the several matters referred to
them was read.
It was resolved unanimously, That the Report be received and
adopted.
Resolved nnanimously, That a petition in favour of the Smithfield
Market and Streets In provement Bill be prepared, signed by the
Alderman, on behalf of this Meeting, and presented to the House of
Lords; and that the Committee be empowered to get a similar peti¬
tion signed by the inhabitants of the Ward, and to present the same
to the House of Lords if they shall deem it expedient to do so.
Resolved unanimously,—That a memorial be presented to the Gover¬
nors of the Charterhouse, complaining of the manner in which the
names of Her Majesty the Queen, the Prince Consort, the Prince of
Wales, and the Right Rev. and Right Hon. Governors have been used
against the Bill before the Select Committee of the House of Commons;
and praying that they will not permit the funds of the Charity, and the
influence of their exalted names and stations to be again employed to
throw out the Bill or to clog it with costly conditions which may pre¬
vent this great undertaking from being carried into execution; and
that the Committee do appoint a deputation to present the same; and
that copies of this resolution be sent to each Governor, requesting him
to appoint an early time to receive the deputation.
Resolved unanimously. That the thanks of this Meeting are due,
and be hereby given, to our worthy and respected Alderman, Sir James
Duke, Bart., M.P., not only for his energetic exertions with respect to
the proceedings of this evening, but also for his constant readiness upon
all occasions to advance the interests of the Ward and his constituents
generally.
JOHN PONTIFEX, Ward Clerk.
