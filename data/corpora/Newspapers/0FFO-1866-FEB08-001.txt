INLAND REVENUE-OFFICE, Somerset-house, London, W. C.,
5th February, 1866.
T having been represented to the Board of Inland
Revenue that an impression prevails among colonial and other
brokers that their license as brokers empowers them to sell colonial
and other produce by auction without being duly licensed as auc¬
tioneers, the Board of Inland Revenue think it right to give notice,
that any person, whether licensed as a broker or not, who conducts a
sale by competition without having at the time a license in force as an
auctioneer, renders himself liable to the penalty of £100, imposed by
the 4th section of the Act 8 Vict., cap. 15.
WM. CORBETT, Secretary.
