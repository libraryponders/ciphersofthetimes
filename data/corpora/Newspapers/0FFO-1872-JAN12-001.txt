 Members.—r T e

the TRIENNIAL ELECTION of SIX MEMBERS to the Local
Marine Board of the Port of London, pursuant to the provisions of
"The Merchant Shipping Act, 1854," will take place at the Mansion¬
house, on Tuursday, the 25th day of January inst., at 12 o'clock at
noon, where and when all persons whose names appear on the revised
list of voters then in force may record their vote or votes for any one or
more persons, not exceeding six, to be members of such Board; every
male who is by such list entitled to vote being qualified to be elected a
member.
The mode of conducting the election will be as follows:
1. The votes will be taken by means of voting papers, signed by the
voters, a printed form of which, with a copy of the revised list of voters
in force, may be obtained on application at this Justice room one week

2. The voter must persoually deliver his voting paper, properly filled
up and signed, to the undersigned (who has been appointed to receive
the votes), at this Justice-room.
3. The election will finally close at 3 o'clock in the afternoon, after
which hour no voting paper will be received.
By order. GEORGE C. OKE, Chef Clerk to the Lord Mayor.
Justice-room, Mansion-house, Jan. 11, 1872.
