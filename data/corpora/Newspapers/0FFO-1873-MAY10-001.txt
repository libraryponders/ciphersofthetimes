IN the HIGH COURT of ADMIRALTY of IRE¬
LAND. No. 282.—The Screw Steamer Torch, of Dublin.—Whereas
a Cause of Limitation of Liability was instituted in the said Court on
behalf of James Stirling of Fitzwilliam-place, Dublin, the owner of
the late Screw Steamer Torch, of Dublin, for the purpose of having his
liability limited in respect of a COLLISION which occurred on or about
the 1st day of March, 1873, between the Steamer TORCH and the Ship
CHACABUCO: and whereas the Judge of the said Court did on the
23rd day of April, 1873, amongst other things declare that the owner of
the said Screw Steamer Torch is entitled to limited liability according
to the provisions of the Merchant Shipping Act, 1854, and the Merchant
Shipping (Amendment Act), 1862: and that in respect of loss or life or
damage-to ships, boats, goods, merchandise, or other things by reason
of the improper navigation of the said Serew Steamer Torch on the
occasion of the collision between the said vessel and the late
ship Chacabuco on the 1st March, 1873, the owner of the said
Screw Steamer Torch is answerable in damages to an amount not ex¬
ceeding £4,860, such sum being at the rate of £15 for each ton of the
gross registered tonnage of the said Screw Steamer Torch, which sum,
together with interest thereon, at 4 per cent, from the said 1st day of
March, 1873, to the 23rd day of April inst., the said James Stirling has
paid into Court. This is therefore to give notice, pursuant to the
order of the Court in that behalf, to all persons having or asserting any
CLAIM in respect of loss of life or personal injury, or loss or damage
to any ship, boat, goods, merchandise, or other things, caused by im¬
proper navigation of the Torch on the occasion of the said collision,
that if they do not come in and enter their claims in this cause, in the
High Court of Admiralty of Ireland, on or before the 31st day of May
next ensuing, they will be excluded from sharing in the aforesaid
amount. — Dated this 29th day of April, 1873.
