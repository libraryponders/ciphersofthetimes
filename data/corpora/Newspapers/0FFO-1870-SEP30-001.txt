has been made to the Board of Trade by the Corporation of the Borough
of Swansea for an ORDER for the REGULATION of an OYSTER
and MUSSEL FISHERY in SWANSEA BAY, and Part of the
Bristol Channel lying off the south coast of Gower, in the county of
Glamorgan, containing an area of 140 square miles or thereabouts,
bounded as follows, that is to say:—on the north and east by the line
of ordinary low water mark from the point known as Worms Head, in
the parish of Rhosilly, to a point called the Sker Point, in the parish
of Aberavon, in the county of Glamorgan, such boundary being within
or adjacent to the several parishes of Rhosilly, Penmaen, Oyster¬
mouth, Swansea, St. Thomas, Briton Ferry, and Aberavon; thence on
the south by an imaginary line drawn from the Sker Point to the Sker
Weather Light, and thence by an imaginary line to the West Helvic
ight, and thence on the west by an imaginary line to the Worms
Head first mentioned.
