OFFICIAL NOTICE.—Proposal to change a ship's
name.—1. Robert Curwen, of Liverpooi, hereby give notice, that
in consequence of the late owner having adopted the famiry name of
Batters as the distinguishing mark of his line, and as her present name
is unsuitable for my general trade, it is my intention to apply to the
Board of Trade, under section 6 of the Merchant Shipping Act, 1871, in
respect of my ship. JEAN BATTERS of London, official number
89,244, of gross tonnage 999 74-100 tons, of register tonnage 644.10 tons,
heretofore owned by George Batters, of London, for permission to
CHANGE her NAME to HENRY, to be registered under the said
new name at the Port of Liverpool, as owned by Robert Curwen.
Any objection to the proposed change of name must be sent to the
Assistant Secretary, Marine Department, Board of Trade, within
fifteen days from the appearance of this advertisement.—Dated at
Liverpool, this fourth day of July, 1878.
ROBERT CURWEN.
