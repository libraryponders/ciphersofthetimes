CAUTION to MINERAL WATER MANUFACA
TURERS and VENDORS.—In the High Court of Justice,
Chancery Division, 1878, A. 115, between the Apollinaris Com¬
pany (Limited), plaintiffs, and Edwards and Co., defendants. By
Judgment pronounced upon motion for an injunction made beforei
Lordship the Vice-Chancellor Sir James Bacon, on the 13th of July
1876, by counsel for the plaintiffs, and upon hearing counsel for the
defendants, and the defendants by their counsel undertaking to hand
over to the plaintiffs all labels and cards in the possession of the de
fendants having the word Apollinaris written upon them, it was, by
consent, ordered that the said defendants should accordingly deliver
over to the plaintiffs, upon oath, all labels and cards having the word
Apoliinaris written upon them and that a perpetual injunction
should be awarded against the defendants to restrain the defendants
Edwards and Company, their servants or agents, from selling or ad
vertising, or offering for sale, any mineral or other waters not being
the genuine Apollinaris Water, under the name of Apollinaris Water,
or under any other name of which the word Apoliinaris so forms part,
as to be calculated to deceive the public, or from in any manner in
fringing or interfering with the plaintiff's right to the exclusive saie
in Great Britain of Apollinaris Water, and to the exclusive use of the
word Apollinaris for describing the mineral water sold by them. And
it was ordered that the defendants, Edwards and Co., should pay to
the plaintiffs, the Apollinaris Company (Limited), their costs off
the action to be taxed by the T.
teining Master. Notice is hereby given,
that the APOLLINARIS COMPANY (Limited) will take immediate
proceedings against any person found to be INFRINGING or inter¬
fering with their RIGHTS in the manner indicated in the above Judg
ment. —Dated this 5th day of August, 1876.
