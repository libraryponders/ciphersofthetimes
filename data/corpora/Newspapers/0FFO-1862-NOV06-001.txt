THE late Countess ANDREW ZAMOYSKI.—
Poland's exiled children anxious to condole with their distin¬
guished countryman, Count Andrew Zamoyski, on the great loss he has
lately sustained intend to celebrate a FUNERAL SERVICE inmemory
of the said lady, since Count Zamoyski, now present in this country,
was not permitted, on his way into undeserved banishment, to pass
through Warsaw, when his wife was lying dangerously ill, nor allowed
to proceed on any term to bid her an eternal adien when telegraphed
for to her death bed. The Polish patriots expect that every friend of
the oppressed will join them in prayers, To-morrow (Friday), the 7th
inst., at 11 o'clock in the morning, at St. George's Cathedral, Lambeth.
