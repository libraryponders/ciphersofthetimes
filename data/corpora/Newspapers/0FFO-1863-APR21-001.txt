THE CITY Of LONDON VOLUNTEER SERVICE
CLUB.—As the requirements for conducting the Volunteer
Movement are so numerous, it is determined to form a CLUB in the
city of London on an extensive scale, where every accommodation can¬
be afforded, from the enrolment of members to a meeting of the Rifle
Association. It is intended to have extensive dining rooms, lava¬
tories, lecture room, rooms for assault at arms, dressing room, coutain¬
ing separate compartments, where members can deposit their uni¬
forms, reading rooms, smoking room, billard room, and, in fact, a com¬
plete establishment to meet the numerous wants of the rifleman and
the Volunteer Movement. All this will be carried forward on a scale
worthy of such an establishment, at the same time economy will be a
leading feature in the management. It is proposed and determined
that the entrance donation fee shall be £2 2s., and an annual subscrip¬
tion of £2 2s. The first £1 1s. to be paid on application for member¬
ship, another £1 1s. to be paid when the member's cards are issued,
and the remaining £2 2s. to be paid the first time the member presents
himself at the club; the yearly subscription taking date from that
time. All members of Rifle Corps, either effectives or honorary, are
eligible to become members of the Club, the membership being the
qualification, ballot is dispensed. There will be certain rules and
regulations laid down for cenducting the Club, to which all members
will have to conform. Officers of Her Maiesty's army connected with
the Volunteer Movement are eligible for membership. Honcrary
members of Rifle corps must be introduced by two effective riflemen,
members of the club. Deposits to be made to the bankers, when a
receipt will be given, the holder of which will be entitled to member¬
ship when the remaining £3 3s. is paid. A donation of 20 guineas will
entitle the donor to a life membership. Members of country corps,
whose head-quarters are out of the 20 mile radius from the General
Post-office, St. Martin's-le-Grand, will be admitted at half the donation
entrance fee, and half the annual subscription. All moneys deposited
with the bankers will be returned in full should the proposed arrange¬
ments not be carried out. Three commanding-officers of Volunteer
regiments are appointed trustees. The Club will be conducted by a
committee of management, consisting of 25 members, who will appoint
a chairman from their number. Full particulars will be advertised
when it is known that the Volunteers are desirous of joining in suffi¬
cient numbers as to ensure success. Those who are disposed to join
the club will be pleased to express themselves to that effect by letter,
addressed C. L. V. S. C., care of the auditor, C. W. Hargrave, Esq.,
71, Cornhill.
