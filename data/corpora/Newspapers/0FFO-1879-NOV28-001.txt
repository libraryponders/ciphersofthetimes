WALLASEY FREE GRAMMAR SCHOOL, Lis¬
card. Cheshire.—Head Master.—The Governors will, on Tues¬
day, the 30th day of December next, proceed to the ELECTION of
a HEAD MASTER, in the place of D. F. Ranking, Esq., M.A., who
has res gned.
The number of boys has gradually increased since the opening of
the school under the new scheme in 1876, and there are now 63 boys
attending the school—36 in the junior and 27 in the senior depart¬
ment.
The Head Master must be a graduate of some university within the
British Empire. He will receive a fixed stipend of £100 per annum,
and also head money of £2 for each boy in the junior department
and £3 for each boy in the senior department, up to the number of 35
in each department; £2 10s. and £3 10s. respectively up to 45; and
£3 and £4 respectively for each boy over 45 in each department.
There are at present over 21,000 inhabitants in Wallasey, and the
bopulation of the district is annually incressing, since the census of
1871, at the rate of about 1,000 a year. At presert there is no Master's
house, and by the terms of the scheme the Head Master is not to have
other employment.
The Governors determine the number of assistant-masters and
nssign the aggregate amount of salary such masters receive.
The Head Master has the sole power to appoint and dismiss the
assistant-masters and to apportion among them the sum assigned by
the Governors. There are at present two assistant-masters.
The course of instruction in the school will comprise Latin, mathe¬
matics, modern languages, natural science, and generally such sub¬
jects as are required in schemes settled by the Commissioners for
Endowed Schools of the Second Grade.
The Head Master will be expected to prepare and pass boys for the
Local Oxford and Cambridge Examinations.
Candidates are requested to forward their applications, with testi¬
monials, before the 18th day of December next, to the undersigned,
from whom further information may be had on application.
ALBERT T. WRIGHT, Solicitor, 17, Water-street, Liverpool,
Clerk to the Governors.
