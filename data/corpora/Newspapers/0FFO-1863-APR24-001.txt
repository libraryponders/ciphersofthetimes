STAR BRAND GENEVA.—In Chancery: Heintr¬
mann and another v. Tobitt and others.—In November last, Messrs.
Heintzmann and Rochussen advertised that Vice-Chancellor Stuart
had granted an injunction against Mr. Thomas B. Tobitt, of Leadenhall¬
street, African merchant, and Messrs. Tuchmann and Merfeld, of
Savage-gardens, commission agents, to restrain them from shipping
Geneva bearing the star brand, that injunction was obtained exparte,
and without notice, and on the 5th of March last, upon motions made
by the defendants, after due notice the Vice-Chancellor DISSOLVED
the INJUNCTION, and ordered Messrs. HEINTZMANN and
ROCHUSSEN to pay the costs of the motion. On Saturday last notice
was given by Messrs. Tuchmann and Merfeld, of their intention to
move to dismiss the plaintiffs bill, and on the same day Messrs. Heintz¬
mann and Rochussen obtained an order dismissing their own bill, with
coste of suit to be paid by them to all the defendants.—April 21, 1863.
THOS. PRICE, Solicitor for Mr. Tobitt, 24, Abchurch-lane.
RUSSELL and DAVIES, Solicitors for Messrs. Tuchmann
and Merfeld, 59, Coleman-street.
