CHANGE of NAME.—I, EDWARD WILLIAM
BROWN, Vicar of Sketty, in the county of Glamorgan, second
son of Alexander Rozel Brown, and grandson of the late William
Brown, Esquire, a Rear Admiral of the Red, do hereby give notice,
that I intend now and henceforth to USE and ADOPT, upon all
occasions and at all times, the family NAMB of BOLNEY, instead of
that of Brown, and that I have this day, as evidence of such my intention.
caused a deed, in the form prescribed by law, to be enrolled in Her
Majesty's High Court of Chancery, so that the name or style which I
shall henceforth use and be known by shall be Edward William
Bolney, and none other. — Dated this 3d day of November, 1868.
E. W. BROWN.
SALMON FISHERY ACTS, 1861, 1865.—Notice is
hereby given, that the Special Commissioners for English Fisheries
will hold a COURT at the Guildhall, York, on the 17th day of
December next, at the hour of 11 o'clock a.m., and at such Court will
proceed to inquire into the legality of all FISHING WEIRS. Fishing
Mill Dams, and Fixed Engines situate on the RIVER WHARFE, in
the Wharfe, Nidd, Ure, and Swale Fishery District; and all persons
interested in such engines are required to attend at such Court from
day to day until discharged, in order that a decision may be made by
the said Commissioners with respect to the removal of such fishing
weirs or fixed engines, or the alteratian of such fishing mill dams, or
that such other order may be made by them in the premises, as the
facts of each case appear to warrant. —Dated at 22, Parliament-street,

JAMES PATERSON. The Special Commissioners

for English Fisheries.
