BANK of ENGLAND.—Unclaimed Dividends.—
Application having been made to the Governors of the Bank of
England to direct the payment of seven dividends on the sum of £25
Annuities for terms of years, heretofore standing in the name of
MARY ANN TOWNES, of Cheapside, spinster, and which dividends
were paid over to the Commissioners for the Reduction of the National
Debt, in consequence of the first thereof having remained unclaimed
since the 5th January, 1857;—Notice is hereby given, that on the expi¬
ration of three months from this date, the said Dividends will be paid
to Giles Clarke, now Giles Clarke Earle, acting executor of Mary Ann
Townes, deceased, who has claimed the same, unless some other
claimant shall sooner appear and make out his claim thereto.

plication having been made to the Governors of the Bank of
England to direct the payment of one dividend on the sum of
£3,858 18s. 9d. Consolidated £3 per Cent. Annuities, heretofore standing
in the names of Sir JAMES COCKBURN, Bart., Paymaster of Royal
Marines, and THOMAS AMYOT, Esquire, Registrar of Colonial
Slaves, and which dividend was paid over to the Commissioners for the
Reduction of the National Debt, in consequence of its having remained
unclaimed since the 5th July. 1853; Notice is hereby given, that, on

be paid to Dame Marianna Augusta Hamilton, wife of Sir James
John Hamilton, one of the executors of Sir James Cockburn, deceased,
who was the survivor, who has claimed the same, unless some other
claimant shall sooner appear and make out his claim thereto.
