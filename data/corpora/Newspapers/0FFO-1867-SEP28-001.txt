PERUVIAN CONSULATE.—In conformity with
the new Consular Regulations of the Republic, it is ordered by
the Article 87, that all SHIPS leaving for any port of Pern must be
furnished with a SANITARY PATENT, signed by the Consul of
Pern at the port of her departure.
By the Article 88 of the same Regulations, it is ordered that the in¬
voices of the goods on board of the vessels going to Pern must be
declared at the Consulate and signed by the Consul; also the papers
relating to the measurements of the ship, her nationality, and pro¬
cedence of her cargo.
The omission by the captains or shipowners of those rules will be
punished with quarantine and fined on arriving at Pern.
London, September 24, 1867.
