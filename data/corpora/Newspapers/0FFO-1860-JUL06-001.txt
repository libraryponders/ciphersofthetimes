PRIVATE INQUIRY OFFICE: founded 1852,
, Devereux-court, Temple, under the direction of CHARLES
FREDERICK FIELD, late Chief Inspector of the Detective Police of
the Metropolis. This office has now been established upwards of eight
years, and during that period has had confided to it some of the most
important and extraordinary events of the day among which may be
enumerated the Rugely Murders, the Smyth Forgeries, and the Wor¬
cester Forgeries, ecclesiastical inquiries, bank forgeries, jewel and other
robberies, horse poisonings, incendiary fires, divorce and libel cases,
The foreion superintendent, who is conversant with the French,
German, Sitalian, Spanish, Turkish, and other languages, is in daily
attendance, between the hours of 2 and 4 p.m.
