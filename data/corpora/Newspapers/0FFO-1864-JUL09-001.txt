WHITEHALL.—Her Most Gracious Majesty the
Queen, having been pleased to GRANT unto HENRY BLISS
(now the Baron de Bliss), of Brandon-park, Suffolk, of Sleddale Forest,
Westmoreland, of Northcomb-hall, Devonshire, and of Berkeley-¬
house, Hyde-park-square, in the county of Middlesex, one of Her
Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the same county, F.R.S.L., Her
ROYAL LICENSE and AUTHORITY that he and his issue might
take the NAME and ARMS of BLISS only; and Her Majesty further
commanded that stich concessions should be void unless duly recorded
in Her Majesty's College of Arms. And whereas His Majesty Don
Fernando, Regent, in the name of His Most Faithful Majesty Don
Pedro V., King of Portugal, having taken into his gracious con¬
sideration that the said Baron de Bliss could only use the surname of
"Bliss," and no other, under his late relative's will, did, by his Royal
letters patent of the 5th day of June, 1855, at the Palace das Neces¬
sidades (for the especial reasons therein named), confer his Royal
favour unto the said Baron de Bliss by granting unto him the honour
to succeed to the dignity and title of a Baron, and that thenceforth he
might succeed to all his estates and territorial properties in the king¬
dom of Portugal under the Title, name, and dignity of the Baron de
Bliss, and enjoy every customary honour and privilege, right, rank,
and precedence as such; and His Most Faithful Majesty did further
command that the said Royal letters patent, together with the arms
of the said Baron de Bliss be duly recorded in the Royal archives at
Lisbon; and, in compliance with such respective Royal commands, the
said Royal license has been duly registered in Her Britannic Ma¬
jesty's College of Arms, London; and that the said Royal letters
patent and arms have been duly recorded in His Most Faithful Ma¬
jesty's Royal archives at Lisbon.
